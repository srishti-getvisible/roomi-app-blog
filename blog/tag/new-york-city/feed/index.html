What’s a Broker’s Fee?

In general, a broker’s fee is the fee you pay someone to help you find a house in one area. Think of the broker as a middleman between your landlord, the house that you’re renting, and you. Brokers are consulted because they know the areas they represent and can help match both parties at the best price.

However, brokers shouldn’t be confused with real estate agents, as they work in partnership with real estate agencies as well.

There was an attempt to ban broker’s fees in 2020, but the decision was reversed a mere few weeks later. Nowadays, broker’s fees cost between 10% to 15% of the annual lease amount according to Bloomberg. And since the rent in NYC is so expensive, a few hundred dollars could easily get added to an already high rent price.

Do You Really Need a Broker?

Brokers are still hired for their service because they also help smoothen the process of renting an apartment in New York City.

While tons of websites can help you find a no-fee apartment in New York, you still need to be mindful of the entire process. This is to ensure that you do not miss out on any of the important paperwork that is required for an individual to rent an apartment in New York. When we say mindful, we mean:

  • Have your W2s and tax returns handy.
  • Investigate your potential landlord and apartment building thoroughly.
  • Never hand over cash before you’ve met the landlord, visited the apartment in person, and done enough research to feel comfortable!

1. Partner Up For A Class

One of our favorite Valentine’s Day ideas in NYC is to take a fun class! This is an especially fun way to spend the day irrespective of who you’re spending it with. There are several Valentine’s Day events in NYC in this category. If you’re a foodie, you can take a cheese-making class or pizza workshop. 2. Relax And Unwind

Although Valentine’s Day falls on a Monday this year, there’s one way you can enjoy some downtime to celebrate: booking a spa! You can book an appointment in the evening if you’re working. You can enjoy the immense starry night while listening to some of the most romantic love stories ever written.

Valentine’s Day dinner in NYC is a classic way to celebrate. Our recommendation is One if by Land, Two if by Sea. It has been voted the fifth most romantic restaurant in the world by Architectural Digest. Serving some of the best American cuisine, you can enjoy your food with its beautiful ambiance. According to Forbes, this will be “one of the best meals you’ve had in ages, traditional French fare with Moroccan influences and a contemporary flair, in a classically appointed dining room with magnificent floral arrangements in the Masson family tradition”.

5. Take a Romantic Cruise at Sunset

A lovely way to celebrate Valentine’s Day in NYC is to book one of the dinner cruises specially planned for this holiday. Enjoy the lovely buffet on this romantic cruise as you watch the captivating skyline of the city all lit up. The cruise, organized by Bateaux New York offers tickets starting at $208. The cruise itself takes about three hours from beginning to end. So if you have other activities planned for the rest of your evening, you might want to schedule them for later!

6. Go Ice Skating on Valentine’s Day in NYC

Get in touch with your inner child and celebrate Valentine’s Day in NYC with some ice skating! Seeing how V-Day takes place in winter, you’re sure to find several ice rinks open and functional. The two most popular rinks are those at the Rockefeller Center and Central Park. A huge bonus for this idea against other Valentine’s Day ideas in NYC is that it’s an open space, perfect for those who prefer not to be indoors with large groups of people.

Celebrate Valentine’s Day in NYC by splurging and creating a memory that’s going to last you a lifetime! Enjoy a helicopter ride over one of the most beautiful cities in the world with your loved one. If you’re looking for something completely new to do on Valentine’s Day in NYC this year, here are a few unique activities.

NYC Taxi or Uber: Which is better?

Is an Uber better than a Taxi in NYC?

We don’t need to explain to you what uber is, you’ve probably already used it a hundred times. But even if you haven’t they’re actually not that different from a regular taxi. In fact, they’re pretty similar to a yellow taxi cab in how they calculate fares. It’s a combination of the time and distance with an additional fare of any tolls added at the  end.

You’ll need a phone!

But of course, the big difference is, you need a working smartphone and an internet connection to book yourself an Uber. So, if you ever find yourself with a dead phone in NYC, uber is not an option for you. Unless, of course, you want to endure the humiliation of going up to a total stranger and asking to use their phone (which, just FYI, is a terrible choice in NYC).

Rating system FTW!

But there’s a lot of ways in which Uber proves itself to be very useful. First of all, you get to check the rating of the Driver (and they get to see yours as well). If they’ve got a good rating, you can get in the Uber without much stress. You can even share your ride with a friend to be even more cautious.

Uber is also extremely proactive about getting feedback from the customer. So, if you have a bad experience, you can report it instantly.

Availability!

Uber is especially useful if you find yourselves in the Suburbs of NYC. Because, while taxi cabs are abundant in the city, you’ll have a real hard time flagging one down once you’re out in the suburbs.

So, now that you’ve got a pretty well-rounded understanding of getting an Uber in NYC, let’s move onto yellow taxis!

Is a Taxi better than Uber in NYC?

Oh what can we say about the New York yellow taxi cab that hasn’t been said before? I mean, it’s an indispensable part of the NYC experience. Just watch any of the thousand movies set in NYC, and we’re sure you’ll find at least a dozen taxis in all the outdoor shots. Yeah, it’s an integral part of the city. But of course, times have changed and perhaps, the taxi might be one of those things that will slowly fade away. After all, aren’t all these new transportation apps supposed to be cheaper anyway?

It’s actually cheaper!

You’d think that but, surprisingly no, a taxi cab is still cheaper than an Uber in NYC. To be precise, study from the University of Cambridge has found that a taxi cab is generally cheaper for shorter trips. Once a trip costs more than $35, then an Uber is likely going to be cheaper for you. But even then, it’s a toss up.

Yellow taxi cabs are everywhere in NYC. Honestly, just wave once as you’re standing at the curb and you’ll find like 10 taxis. Of course, be prepared to be yelled at if flag down a taxi around for no reason in NYC. You don’t need a smartphone or an internet connection to get a taxi, one flailing arm will do. So, if you’re in the City and in need of a cab immediately, a yellow taxi wins hands down!

Customer service and feedback

It’s not unheard of to have a bad experience with your cab driver. But the procedure to report such an incident is not as instant as it is with Uber. For starters, The NYC Taxi and Limousine Commission mandates that all taxis have a passenger bill of rights inside the cab that drivers and cab owners must adhere to. If you want to file an official complaint, you need to dial 311 or go online and report the incident through this form.

]]> If you’re looking for a place to stay in New York for a brief period of time, short term apartments might be a great idea for you. Rentals are a great alternative to traditional hotels, especially if you’re looking to rent an apartment on a monthly basis in NYC. While short term rentals are more expensive than regular apartments, they are a great option for many people. Whether you’re renovating your home or just a tourist in New York for a month, short term rentals might be just the thing you’re looking for. So, if you’re somebody in the market looking for a short term rental, here’s what you need to look out for.

Do your research on the neighborhood before you sign the lease for your short term rental

Yes, we know, you’re not going to be living there for long, but knowing your neighborhood is a very good idea. There are many areas in New York, where renting short term apartments would be an unsafe choice. If it is an option, walk around the neighborhood once or twice to really get to know the vibe of the place. See what your potential neighbors are like. Trust us, it’s very easy to get stuck with terrible short term rentals if you’re unfamiliar with the city. Of course, this research becomes more difficult if you’re from out of town. In that case, do all your research online. See what other people have said about the neighborhood. The subreddit, AskNYC, can be a fantastic resource for you. Ask any questions you have and we’re sure some good NYC samaritans will help you out.

Related: Where To Find A Short Term Rental Furnished Apartment In NYC.

Make a list of your wants/needs before looking at short term rentals

Wouldn’t we all love to rent a beautiful fully-furnished penthouse that overlooks Central Park? But alas, our budgets bring us back down to earth and force us to be realistic. Apartment rentals in NYC, especially if it’s on a monthly basis, are really expensive. So here’s where you really need to sit down and ask yourself what it is that you really want. Yes, a Central Park facing apartment is really nice, but do you really need it? Similarly, if you’re looking for short term apartments in New York for 3 or 4 months, it might be smart to invest in one that is fully furnished. Only you know the specifics of your situation, so make sure you do a cost/benefit analysis before signing anything.

Make sure you know the legalities before looking at short term rentals

As short term vacation homes have become more popular, there has been some serious backlash from residents. Especially in New York where several communities have passed ordinances that regulate short term rentals. So, for example, you have to lease apartment rentals in NYC on at least a monthly basis, not less than that. Make sure you know the rules and regulations of the building beforehand or may end up paying for it, literally. All of this confusion around regulations has led to an increase in scams, and you really don’t want to get sucked into any of that.
