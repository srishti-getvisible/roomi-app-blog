Getting to know Daytona Beach

The Daytona Beach area was originally home to the indigenous Timucuan Indians, who lived in fortified villages. Later home to the Orange Grove Plantation, it was bought by Mathias Day Jr. in 1871. There, he established a hotel, around which the town grew. However, Day lost the deed to the land after running into financial trouble. But locals still named the town Daytona in his honor.

Daytona Beach has a humid subtropical climate, with two major seasons. Hence, there is the warmer, wetter season (May-October) and the cooler and drier season (November-April). While the weather is mostly stable, there can be occasional adversities. Therefore, watch out for tropical storms or hurricanes if you are looking for rooms for rent in Daytona Beach.

How transit friendly is Daytona Beach?

1. Airports

Daytona Beach has passenger airline services located at the Daytona Beach International Airport (DAB). American Airlines and Delta Airlines offer flights to the city with non-stop service to Charlotte and Atlanta. However, keep in mind that options are limited if you are flying into Daytona Beach. If you are flying in to find cheap rooms for rent in Daytona Beach, Orlando International Airport and Jacksonville International Airport have more flight options.

2. Taxis

Taxis in Daytona Beach are generally safe, and cheaper when compared to other cities. However, you may run into difficulties finding one in certain places or late at night. Hence, it makes sense to book one in advance. Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are also available, which can be handy if you are looking for cheap rooms for rent in Daytona Beach in an out-of-the-way area.

3. Buses

Greyhound buses are a great option if you want to reach Daytona Beach by bus and have regular service to the city. Buses in the city are operated by the Volusia County Public Transit System (Votran). It runs fixed-route buses as well as paratransit services throughout the entire county. Single rides are $1.75 per trip and a 24-hour pass costs $3.75. Hence, it’s a great way to get around the city if you are finding rooms for rent in Daytona Beach.

4. Railways

With the passenger railway being set up in Daytona Beach in 1889, it remains a great way to get to the city. Amtrak offers regular passenger service with the closest stop being near DeLand. However, a Thruway Motorcoach connection between the beachside and Amtrak’s DeLand Station connects the two cities.

Cultural activities in Daytona Beach

  • Daytona International Speedway: This speedway has a capacity of 168,000 spectators and hosts motor vehicle races of various kinds. However, most notably, it hosts the Daytona 500, which is also called ‘The Great American Race.’ Additionally, it is also home to a historical museum, racing simulators, pit stop demonstrations, and more. Thus, this is a must-visit if you are a racing fan and are finding rooms for rent in Daytona Beach.
  • Museum of Arts and Sciences: The Museum of Arts and Sciences hosts both traveling exhibitions and a permanent collection. Furthermore, the galleries feature Chinese, African and Cuban art. Additionally, a planetarium is also on-site with laser concerts and educational shows. Hence, it is a great place to explore if you are finding rooms for rent in Daytona Beach.
  • Daytona Beach Flea Market: The Daytona Beach Flea Market opens on weekends with stalls that sell anything you could want. For example, jewelry, produce, clothing, toys, and electronics are all available here. One of the largest and most highly rated flea markets in the country, it is a must-see if you are finding rooms for rent in Daytona Beach.
