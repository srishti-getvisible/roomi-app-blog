Getting to know Baton Rouge

The name Baton Rouge comes from ‘Bâton-Rouge’ which is French for ‘red stick.’ The name came about when French explorer Pierre Le Moyne d’Iberville arrived and spotted a red pole. This pole was actually a divider between tribal hunting grounds in the area. Baton Rouge has a humid subtropical climate with mild winters and hot and humid summers. However, its proximity to the Gulf of Mexico exposes the area to hurricanes around July. How transit friendly is Baton Rouge?

1. Airports

The Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport is the major airport that serves the city and is located in North Baton Rouge. Airlines like American Airlines, United Airlines, and Delta Airlines offer non-stop service to the city. However, the Louis Armstrong New Orleans International Airport is also popular among travelers. It’s only an hour away and has more budget airlines flying in. Both airports have express buses and taxis connecting them to the city.

2. Buses

Greyhound, Megabus, and Flixbus are the leading options if you want to reach Baton Rouge by bus. If you are finding rooms for rent in Baton Rouge, be sure to check for local bus stops. The Capital Area Transit System (CATS) provides urban transportation in most of the city. These buses also connect Baton Rouge with nearby cities in the metropolitan area. Their updated fares and routes can be found online.

3. Taxis

Taxis in Baton Rouge are generally safe, but more expensive compared to other cities, and you might run into difficulties finding one in certain places. Hence, it makes sense to book one in advance. Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are also available, which can be handy if you are looking for rooms for rent in Baton Rouge in an out-of-the-way area.

Cultural activities in Baton Rouge

  • Louisiana State Capitol: Perhaps the most notable building in the Baton Rouge skyline, this is an iconic building. Be sure to visit the new state Capitol if you are finding rooms for rent in Baton Rouge. A skyscraper built in the Art Deco style, it was built on the vision of Huey P. Long, former Governor, and U.S. Senator.
  • Louisiana State University: This university is not just a campus of learning, but a historical monument as well. The vast campus is more than a century old and has everything from lush gardens to historical buildings. Explore the campus and visit the Tiger Stadium, the Indian Mounds, the Quad, or the outdoor Greek Amphitheater. There are also several lakes near LSU that are open to the public for fishing.
  • African American Museum: Also known as Odell S. Williams Now And Then African-American History Museum, this is a must-visit. The museum was founded by Sadie Roberts-Joseph in 2001 and is a testament and record of the city’s rich heritage. You can also find rural artifacts, African Art, and minority inventions. Be sure to check it out if you are finding rooms for rent in Baton Rouge.
