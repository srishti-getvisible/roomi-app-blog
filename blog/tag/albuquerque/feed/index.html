How transit friendly is Albuquerque?

1. Walkability in Albuquerque

Albuquerque is the 28th most walkable city of the 50 largest cities, as per WalkScore, in terms of commuting. Moreover, it has walkable zones where you can enjoy a quiet stroll that leads to nowhere.

2. Cycling in Albuquerque

Albuquerque has trails, bike routes and dedicated paths throughout. With its first Bicycle Boulevard up and running in 2009, there are plans for more investment in biking infrastructure and convenience in the future.

3. Public transportation in Albuquerque

The Alvarado Transportation Center hosts the city bus system, ABQ RIDE which includes the rapid ride express bus services for inter city travel. Amtrak’s railways serve Albuquerque too with daily commuter rail services.

4. Airports in Albuquerque

2 airports serve the city: Albuquerque International Sunport and Double Eagle II Airport. While the former serves civilians, the latter is primarily used as an air ambulance, corporate flight, military flight, training flight, charter flight, and private flight facility.
