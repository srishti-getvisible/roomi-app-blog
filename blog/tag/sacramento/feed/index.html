Getting to know Sacramento

A Spanish writer with the Moraga expedition wrote about Sacramento:

“Canopies of oaks and cottonwoods, many festooned with grapevines, overhung both sides of the blue current. Birds chattered in the trees and big fish darted through the pellucid depths. The air was like champagne, and (the Spaniards) drank deep of it, drank in the beauty around them. “¡Es como el sagrado sacramento! (It’s like the Blessed Sacrament.)”

Hence, the valley and the river were christened after the “Most Holy Sacrament of the Body and Blood of Christ”, referring to the Catholic sacrament of the Eucharist.

Long after it was discovered, Sacramento was known as the ‘City of Plains’ due to lack of trees. Thereafter, it was developed into being nicknamed the ‘City of Trees’ by planting imported trees in the urban landscape.

Sacramento has also been declared the sunniest location on Earth from July through September every year. In July, it gets an average of 14 hours of sunshine per day! Something to surely remember about life in Sacramento when you’re finding rooms for rent in Sacramento.

Along with all that, Sacramento boasts an extensive park system consisting of over 5,000 acres of parkland and recreation centers too. So if you’re on the hunt for rooms for rent in Sacramento, be assured, greenery and scenic beauty comes along with the life in Sacramento!

How transit friendly is Sacramento?

Ranked as the 24th most walkable city in the U.S., a vast majority of people in the area commute via personal automobile, with 76.9% driving alone and 9.5% carpooling. And the remaining commute via public transit, cycling, walking, and motorcycle riding when living life in Sacramento.

1. Bicycling in Sacramento

Due to its mild climate and flat terrain, bicycling is a popular mode of transportation in Sacramento, especially in the older neighborhoods like East Sacramento. Sacramento was also designated as a Silver Level Bicycle Friendly Community by the League of American Bicyclists in September 2006.

2. Railways in Sacramento

The many rail services that serve the city of Sacramento includes, the Amtrak, The Sacramento Regional Transit District Light Rail system and California Zephyr. Sacramento also boasts of having the second busiest Amtrak station in California, and the seventh busiest in the country!

3. Buses in Sacramento

The Intercity buses that serve Sacramento include Greyhound and Megabus services. The Sacramento Regional Transit District(SacRT) also offers local bus services throughout the Sacramento and some surrounding areas from 5 a.m. until 11 p.m. daily. The bus system consists of 70 routes and over 3,100 total bus stops to make life in Sacramento easier.

Cultural activities in Sacramento

California Automobile Museum

If you love cars, then you need to visit the California Automobile Museum. With up to 150 old school vehicles on display, you can learn all about the history of how cars evolved!

Farmers’ markets

As Sacramento is the farm-to-fork capital of the USA, the farmers’ markets here give you a good picture of the life in Sacramento. And there’s plenty to see and shop from in the 1.5m acres of farms and ranches in the area.

Crest Theatre

Opened more than a century ago as the Empress theatre, the Crest went through another makeover before becoming a venue for niche films, live performances and lectures. It now hosts events such as the Sacramento French Film Festival, the Sacramento Japanese Film Festival, the Sacramento Gay and Lesbian Film Festival and the Sacramento International Film Festival.

Historical spots in Sacramento

Governor’s Mansion and Leland Stanford Mansion

A 30 room mansion built in 1877, it stores all objects left by previous governor residents, which includes a 1902 Steinway piano, hand tied Persian carpets, and more. You can view it all through the museum’s daily tours.

Tower Bridge

Across the Sacramento river, connecting West and East, is the beautiful golden tower bridge. One that you would not miss to visit anyway. Originally painted silver, popular votes transformed it into getting the gold color it has now.

California State Capitol

Modernized repeatedly, the California State Capitol was built in 1860. Now it is both a museum and the work place of the state government. Enjoy knowing about the history of the structure through its guided tours and historic films (all free of cost.)
