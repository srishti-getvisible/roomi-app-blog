]]> There’s something about art that inspires the soul. Fortunate are those who are able to express themselves through art and design. If you’re someone who’s got a knack for designing and want to make a career out of it, lookout for art schools in NYC. Roomi has curated a list of the best art schools in New York, based on the QS ranking 2021. Moreover, QS rankings are internationally recognized and thereby attract a great deal of interest and scrutiny each year.

On what basis does QS rank art schools in NYC?

In fact, QS uses a number of academic indicators by which to measure institutions, depending on the specific ranking in question. These include:

  • Academic Reputation – A score based on the reputation of an institution.
  • H Index – An index that attempts to measure both the productivity and impact of the published work of a scientist or scholar.
  • Citations per Faculty – Citations, evaluated in some fashion to take into account the size of institution, are the best understood and most widely accepted measure of research strength.
  • Staff with PhDs – The indicator is based on a simple proportion of each institution that hold a PhD or equivalent terminal degree.

Let’s look at the best art schools in New York

Parsons School of Design at The New School

QS WUR Ranking By Subject #3

Parsons offers rigorous interdisciplinary programs across the spectrum of art and design, from fashion and technology to urban design, fine art, and management. It also has a branch institute in Paris. In fact, 81% percentage of students receive scholarships and grants from The New School (2018–2019). Indeed, this factor makes Parsons stand out from other art schools in NYC.

In addition, Parsons’ faculty are skilled in employing design-led problem solving to address society’s most pressing needs. This school showcases progressive creativity and activism. Moreover, it is set within a dynamic urban environment. Plus, they have the coolest and most interactive website that engages the designer within you!

Pratt Institute

QS WUR Ranking By Subject #8.

As one of the world’s multicultural epicenters for arts, culture, design, and business, this is one of the most esteemed art schools in New York City. Pratt students are provided with an exceptional learning environment that extends beyond the Pratt campuses in Brooklyn and Manhattan. Pratt offers more than 25 undergraduate degree programs and concentrations and more than 26 graduate degree programs in its Schools of Art, Design, Architecture, Liberal Arts and Sciences, and Information. If this fascinates you, explore more about their certificate, non-degree, minors and online programs.

Columbia University is one among the most affordable art schools in New York

QS WUR By Subject #4

Columbia University is a private Ivy League research university in Upper Manhattan, New York City. Moreover, this University has very high research output and international students. Columbia University School of the Arts awards the Master of Fine Arts degree in Film, Theatre, Visual Arts, and Writing and the Master of Arts degree in Film and Media Studies; it also offers an interdisciplinary program in Sound Art.

School of Visual Arts

QS WUR Ranking By Subject #32.

SVA has been a leader in the education of artists, designers and creative professionals for seven decades. With a faculty of distinguished working professionals and a dynamic curriculum, it emphasis on critical thinking. Also, it comprises of 6,000 students at its Manhattan campus and has 38,000 alumni in 75 countries. Accordingly, SVA offers 31 undergraduate and graduate degree programs! This is one of those art schools in NYC that also represents the most influential artistic communities in the world.

New York University

QS WUR Ranking By Subject #1

Saved the best for the last! New York University (NYU) is one of the top private art schools in New York City, United States. In addition to that, NYU is ranked #35 in QS Global World Rankings 2021. Likewise, it is one of the few art schools in NYC that’s rated the maximum five QS stars. Also, NYU has campuses in Manhattan and Brooklyn, with its main buildings around the lovely Washington Square Park.

Fun fact: Famous alumni include actor Alec Baldwin, Lady Gaga, and playwright Tony Kushner.
