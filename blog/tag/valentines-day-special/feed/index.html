]]> Flowers, chocolates, cards with cheesy love quotes, or maybe a Valentine’s Day getaway, there are many ways to show your love to that special someone on February 14th. But, one thing is for sure, whether you plan to go big or go small, you can never go wrong with including a romantic dinner into the mix. Especially in San Francisco.

As one of the most culturally diverse cities in the U.S., there is no shortage of amazing restaurants or cuisine options. It’s been held as one of the best cities for foodies on many lists and bolsters a long list of Michelin star restaurants. But don’t worry, there are also just as many amazing budget-friendly restaurants that won’t be disappoint!

From upscale restaurants to unique hole-in-the-wall eateries we’ve included some of the best restaurants to explore for a great meal with your sweetheart on V-day.

So let’s get to it…

1. Café Jacqueline

Looking for the perfect spot for a first date? Café Jacqueline, is a great choice! A venture by Chef Jacqueline Margulis, It’s a tiny eatery it’s located in North Beach, where you’ll find old-school service. They only take reservations by phone. It’s known for its ‘slow food’ approach where every dish is made-to-order. It’s a laid-back spot that makes perfect for romantic dinners in San Francisco where you can take the time to really get to know more about your date. The menu includes varying lunch and dinner soufflés, giving these French dishes an interesting twist. Fair warning, though, each item is meant for sharing, so make sure you aren’t heading there with Joey!

2. Firefly Restaurant

Fried chicken and buttermilk biscuits? Mashed potatoes and gravy? While these dishes may not exactly scream Valentine’s Day dinner to you (or maybe it does, we’re not judging!), you’ll change your mind once the food is on the table. This is the perfect spot for couples looking for a more casual date night and an amazing meal. Apart from a delectable menu featuring everyone’s favorite comfort food, the restaurant’s cozy and intimate atmosphere makes it a great choice for Valentine’s Date.

Learn More about Firefly

3. Penny Roma

Co-chefs Ryan Pollnow and Thomas McNaughton of Flour+Water Hospitality Group bring Italian cuisine to your plate using locally-sourced ingredients. If you’re looking for one of the most romantic restaurants in San Francisco, look no further. For those who don’t prefer the moody ambiance of the dining room, the courtyard featuring a water fountain and retractable roof makes for the perfect spot for Valentine’s Day date. Enjoy an eclectic collection of natural wines curated by Flour+Water Hospitality Group’s Wine Director, Samuel Bogue, that pair beautifully with the hand-made pasta dishes on the menu.

Learn More

4. Trestle

Don’t want to go all out on Valentine’s Day yet want to make it special? Trestle offers fixed-price meals which don’t cost more than $39 per person. Made with high-quality ingredients, the restaurant is a popular date spot, and probably one of the most romantic budget-friendly eateries on our list. Not just delicious food, you also get a craft beer and wine served here; but most people agree that the add-on pasta is definitely the highlight. For the herbivores out there, they also have vegetarian options!

Learn More

5. La Ciccia

What makes La Ciccia the most romantic restaurant in San Francisco? The fact that it’s headed by a husband-wife duo, of course! Apart from the inherent lovey-dovey vibes, this seafood-focused spot has some intriguing items on the menu. Fusilli with sea urchin or baby octopus stew wouldn’t be the first dishes to pop up in your mind when you think ‘Italian,’ but their bold menu is what makes La Ciccia a unique choice.

Learn More

6. Foreign Cinema

As the name suggests, you get more than a delectable meal at the Foreign Cinema, you also get a movie! Going strong for 20 years, this is one of the best romantic restaurants in San Francisco, where you can enjoy a meal and a movie without worrying about being late for either. The courtyard is decorated with string lights and heat lamps which create a cozy atmosphere that even romantic rooftop restaurants in San Francisco can’t compete with. With 18-foot ceilings, marble, and stone expanses, the indoor seating is not too shabby either, so you’ve got options when you head to this place. Airing classic and contemporary foreign movies, you can enjoy delectable Californian fares with ingredients that have been sourced directly from farms. Oh, and don’t miss out on getting yourself a wine bottle from their truly extensive wine list.

7. 25 Lusk

25 Lusk is not just one of the most romantic rooftop restaurants in San Francisco; it’s one of the trendiest. With dim lighting, cozy ambiance, and ever-evolving champagne and wine list, make for the perfect ambiance for a romantic dinner. While the rooftop seating is undoubtedly ideal for romantic dates, there’s also the main restaurant and the social space to choose from.

Learn More

From classy outlets with an elegant vibe, to SanFran spots where you can find some easy-on-the-pocket grub, we’ve listed the coziest, most romantic spots in the city. Clearly, San Francisco is the dream city for those who believe the way to heart is through the stomach.

]]> El 14 de febrero es el día en el que los enamorados florecen en cada esquina de la calle. Todos están pensando en cuáles son los mejores planes para San Valentín y gastan fortunas intentando impresionar a su amante.

Awww, ¿Todo muy romántico hasta aquí, no? Pero, en el día que es conocido internacionalmente por enaltecer el amor, nadie piensa en aquellos solteros que se separaron y todavía siguen despechados. En su cabeza, puede que se les cruce esta idea mega fantástica: ¡Qué tal si aprovecho el día más romántico del año para llamar a mi ex y decirle que volvamos!

Ya todos sabemos cómo va a terminar eso, ¿no?, es por eso que este blog de Roomi viene hoy a presentarles ideas más entretenidas para este 14 de febrero, en las que se contemplan varios planes para San Valentín donde no esté involucrado ningún tóxico o tóxica.

¿Para qué sirve el día de San Valentín?

El día de San Valentín está hecho para festejar el amor, pero no solo el que sientes por una pareja, también está uno de los amores más puros del mundo, la amistad. Porque tus amigos son esa familia que no escogiste y que te apoya en las buenas y en las malas. Sí, es esa amiga que te recogió el pelo cuando estabas vomitando en el baño, o aquella que festejó contigo tu primer beso, el que te llevó una sopa cuando estuviste enfermo o los que te acogieron en casa cuando no tenías a donde ir.

El top de los 5 mejores planes para San Valentín

1. Haz una fiesta:

Invita a tus amigos o primos, reproduce la música que más te gusta a todo volumen, tírate unos pasos prohibidos, pasa tu tarde con buena compañía y olvídate del mundo por unas horas. Es más, si quieres, dale tu celular a alguien de confianza por si sientes que en algún momento, y después de haber bebido algunas copas, se te va a mover la mano involuntariamente para escribir el mensaje de “¡Hola perdido!”.

2. Pide tu comida favorita y mira una saga:

Catalogado dentro de los mejores planes para San Valentín y para la vida está el placer de disfrutar de un almuerzo o cena que haga brincar tus papilas gustativas. Acompaña la comida con un poco de entretenimiento y, para hacértelo más fácil, te regalamos esta lista de las mejores sagas de la historia.

3. Sesión de relajación y auto-cuidado:

Ya sea con amigas o sola, tómate el día para reflexionar; medita, hazte una mascarilla o lee algo que nutra tu mente y te mantenga enfocada en algo interesante. Olvida al cucaracho y recuérdate lo mucho que te amas y te valoras.

4. Viaja:

Date un mimo a ti misma y sube tus niveles de adrenalina viajando a un nuevo lugar. No es necesario que te vayas a la otra punta del mundo (Obvio que si tienes esa oportunidad, hazlo sin pensarlo dos veces) pero si prefieres algo más corto, tómate el fin de semana para desconectar y conocer un pueblito mágico o una ciudad cercana. Esto te va a sonar medio cliché, pero está comprobado que tener una cita contigo te hace mejorar tu salud mental. Debes entender que estar solteros no significa estar solos. Ve al cine, a tomar un café, haz un recorrido por la ciudad o siéntate en el parque para hacer un pícnic y respirar aire fresco.

]]> It’s that time of the year again where love is everywhere! Gone are the times of celebrating Valentine’s Day with just chocolate and flowers. Whether you’re celebrating the day with your friends, your partner, or just yourself, there’s a whole host of activities you can engage in. In a city as multicultural and diverse as New York, there’s no end to what you can do. To start you off, here’s a list of eight ways you can celebrate Valentine’s Day in NYC!

1. Partner Up For A Class

One of our favorite Valentine’s Day ideas in NYC is to take a fun class! This is an especially fun way to spend the day irrespective of who you’re spending it with. There are several Valentine’s Day events in NYC in this category. If you’re a foodie, you can take a cheese-making class or pizza workshop. If you’re looking for something artsier, you can learn pottery or even graffiti! Whichever you choose, you’re sure to have a lot of fun. With the current COVID-19 situation, you can even find virtual classes.

2. Relax And Unwind

Although Valentine’s Day falls on a Monday this year, there’s one way you can enjoy some downtime to celebrate: booking a spa! You can book an appointment in the evening if you’re working. But if you decide to take the day off to spend it with your partner, booking a day spa for a couple’s massage is one of the most romantic things to do in NYC on Valentine’s Day. The Couple Spa has great packages you can choose from!

3. Visit NYC Attractions

While this might not sound like an enticing way to spend Valentine’s Day in NYC, here are a few attractions that will change your mind!

  • The Empire State Building – one of the most romantic things to do in NYC on Valentine’s Day is going all the way to the top of the Empire State Building with your partner. What better way to celebrate the day of love than by being surrounded by love? A popular spot for proposals, the building even celebrates by lighting up red!
  • Roosevelt Island Tramway – get your fellow celebrators and yourself subway tickets across the East River and you’ve got yourself romantic views of the river facing Uptown and Downtown Manhattan!
  • The American Museum of Natural History – visiting this museum is another one for the list of iconic Valentine’s Day ideas in NYC. Every year on this special day, the Hayden Planetarium hosts a “Romance Under The Stars” event. You can enjoy the immense starry night while listening to some of the most romantic love stories ever written.

4. Enjoy A Romantic Valentine’s Dinner in NYC

Valentine’s Day dinner in NYC is a classic way to celebrate. Our recommendation is One if by Land, Two if by Sea. It has been voted the fifth most romantic restaurant in the world by Architectural Digest. Serving some of the best American cuisine, you can enjoy your food with its beautiful ambiance. According to Forbes, this will be “one of the best meals you’ve had in ages, traditional French fare with Moroccan influences and a contemporary flair, in a classically appointed dining room with magnificent floral arrangements in the Masson family tradition”.

5. Take a Romantic Cruise at Sunset

A lovely way to celebrate Valentine’s Day in NYC is to book one of the dinner cruises specially planned for this holiday. Enjoy the lovely buffet on this romantic cruise as you watch the captivating skyline of the city all lit up. The cruise, organized by Bateaux New York offers tickets starting at $208. The cruise itself takes about three hours from beginning to end. So if you have other activities planned for the rest of your evening, you might want to schedule them for later!

6. Go Ice Skating on Valentine’s Day in NYC

Get in touch with your inner child and celebrate Valentine’s Day in NYC with some ice skating! Seeing how V-Day takes place in winter, you’re sure to find several ice rinks open and functional. The two most popular rinks are those at the Rockefeller Center and Central Park. A huge bonus for this idea against other Valentine’s Day ideas in NYC is that it’s an open space, perfect for those who prefer not to be indoors with large groups of people.

7. Adventure Away With a Helicopter Ride

Celebrate Valentine’s Day in NYC by splurging and creating a memory that’s going to last you a lifetime! Enjoy a helicopter ride over one of the most beautiful cities in the world with your loved one. There are several tour operators that offer helicopter rides and you can choose from this comprehensive list!

8. Do Something Absolutely Unique!

If you’re looking for something completely new to do on Valentine’s Day in NYC this year, here are a few unique activities.

  • Join A Mass Vow Renewal Event – Every year, Times Square hosts a Vow Renewal Ceremony in which hundreds of people gather to declare their love for one another again. You can register to be a part of this ceremony here.
  • Enjoy The Green-Wood Cemetery’s “Happily Never After” Tour – This cemetery, located in Brooklyn, organizes a “Happily Never After” tour every Valentine’s Day in NYC. If you’re interested in the heartbreaks and love affairs of historical people, writers, and artists, this is the tour for you! You can book your tickets for the tour on the website here.

We hope this list has given you fun things you can do to enjoy this Valentine’s Day in NYC.

]]> Galentine’s Day Party Ideas That Your BFFs Will Love

First of all, let us clarify that that’s not a typo! Galentine’s Day does exist. If you’ve ever watched Parks and Recreation, you know what Galentine’s Day is. For the uninformed, it’s a holiday celebrated on February 13th where we cherish our friends and the deep bonds we share. The day was created and popularized by Amy Poehler’s character Leslie Knope from the same sitcom. She described the day as “ladies celebrating ladies,” and we said YES! However, the day has evolved and become more inclusive over the years.

So, if you want to shower some love to your BFFs and receive some in return, then this is the day for you! If you’re planning to celebrate it for the first time, we have got some cool Galentine’s Day party ideas. Invite all your BFFs for the night for some laughter, tea spilling, and have a fun-filled day!

Galentine’s Day Theme

You can choose any theme for the party, from a sleepover to a pizza party or a wine and chocolate night. If you’re overwhelmed by too many Galentine’s Day party ideas, create a fun poll among your friends to help you decide! Additionally, here are a few things that you might want to do for the big day.

Suggest a Dress Code

Think of a dress code for the party and hit up your group chat for some cool ‘fit suggestions. You can take the traditional route and choose to match colors (like red since it’s close to Valentine’s Day), or you can rock matching pajamas. Whatever you choose, just ensure everyone feels comfortable and ready for the camera click.

Bake Off

Face off? Nah, we suggest a bake-off! Organize a friendly competition among your best pals to see who’s got the skills to bake the best looking and best-tasting dish ever! Select a recipe and let everyone try their hands baking it. The best part is everyone wins here, because unlike other competitions, everyone gets to try all these tasty treats!

Galentine’s Day Party Games

White Elephant is a popular holiday game you can makeover as a Galentine’s Day party game. Although there are many versions of it, here is the basic concept:

Everyone has to bring a gift. Each player will be assigned a random number, and then each turn, one person chooses to either unwrap a new gift or steal someone else’s. For example, the person who goes first gets to open any gift of their choice. Each person then continues in numerical order. If your gift gets stolen, you get to go again! The game ends once everyone has received a gift.

Note: It’s always good to have a dollar limit so everyone receives a gift of the same value.

Galentine’s Day Decorating Ideas

Choose a color palette for your party and start shopping for decorations if you haven’t already! Pink, red, gold, and white are some of the most popular colors for a Galentine’s party. Since it’s a day to shower your platonic love for your pals, you can deck up the party venue with balloons, streamers, and banners. Additionally, if you plan to sit down for a meal, you also need to set up the dinner table. A colorful tablecloth, some scented candles in fashionable holders, and fresh flowers of the season will do the trick.

Galentine’s Day Party Food

Did we hear food? Of course! No party can be complete without a delicious spread, a few bottles of vino, some hearty chats, and endless laughter. But if you’re a true fan of Parks and Recreation, you must be familiar with Leslie Knope’s obsession with breakfast food! So, why not set up your Galentine’s party table with a brunch menu in her honor? Serve heart-shaped waffles, bacon, eggs, bagels, muffins, scones, croissants, and coffee to your pals.

And here’s a pro tip to take your party to the next level: arrange your breakfast spread on a charcuterie board. Voila! You just created an art piece that will surely grab eyeballs on the ‘Gram.

Galentine’s Night Drink Ideas

No party is complete without an excellent selection of boozy beverages. You can either keep it simple or have a nice collection of red and white wine.

You can also create tasty concoctions like strawberry-basil cocktails, sparkling peach bellinis, or chocolate martinis. If your party color is red, Sangrias go best for Galentine’s day party ideas and can be prepared in advance.

Galentine’s Day Party Activities

When you have your pals around, you can do anything! Well, almost. But if you want to keep your besties entertained and jazz up the spirit, we have rounded up a list of Galentine’s Day Party ideas for activities!

Marathon Movie Night

If you all are into movies, nothing can beat the pleasure of relaxing on the couch and binge-watching your favorite films throughout the night. You can go with one movie from each of your friend’s choices or pick a genre loved by all.

Time To Get Creative

Grab some art supplies and a picture to replicate (probably something that all of you love, like food?) and spend the night creating a piece of art.

Nail Art

Get a good nail art set at home, and spend the evening with your buds creating some fancy nail art. Don’t forget to click some pictures and quick videos for your IG fans!

Selfie Booth

A Galentine’s night is a special moment for you and your besties that you will want to preserve and cherish for years to come. So, set up a DIY photo booth with some gorgeous backdrops (for example, you can set up the backdrop with heart-shaped balloons). Click some selfies in the photo booth with your pals and capture the moment.

Game Night

You can print and cut out scavenger hunt riddles and hide them around the party venue for guests to find. Once all the participants are ready, hand out the first clue and let them race around to find the remaining clues until one person finds the final surprise! You may also play Galentine’s Day-themed guessing games. For example, you can say some facts (a few correct and a few incorrect) about one of the guests, and let the others guess which of the facts is correct. Or, guests can take turns answering questions about each other or about their friendships with the hosts.

Sing-Along

Not in the mood to dance? Get hold of a karaoke set, and let everyone sing their favorite tune! Alternatively, you can just play your favorite tunes with accompanying lyric videos on YouTube and sing along!

Personalized Goodie Bags

Conclude your party with a sweet gesture by sending your guests home with personalized goodie bags and handwritten thank you notes. While the goodies will remind them of the fun they had at the party, the customized card will be a memento of the time you spent together.

]]> Love is in the air, and we’re all here for it. If you’re looking to spice things up this Valentine’s Day, you’ve come to the right place! From Santa Barbara to Cambria, California houses some of the most romantic getaways ever — and we’ll be giving you all the juicy deets. Here are some of the best Valentine’s Day getaways in California you can try this year.

1. Napa Valley

Known as one of the world’s premier wine regions, Napa Valley is home to several romantic getaways in California. From hotels and resorts to high-style restaurants, this place has a lot to offer. One night may not be enough to experience all the wonders that Napa Valley has in store for you. So, if you want to make the most of your trip, you can organize a longer visit.

  • Carneros Resort and Spa: In this perfect romantic getaway, you’ll have access to private cottages and luxury homes that ensure a relaxed and quintessential Napa experience.
  • Napa Valley Wine Train: Are you a wine lover? Well, you can have the best romantic dinner you’ve ever had on this scenic wine country tour.

2. Joshua Tree

If you and your partner love a good adventure, then Joshua Tree is the ideal spot for you both. Hidden in this city are some of the most secluded romantic getaways in California that will give you the privacy you need.

  • National Park: Here, you can go on enjoyable hikes, enjoy the beautiful view, and even have a picnic. The famous Heart Rock is nearby (a large boulder shaped like a heart), where you can take adorable pictures.
  • The Desert Lily Lodge: Get your blankets ready, and watch shooting stars across the beautiful night sky in this B&B. In this awesome Valentine’s Day getaway, you can fully relax and have much-needed alone time.
  • Sacred Sands: A truly magical holiday destination, Sacred Sands will give you an experience like no other. Their services include free Wi-Fi, meditation platforms, posh indoor bathrooms, outdoor private showers, and bathtubs.
