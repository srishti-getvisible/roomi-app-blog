]]> Looks like work from home is here to stay. And no one is complaining! We all are adjusting and adapting to the new normal. Now that most of the population is desperately looking for legitimate online jobs to sustain themselves amidst a pandemic, unfortunately, scammers have a new market to explore. So when you’re looking for stay at home jobs online, you need to be extra cautious and take certain measures to protect yourself and your career.

Things to keep in mind for stay at home jobs and offers

1. Email address

When a legitimate employer contacts you, they’ll do so with a legitimate company ID, (x@companyname.com) So if you get an offer for stay at home jobs from a personal Gmail, Yahoo or Hotmail ID, that’s worrisome and you should not trust the job poster.

2. Job description

Another way to find out if a company is legitimate is if the stay at home job offer has a detailed job description that matches what you do for a living. If the job description is too vague. Or maybe it offers a job you’re not trained for. And they propose to train you on the job, then it’s probably a job scam.

3. Personal information

A surefire way to spot a job scam is when a potential employer asks for your personal information such as your social security number, your bank details etc. during, or right after the interview even before they hire you. Just make sure you never give out personal details on an interview call for stay at home jobs.

4. Application fee

This is probably one of the most obvious ones. But sadly, people who really need a job, and are not well versed with how the online world works, can get trapped into an employment scam like this. A company will ask for a nominal fee so they can interview you, or hire you. THIS IS THE REDDEST FLAG YOU WILL EVER SEE. No job can require you to pay so you can get hired. No matter what they say, do not ever pay a company to hire you. It always works the other way around.

How should you find legitimate stay at home jobs?

When looking for legitimate stay at home jobs, it’s always a good idea to find them on legitimate websites that allow job seekers and posters to come together and find the perfect match.

Social platforms

LinkedIn is your safest bet. You can filter your search options to find stay at home jobs, and find legitimate employers and organizations easily.

Facebook and Instagram have also become budding job seeking and posting portals. You just need to be scrolling at the right time, and at the right place, and you never know, you might just find the perfect job.

Use remote job portals to find stay at home jobs

Other remote job portals work best when looking for stay at home jobs, such as Upwork, Fiverr, We Work Remotely, etc.

Can anyone become a freelancer?

That surely depends on the kind of work you do for a living. Additionally, the conditions of being a freelancer would also vary with the kind of occupation you do. With the internet boom and most work requiring just a laptop and a stable internet connection, it is quite possible to find online jobs across all fields.

However, if your work requires physical presence, you can still look at becoming a freelancer. If not for finding work-from-home jobs, it can allow you the possibility of setting your own hours in the least.

What are the pros of working from home?

Excited? Let’s look at a few pros of becoming a freelancer. We will also tell you about how to look for freelance jobs for beginners.

You have the freedom to make your own schedule

The number one reason for hating your regular 9-5 could be well.. the 9 – 5. You have to clock in at a certain time. You can’t leave before a certain time. And you have to deal with all the drama that ensues in between.

But when you’re a freelancer with work from home jobs, you aren’t as time-bound as you are at a regular job. No one can demand you to clock in or clock out. And all you need to keep in mind is submission of your work within the deadlines.

But stay wary though. You either have to have super organizational skills and be a self-motivator or have to be willing to learn those skills to make it as a freelancer. Otherwise, you’d end up working online jobs ALL DAY LONG. And being in your PJs wouldn’t help even a tad bit.

It’s not easy. But thanks to the pandemic (can’t believe we can actually write that), there are a lot more online jobs than before. So becoming a freelancer or looking for work from home jobs can give you better results now, owing to the increased numbers of such online jobs.

But that said, you still have to put in a lot of effort to find a good job that pays well and doesn’t exploit your time or energy. Especially if you’re looking at freelance jobs for beginners. And oftentimes, you have to wait for a long time to hear back from a potential job.

And how exactly do you find a job online?

There are several basic ways to look for online jobs, but you’d have to do it all simultaneously to increase your chances of finding a good fit.

Trusted job portals

Spruce up your resume and stay active on portals such as LinkedIn, Upwork, Fiverr, etc. Make a kickass portfolio and keep it ready to send through when you get in touch with a potential client.

Social media websites and groups

A lot of employers are looking for people on social media – especially for marketing-related roles. So join some groups that post online jobs. You will find plenty work from home jobs, freelance jobs for students, and freelance jobs for beginners on such groups. The next step is to stay active in these groups.

Tap into your network

If you know people who work in the HR department, consider getting in touch with them for potential work from home jobs. Connect with previous colleagues and ask around if they personally know any freelancers or people looking for freelancers. Knowing freelancers will help you enter this world and find online jobs for yourself!

So are there any downsides to being a full-time freelancer?

Now before you’re determined to find your dream online jobs, let’s look at a few downsides that you need to be aware of. However tempting it may seem, the truth is, that it’s not for everyone.

Beware of scams when looking for online jobs

Unfortunately, with an increase in demand for online jobs, a bigger scam employment market has crept up online. So you have to be extra careful when looking for work from home jobs and freelance jobs for students. Stay wary of all red flags so you don’t become a victim in these troubling times. Especially if you’re on the lookout for freelance jobs for beginners.

Understand the process of making money in online jobs

Firstly, your payments will be irregular, depending on the type of clients, and how many of those you have. Many freelancers find managing administrative tasks difficult. One such task is generating and sending invoices for completed work. Thankfully, there are tools available to streamline this process, such as invoice templates for Excel. These templates can simplify billing and payments, allowing freelancers to focus more on their core activities rather than manual calculations.

Secondly, you will be responsible for your own payments when working online jobs. You’ll be sending invoices and following up with the client in case of delay – as opposed to a regular paycheck at the same time every month at a regular job. And one thing all freelancers dread – you’d also have to learn to do your own taxes!

Accept the job insecurity that comes with it

Because of a lack of personal relationship with your client and lack of accountability, you have less job security when you’re freelancing. And it can be rather erratic to find and sustain online jobs and freelance jobs for students.

In conclusion, becoming a freelancer comes with its risks. But if you’re determined to become a successful freelancer, you shall become one!

]]> Let’s face it, 2020 has been a year of significant change.

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, individuals all over the world have had to make the decision to work from home.

While you might enjoy the routine of working from an office, 2020 has shown us that staying home to work isn’t as bad as we thought it would be!

In fact, there are plenty of benefits. When you work from home, there’s no need to worry about long commutes or showing up late to meetings… plus, you can wear sweatpants all day!

So, how do you navigate working from home while co-living with a roommate?

From Zoom meetings to business calls, it can be hard to stay focused and keep work private while living with another person.

Here are a few tips to help you work from home effectively while living with roommates!

Create a schedule

Creating structure is an absolutely crucial part of working from home efficiently when living with a roomi or roommates. Why? Because can be super easy to get distracted when sharing a space with another person! If your roommate is on a different schedule than you and spends their days watching Netflix on full volume while you’re trying to make a business call, things can get tricky.

In order to avoid schedule clashes with your roomi, try and map out your days during the week in terms of what time your meetings fall, or when you really need some quiet time to knuckle down. Let your roommate know when it’s okay to make noise in the kitchen and when you really need the apartment to be quiet. Creating a schedule for yourself will help you and your roommate structure your time more efficiently.

Be flexible, life happens!

Sometimes things don’t work out the way we want them to, and that’s okay!

When working from home, there are a number of distractions that can arise compared to when working from an office. We can’t always control outside factors, so we need to be able to be flexible with our schedule and structure! Maybe there’s construction going on in your apartment, or your roommate is working out in the living room? if you can’t block out the noise and focus. why not head to your local coffee shop to complete your work instead of working from your apartment that day? Try and remember that life happens and issues can come up unexpectedly, and sometimes you just have to run with it!

Take a deep breath and try and remain relaxed and flexible.

Create a daily routine

Making a routine for yourself will help you feel more normal amongst these challenging times. Just because you’re working from home, it doesn’t mean that your professionalism has to go out of the window (the sweat pants can stay of course!). Make sure that you go to bed at the same time as you would normally, wake up early, and eat breakfast before you start your day.

It’s a great option to incorporate daily planner templates into your daily routine to plan your day effectively and maintain a structured schedule. Be sure to wear a professional work shirt in order to make you feel the part, and if you can, encourage your roomi to get into a work routine with you – it’ll make creating structure much easier!

Communicate with your roommate

As with any relationship, communication is key! Working from home is a huge life change for everyone, and it may take some time for you and your roommate to get used to your new routine. Be sure to communicate with your roomi about what you need from them in order to work efficiently and effectively. Your job depends on it! Set aside blocks of time when you need the apartment to be quiet and decide which area in your apartment will act as your office space, even if it is a corner in your bedroom. It’s important to separate your relaxing area from your work area.

Let your roommate know if something isn’t working for you, and make sure that they do the same with you! After all, you’re in this together!

]]> With the pandemic life becoming regulated (thanks to the ‘new normal’ terms), more and more offices are demanding their employees to get back to work in their physical offices. While most employees are okay with not working remotely, others are anxious about traveling to work. Mostly because that means coming into contact with people. Is working remotely even possible for you?

Okay, this one goes without saying; surgeons, pilots, Uber drivers, and so many other professionals can’t work from home.

However, there are plenty of teleworking roles that can be managed remotely. This includes roles such as copywriters, social media managers, designers, and sales jobs. Analyze your role to the finest detail. Moreover, read your job description and note tasks that can be managed from home. This activity will come in handy when you want to propose WFH to your bosses.

Make sure you’re good at what you do!

Your bosses won’t let you take this remote working opportunity if they don’t even trust you with your work. So it’s essential to know your place! If you’re not a top performer well versed with culture and operations, your boss can easily hire someone new to replace you. We recommend that you ask yourself the following questions before you present this idea to your employers:

  • What’s your track record when it comes to performance reviews?
  • Did you make any recent mistakes? If so, how did you recover?
  • How’s your rapport with your coworkers & clients?
  • Have you received a mention/positive feedback from clients or team leads?
  • What’s your USP?

Explore what’s working remotely means for the company and your boss

Your bosses will have to weigh many essential questions before they give you the go-ahead for this.

  • Will this impact your productivity & output?
  • Does this make life harder or easier for your coworkers?
  • Will other coworkers demand the same treatment?

  • Increased employee productivity
  • Increased employee morale & health
  • Decreased spread of the coronavirus contagions
  • Lower employee turnover
  • Increased sense of ownership to drive tasks to completion
  • Lesser stress levels = Less downtime/sick days
  • Less expenses on electronics, office bills etc.

A Government study under the Obama administration Office of Personnel Management also indicated common organizational benefits of teleworking, including:

  • Better emergency preparedness (59%)
  • Improved employee attitudes (58%)
  • Better recruitment (35%)

  • Reduced employee commute miles (29%)
  • Improved employee performance (17%)
  • Reduced real estate costs (17%)

For instance:

  • Taking public transport
  • Interacting with groups in the office
  • Anxiety about passing it on to elderly parents or grandparents who are most at risk of the virus

The post How To Create The Perfect Workspace At (Your Rented) Home appeared first on Roomi | blog.

]]> Before we get started on how to create the perfect workspace at home, here’s your daily reminder to straighten your back when you work from home. Now, moving on!

It’s been 6 months since we’ve been spending time in a work from home situation. And chances are, most of us are still trying to find that sweet spot of the perfect workspace. And one that intersects optimal productivity and comfort to get your through 8 business hours.

There are many DIYs to inspire you to re-do your workspace at home. But if you’re renting a fairly small space, the landlord might not want you overstepping.

However, the great thing about re-doing your workspace at home is that it doesn’t take a lot of effort (or money.) To create the perfect workspace requires no alteration to the property you are renting.

But what does it mean to have the perfect workspace? A work space that feels right. And one that helps you avoid distractions the best you can, so you can work the way you like. Basically, the kind that doesn’t make you want to doze off after reading just 3 emails.

Here are our key pointers that will make all the difference to your work from home days.

1. The desk for your perfect workspace at home

Ergonomics calls for matching your workspace to your work needs. Rule number one is matching the top of your monitor to your eye level. However, setting a functional desk also requires other additional components that we will cover in just a second. But the perfect workspace requires, well, nothing at all. Yep, a clutter-less desk is one of the key components to optimizing your productivity. Some people swear by standing desks. These keep the posture correct, especially for those with more severe issues. And also allows you to take a healthy amount of breaks, which are just as much of a need during this work from home period.

2. The chair for your perfect workspace at home

They key to not dozing off when working from home is creating a comfortable space that isn’t too comfortable. All you need is cushioning and ample armrest. Nothing more/less. That is basically it to make your perfect workspace. Get a cushion to support your back and make sure your arms are slightly lifted at the shoulders at all times – if not, they cause your shoulders and neck to work overtime inflicting long term posture issues.

The backrest of your chair is possibly the most important component in keeping your posture in check, keep you working for the exact amount of time you need to, by keeping the pain away. We definitely recommend investing in a comfortable, quality chair to create your perfect workspace.

3. The window

Believe it or not, some people prefer working in slightly darker rooms. But countless studies show that natural light can charge our work-time productivity and make a substantial difference to our moods. Harvard Business Review goes as far as calling natural light, the number 1 perk employers can offer their employees.

For this reason alone, we recommend setting up shop as close to a source of natural light as you can to create the perfect workspace at home. Even if you are working from your bed in a friend’s basement, optimize all sources of light through hacks like mirrors and clutter-less furniture. That fresh air is a great supplement for your 4th cup of coffee. Maybe not the 1st or the 2nd, but definitely for the 4th.

4. The décor for your perfect workspace at home

The simplest of additions, like adding artwork or a funky calendar to the nearest wall in view, placing a plant close by, maybe even some incense sticks can alleviate your mood while you work from home. Fake plants would probably not have the same desired effect here, as an actual plant comes with some added benefits like helping with stale air, for free.

There are also people who prefer adding accent pieces like a really fancy chair, maybe a cozy blanket nearby, a hanging desk? These are all things that make the space more you, and personalization of home offices is a known trick to make the perfect workspace at home!

5. The power

There can honestly never be enough organizing for your perfect workspace at home. Every power-based device you use requires to be charged throughout the day and there are truly, few things as frustrating as a bunch of tangled wires stopping you from finding the one charging cable you are looking for.

Power strips also help you not overwhelm all of your outlets. And make it easier to not fight with your roommate over power points or trip accidentally.

6. Dealing with the noise when you work from home

A highly recommended addition to office spaces in general is adding some sort of sound. The logic being, absolute silence can be distracting. White noise playlists are a great replacement for people who find it difficult to focus with lyrical music. There are also tons of “beats to chill/work/study to” playlists on YouTube and Spotify with fitting music that can help (increase) your focus. You might need to spend some time figuring what kind of music helps you focus best, so be sure to take your time.
