Explore the outdoors in San Antonio

1. Riverwalk

Looking for places to visit in San Antonio for a relaxing day out near the river, while you walk through paths lined with Cypress trees, restaurants, hotels, shops, museums, and more? Then put this one in your itinerary of the best things to do in San Antonio!

Costs:

Entry is free. Activities come with costs.

Timings:

The downtown and museum reach sections are open 24 hours a day. Individual business hours vary.

2. Brackenridge Park

If you like spending time amongst nature, add this to your list of places to visit in San Antonio! You’ll find a 343 acre public park home to part of San Antonio river, a Japanese tea garden, a sunken garden theatre, events and much more. It’s the perfect place to enjoy a peaceful day out.

Costs:

Entry to the park is free. The pavilions reservations can cost up to $15 – $25 an hour.

Timings:

5:00 am – 11:00 pm

3. Natural Bridge Caverns

Never been inside a cave? Step into this one for an adventurous day out when looking for cool things to do in San Antonio! With different tours, from watching massive, otherworldly natural formations to mining for gems, fossils and other treasures, you’re in for a fun experience. (And don’t worry: this is one of the less scary caves!)

Costs:

$25 – $30

Timings:

9:00 am – 5:00 pm

Visit the museums in San Antonio

7. Guinness World Records Museum

Into all things weird? Head to the Guinness World Records Museum to view exhibits of world records from amazing sports, human achievements, weirdness and more! You can also participate and earn an official Guinness World Record title, if you can break a record. Overall, this is one of the seriously cool things to do in San Antonio – don’t forget to take lots of photos!

Costs:

$15 – $24

Timings:

Friday & Saturday: 10:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m.

San Antonio tourism would be incomplete without wonders like these. Take a trip around the world and through 5,000 years of art at the San Antonio Museum of Art. It is renowned for ancient Greek, Roman & Egyptian art collections, and will engage your curious mind like no other.

$10 – $20

Get a taste of culture in San Antonio

10. Tobin Center for the Performing Arts

If you like both architecture and the arts, head to the Tobin Center for the Performing Arts that combines one of San Antonio’s most beloved architectural icons with advanced technology. The result: one of the most flexible multi-purpose performance halls in the United States!

Timings:

10:00 am – 06:00 pm; Sunday closed

Visit the website

And just like it sounds, you get to relive the spooky past of the area, while listening to ghost stories and historical facts. Real locals will engulf you with their personal paranormal experiences and chill you to the core (in a good way.)

13. The Barwalk

I strive to make each Barwalk a personal experience and conversation where more than a guide, I am a guy that you would want to have a drink with who just happens to enjoy a passion for history, storytelling, and sharing my hometown. – Founder of the Barwalk, Emzy King

And exactly as it sounds, you get to walk around the city, bar hopping while having a great time with people who know the place like no other.

$35 – $70

Timings:

Varies with the tour you choose

14. La Villita Historic Arts Village

One of the best places to visit in San Antonio: an artsy square block in the heart of downtown, this artisan village is a historic beauty that doubles up as a great shopping centre.

It includes over 25 shops and galleries showcasing local handmade goods, along with great many events throughout the year.

Timings:

Thursday – Saturday: 10:00 am – 06:00 pm; Sunday: 11:00 am – 04:00 pm

Leave all the other places to visit in San Antonio behind, because the sights and flavors of old Mexico should be enough to entice you to head to the Historic market square – and keep you here. But to add to that, there are over 100 local businesses, live entertainment, and much more for everyone who visits.

Timings:

Saturday: 10am – 6pm

Sunday – Friday (Tuesday & Wednesday closed): 10am – 5pm

How transit friendly is San Antonio

San Antonio commute is actually quite a pleasant experience. The city is fairly easy to navigate, the traffic isn’t too bad and there’s some quirky transportation options available for you. Here’s how you get around the city!

Walkability

San Antonio is not the most walkable city in the world. Most people here drive to get around, which is by far the easiest and the most convenient. However, Downtown San Antonio is quite walkable with a Walk Score of 96!

Roads/traffic

The average one-way commute time in San Antonio, 24.1 minutes, is actually shorter than the natural average. It might take some time to get used to the freeways and suburbs, but, that’s what Google Maps is here for!

Taxis

While Uber and Lyft are popular options, you can also use Yellow Cab. Keep in mind, it’s a little hard to find cabs on the street. You can also try the river taxi. Yes, that’s right, the city has a river taxi! Go Rio has river-taxis that run along the Downtown Reach and Museum Reach sections of the River Walk daily from 10am to 9pm.

Public Transportation

Public transportation isn’t the most popular option here, but that doesn’t mean you don’t have any viable options! The VIA Metropolitan Transit Service offers regular bus services at the extremely reasonable fare of $1.30.

Airports

The San Antonio International Airport is just 10 miles away from the center of the city.

Cycle-Friendly

The city has a public bike-share program called the San Antonio Bike Share. It’s great for short trips around the city!
