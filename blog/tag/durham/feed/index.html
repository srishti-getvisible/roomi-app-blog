Outdoor Activities in Durham, NC

1. Early mornings at Durham Central Park

Looking for a place to get brunch, walk your dog, and shop at the same time? Durham Central Park is certainly the place to go to. Stop by the Farmer’s Market in the park every Saturday, then wander around the tree grove are dedicated to Durham’s sister cities. You can also lay on the grass at the Great Lawn and watch skaters do tricks at the Skate Park. There’s even an interactive pixel wall overlooking the Great Lawn! Talk about a mix of activities in Durham, huh?

  • Admission: Free
  • Time: Dawn to dusk
Map of the Park

2. Have a picnic at West Point on the Eno

West Point on the Eno is a 404-acre park along the Eno River in Downtown Durham. It was once home to the Shocco and Eno Indians and is now a natural and historic city park that’s a fusion of woods, waters, and wildlife. You can check out three Durham attractions here: West Point Mill, McCown-Mangum House at West Point, and Packhouse & Hugh Mangum Museum of Photography.

Another part of the park holds the Amphitheatre at West Point where concerts, festivals, and performances are held. The park also offers picnic facilities that can accommodate up to 75 people with tables, chairs, and grills. Once you’re done eating, take in the natural area along a 5-mile trail!

  • Admission: Free
  • Time: The buildings are open every Saturday and Sunday from 1 to 5 PM.
Event and Activities Calendar

3. Stroll around Duke University Campus

Spend a day like a university student at Duke University! As one of the most prestigious colleges in the country, Duke University boasts an exceptional campus with historic architecture and amenities. You can explore the Sarah P. Duke Gardens or the 7,060-acre Duke Forest, or maybe whip out your camera and take photos of the Duke University Chapel. Either way, this is surely one of the best places to visit in Durham.

Sports in Durham, NC

4. Bike the American Tobacco Trail

What used to be a railroad for the American Tobacco Company is now a 23-mile trail that goes from the western edge of Apex to Downtown Durham. The trail can be used for horseback riding, cycling, skating, roller-blading, and more. So, if you love the outdoors, it should definitely be on your list of things to do in Durham, NC.

  • Time: Dawn to dusk
American Tobacco Trail Map

5. Sign up at the Tri Sports Social Club

Tri Sports hosts recreational sports leagues for groups who are 21 years or older. They not only offer sports, but they also run social events where you can meet new and interesting people. If you’re looking for sporty activities in Durham, you should definitely sign up.

Museums in Durham, NC

6. Explore the Museum of Durham History

If you’re interested in learning more about Durham, the Museum of Durham History tells stories about the city’s community, places, and history. With a variety of exhibits and events, the museum is definitely one of the best places to visit in Durham. And, since it’s indoors, it’s even great for rainy days!

  • Admission: Free
  • Time: 10 AM to 5 PM (Tuesday to Saturday), 1 PM to 5 PM (Sunday)
Visit Website

7. Visit the Nasher Museum of Art

The Nasher Museum of Art boasts more than 13,000 works that vary from antiquity to contemporary art. Additionally, they host temporary exhibitions, events, and even public and private tours. Since the museum is located inside Duke University, it’s also a learning institution that offers programs focusing on visual arts. Don’t forget to grab a snack at the Nasher Café before you leave!

Visit Website
Hours & Admission

8. Play and Learn at Museum of Life + Science

A quick Google search of “things to do in Durham, NC” will lead you to the Museum of Life + Science. The museum is for all ages with hands-on experiments and interactive exhibits that will surely make you feel like a kid again. One of their most mentioned exhibits that you need to try is the Aerospace exhibit. This top Durham attraction displays NASA artifacts and a three-story glass Magic Wings Butterfly House.

  • Admission: Tickets range from $13 to $23 (children ages 2 and under are free!)
  • Time: 10 AM to 5 PM
Plan Your Visit

Culture in Durham, NC

9. Experience Broadway at Durham Performing Arts Center (DPAC)

DPAC hosts more than 200 one-of-a-kind performances each year, making it the center for live entertainment in North Carolina. Featuring 2,700 seats and cutting-edge sound and video, DPAC holds Broadway productions, concerts and family events. DPAC is also home to the annual American Dance Festival, which is a great way to experience Durham’s culture!

Events & Tickets

10. Celebrate the OutSouth Queer Film Festival

OutSouth Queer Film Festival is an annual LGBT film festival held and produced by the Carolina Theater every August. It’s also the second largest queer film festival in the Southeast, featuring different shorts, documentaries, and feature films every year. Moreover, the festival celebrates a glimpse of queer life and brings the community together through art.

  • Admission: All-Access Passes cost $100 each.
Read more

11. Create at Durham Fruit Company

If you’re looking for more attractions in Durham, check out the Durham Fruit Company. Also known as ‘The Fruit’, this 22,000 sqft warehouse complex acts as an art space and creative playground in Downtown Durham. Not only does it host art exhibitions, but there’s also great DJ performances at night!

  • Time: Open 24 hours (Monday to Thursday), 6:00 PM to 11:45 PM (Friday to Sunday)
Book Online

Restaurants in Durham, NC

12. Grab a Steak at Metro 8 Steakhouse

After checking out all the things to do in Durham, it’s time to feast, and Metro 8 Steakhouse is a must! This upscale steakhouse mainly serves American cuisine, and their best-sellers are crab-stuffed shrimp, filet mignon, and lamb chops. They also have dishes for vegetarians and the perfect wine to pair with whichever meal you choose.

Menu

13. Have a Picnic

Picnic is a go-to spot for pulled pork, fried chicken, and more in Durham. As a barbecue joint, Picnic offers smoky meat paired with side dishes that will taste “like your best memories at the county fair”. After the main dish, they also offer quality desserts that you shouldn’t miss.

14. Dine at Elmo’s Diner

You can never go wrong with pancakes and milkshakes at Elmo’s Diner! As a local favorite, Elmo’s treats their customers as friends and serves delicious (and fast!) food. Take a seat in one of their booths or grab a coffee at the bar. One thing is for sure, Elmo’s will make you feel right at home in Durham, NC!

  • Time: Open every day from 7:00 AM to 3:30 PM
View Website

15. Fill Up at Rose’s Noodles, Dumplings and Sweets

One of the best food places to visit in Durham is Rose’s Noodles, Dumplings, and Sweets! This restaurant includes a pastry counter, noodle joint, and dumpling bar in one. They offer desserts, pastries, ice creams, and an East Asian menu, the perfect treat after a day exploring the city.

Where is Durham?

Durham, popularly known as the “Bull City,” is the county seat of Durham County in North Carolina in the United States. Small swathes of the city’s boundaries stretch into Orange and Wake counties. The city is situated along the Eno River in the Piedmont area’s east-central section. Durham is the hub of the Durham-Chapel Hill Metropolitan Area, which spans four counties.

Parks in Durham

Durham is a naturalist’s paradise. You’ll find green lush patches of forests, even while taking a small walk or looking for rooms for rent in Durham.

Surrounded by parks, gardens, and protected woods, it’s simple to get away from the rush and bustle of city life and breathe in some fresh air. The American Tobacco Trail, a 22-mile abandoned railroad route that was turned into a paved greenway for walkers, cyclists, and runners, lies right in the city’s backyard.

Check out Duke Forest, if 22 miles of trail isn’t enough for you. Much of the woodland in the surrounding areas is managed by the university for research, teaching, and leisure. You’ll find yourself at Duke Forest about 10 minutes outside of downtown. Local hikers, runners, mountain bikers, and even horseback riders may enjoy the forest’s pathways.

Perhaps all you want to do is get out and smell the roses. Duke Gardensapproximately 400,000 people each year from all over the world, and admission to their 55-acre botanic park is free. Living in Durham is tempting to people who seek the best of both worlds since it is so easy to get from the city back into nature.

Culture of Durham

If you’re visiting or looking for rooms for rent in Durham, you’ll notice how much the city loves the arts. Here are only a few examples of the city’s cultural offerings:

DPAC – the Durham Performing Arts Center hosts Broadway shows, concerts, and even stand-up comedians in downtown Durham. DPAC is consistently ranked in the top ten theaters in the United States, with over half a million visitors each year. With over 200 annual performances, it certainly has something for everyone.

Moogfest – a music event that takes place every year in downtown Durham. People go from all around the world to celebrate the confluence of music and technology for four days. Ticket holders may attend talks that bridge the gap between engineering and creativity, as well as see cutting-edge performances by early pioneers in electronic music.

Third Friday – every third Friday of the month, the city of Durham conducts an art walk and gallery crawl, bringing locals and visitors together to browse for art in a bustling environment. Third Friday is a free event during which art is presented in various locations across the city. As a result, visitors have the opportunity to connect with both art and local businesses.

Nasher Art Museum – visit the ground-breaking collection of modern art that includes African art, European medieval art, classical antiquities, and Native American art.

Durham offers a wide range of activities and experiences, including museums, festivals, art galleries, and theater.
