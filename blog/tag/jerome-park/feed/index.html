Where is Jerome Park?

Jerome Park is located in New York City’s North Bronx region, just east of the Hudson River. It belongs to the “Annexed District,” a group of neighborhoods that were moved over to Bronx County in 1874. Jerome Park includes the Van Cortlandt Village, the Kingsbridge Heights Village, and Norwood. The Jerome Park Reservoir in Jerome Park was emptied in 2008 to make way for the construction of the Croton Water Filtration Plant.

Public Transit in Jerome Park

Walkability

Apart from there being affordable rooms for rent in Jerome Park, it’s great to know that Jerome Park neighborhoods are among the most walkable in New York City. With an average Walk Score of 93, this means you can easily perform daily errands on foot.

Roads & Traffic

According to the New York State Department of Transportation, Bronx County is home to 10 regional highways. The Bronx has the third-highest population density in New York City, so you’re advised to take public transportation where possible.

Nearby Train Stations

Two of New York City’s subway systems pass through the Bronx: the Interborough Rapid Transit Company (IRT), and the Independent Subway System (IND). The closest transit lines to Jerome Park are the MTA Subway’s 1, 4, and D routes. Rooms for rent in Jerome Park are a sure winner if they’re near a train station.

Nearby Bus Stops

The Bronx is serviced by a vast network of local and limited services as well as express routes. The BX3, BXM3, and BXM4 bus routes pass near Jerome Park. You can find out the nearest bus stop by checking out the Bronx bus routes on the Metropolitan Transportation Authority website.

Learn about the Valentine-Varian House, which was restored and opened as the Museum of Bronx History. The Valentine home was occupied by American, British, and Hessian troops during the Revolutionary War.
