Must do outdoor activities

1. Centennial Park

Centennial Park, located in Nashville’s West End neighborhood, is a green sanctuary that provides a peaceful escape from the flashing lights of Music City’s Honk Tonk Highway. This beautiful natural environment, which spans 132 acres, is one of the most popular parks for those looking to explore Nashville tourism. This park has jogging paths, perfect spots for picnics and a lake for paddle boating amassing the best things to do in Nashville.

2. Shelby Park

Shelby Park is a huge city park along the Cumberland River about three miles east of downtown Nashville. Playgrounds, a dog park, baseball fields, two golf courses, and a community center put this among the best places to visit in Nashville.

Website

3. Nashville Zoo

If you’re looking for the best things to do in Nashville, head to this outdoor adventure! Ride the Soaring Eagle zip line, learn about the Zoo’s history at the Historic Home, and explore immersive animal habitats like you’ve never seen them before. You’ll also experience the beautiful new Andean bear or Sumatran tiger exhibits.

Don’t miss out on the sports scene

1. Centennial Sportsplex Ice Skating

When looking for cool things to do in Nashville with your friends or a date, there’s nothing better than ice skating. Enjoy the thrills, adrenaline – and maybe some falls if you’re a newbie – at any of the public skating sessions on offer. Bring a group, have a party, or participate in organized sports leagues like ice hockey. Skate rentals are available, so there’s no need to bring your own!

2. First Horizon Park

First Horizon Park, originally known as First Tennessee Park, is a baseball park in Nashville. It’s one of the most exciting places to visit in Nashville, if you want to immerse yourself in the sports scene here. It’s the home of the Triple-A Nashville Sounds of the Triple-A East. The state-of-the-art facility is fully prepared to transform into a concert stage, a beer tasting line, or a soccer pitch.

3. Nissan Stadium

One of the best things to do in Nashville is catch a live game of The Tennessee Titans at Nissan Stadium. The home of the Titans also hosts Tennessee State University football games, the late-December Music City Bowl, and a multitude of concerts and other events throughout the year.

Must visit museums

1. Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum

The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum is a real history-rich place to visit in Nashville. It has major museums and research institutions dedicated to the preservation and interpretation of American folk music in the world.

Plan Your Visit

2. National Museum of African American Music

One of the best things to do in Nashville is to immerse yourself in the city’s beautiful history. The museum showcases the musical genres inspired, made, or influenced by African-Americans. Its location at Fifth + Broadway in Downtown area, as opposed to historically-Black Jefferson Street, has been controversial.

3. Adventure Science Center

The Adventure Science Center is a non-profit science museum which exhibits over 175 interactive displays covering biology, physics, visual perception, cognition, air and space, energy, and earth science. If you’re looking for cool things to do in Nashville with kids, it’s the perfect place to visit!

Soak in the culture

1. Downtown Nashville

Downtown, the heart of the Nashville Tourism, is a powerhouse of entertainment, food, and cultural events. This also comes under the best things to do in Nashville as it’s always bustling, with a vibrant pub scene and a reputation for star sightings.

Upcoming Events

2. Belle Meade Historic Site & Winery

Take a tour of the Greek-Revival mansion and numerous historic outbuildings, which have been restored to their turn-of-the-century look. While a costumed guide explains the history of the site, you can tour the dairy and rebuilt slave quarters. Following the tour, stop by the on-site winery for a complimentary tasting. Hands down, one of the most exhilarating places to visit in Nashville.

  • Timings: Open Daily 9am – 5pm

3. Nashville’s Tractor Tour

Plow through Downtown Nashville for nonstop fun, dancing and partying. This party tractor is music, a light-up dance floor, onboard bar, and more – on wheels! This is definitely one of the best things to do in Nashville, as it’s the biggest and wildest party that lets you see the city from a different perspective. They even take bonus complimentary snaps of your group and send them over 72 hours after your tour.

Explore restaurants, bars & shopping centers

1. Hattie B’s Hot Chicken

The Nashville tourism package isn’t complete without hot chicken! Made legendary by Prince’s Hot Chicken Shack in North Nashville, the fiery fowl is fried, then basted in a nostril-burning spice mix. A must try and one of the best places to visit in Nashville for an amazing experience!

Website

2. The Patterson House

The Patterson House, one of the originals in Nashville’s artisan cocktail culture, still knows how to shake ’em up and pour ’em out. Order anything from the menu or have one of the bartenders make something up on the spot. Either way, it’s a win.

Website

3. Two Old Hippies

This indie clothing/music shop in one of Nashville’s trendiest neighborhoods is one of the best places to visit for deep-pocketed music lovers. The shop has it all: men’s and women’s clothing, jewelry, instruments, home accents and much more!

Getting to Know Nashville, TN

Nashville is a golden nugget for anyone who loves to explore a new city and learn about its history. Nicknamed “Music City” during the 1950s, Nashville has a lot to offer its residents. From diverse cuisine to mesmerizing live music, the city is filled with cultural activities to take part in. Home to Gibson guitars and the world’s oldest radio show, once you’re in Nashville, it is hard to look back.

How Transit Friendly is Nashville, TN?

Nashville is ranked 31st among the most congested cities in the US. While it is not the worst rank, it still means that you need to account for traffic when commuting to work. Finding parking in Nashville is much easier than other cities in the US, and most metered parking is free after 6 in the evening.

1. Walkability in Nashville

Walkability differs in different neighborhoods. Downtown and Midtown Nashville are fairly walkable, and you can complete your chores easily without a car. Areas such as Berry Hill, Tennessee State University campus and Bordeaux Area are the least walkable parts of the city.

2. Roads/traffic in Nashville

Nashville can be quite congested, especially during rush hours. Getting to and from Downtown Nashville is easy, but try to avoid bottleneck areas like Interstate 40 at the eastbound exchange near exit 209 where drivers try to access highways 24, 40 and 70.

3. Taxis in Nashville

Like any city, Nashville has a fairly standard cab situation. Companies such as Magic Taxi and Green Cab let you book trips from the airport ahead of time. If you’re looking for rooms for rent in Nashville or Broadway, you can easily hail a cab for your trip. You can also avail Uber and Lyft services when you’re in a rush.

4. Public transportation in Nashville

Though the city does not have a subway system, you can avail bus routes serviced by the Nashville MTA. Citizens often overlook the Music City Circuit, which is the free bus route provided by MTA. It has two routes, the Green Circuit which runs from the Gulch to the Bicentennial Mall, and the Blue Circuit which runs between the Riverfront Station to the Tennessee State University campus area. Electric scooters are another great alternative that residents can opt for, companies like Lime and Bird have apps which allow users to track down their nearest station once they’ve entered the required payment information.

5. Airports in Nashville

The Nashville International Airport is the busiest airport in Tennessee, operating over 500 flights. Located in Southeast Nashville, the airport serves an average of 12 million passengers per year. It offers the most extensive routes in the state and has limited international flights as well.

6. Cycling in Nashville

Nashville is quite bike-friendly and even has free bike-sharing programs for its residents. The B-Cycle program offers daily, monthly or yearly subscriptions and users can easily pick up a bike from the nearest station to explore the city. Once you’re done, find another B-Cycle station to drop off your bike, and you are good to go.

Nightlife in Nashville, TN

Nashville is famous for its whiskey and bourbon, so it comes as no surprise that it has a bustling nightlife. When you want drinks, you won’t be too hard-pressed to find a decent place around the city. From the mysterious Old Glory to the alluring Skull’s Rainbow Room and Music City Pub Crawl, the nightlife in Nashville is worth exploring.

Cultural Activities in Nashville, TN

Nashville isn’t known as the “Music City” for nothing. Since the fifties, the music scene has been booming in this city, and has found mentions in the Rolling Stone, SPIN and Nylon publications as well. From the famous Bluebird Café, where numerous superstars found their start, to famous venues like the Schermerhorn Symphony Center which provide beautiful live music even today, Nashville has definitely secured its title.
