Exciting Outdoor Activities in Raleigh

1. Pullen Park

Pullen Park is an excellent nature park and one of the best places to visit in Raleigh. Complete with boat rentals, hiking trails, and a miniature railway- you’re in for a wholesome adventure!

2. Yates Mill County Park

If you love walking or biking on trails, you’ll love the fact that the Raleigh area has 180 miles of the greenway to explore. Likewise, Yates Mill County Park offers the best things to do in Raleigh! You can indulge in countless events focused on natural, cultural, and recreational opportunities.

  • Hours of Operation: Open Daily- 8 a.m. to Sunset
  • Pricing: Adults – $7, Senior – $4, Youth – $4. Children 6 and under are free.

3. Mordecai Historic Park

Mordecai House is the centerpiece of Mordecai Historic Park, adjacent to the Historic Oakwood neighborhood. Moreover, it’s the oldest house in Raleigh in its original location.

Cultural Places to Visit in Raleigh

1. North Carolina Opera

While looking to experience the best things to do in Raleigh, don’t forget to visit the North Carolina Opera. In fact, it is dedicated to presenting operatic performances at the highest level throughout the Triangle.

2. Historic Oakwood

Historic Oakwood is a leafy, residential neighborhood known for well-preserved Victorian houses. If you’re eager to explore early 20th century homes in a variety of styles, from Queen Anne to neoclassical and Craftsman, head to this neighborhood!

Website

3. Raleigh Little Theatre

One of the best things to do in Raleigh is to attend a performing arts venue! It’s a community theatre that produces 10 to 11 full productions annually. Further, it conducts youth and adult theatre education programs.

Explore Popular Bars, Shopping Centers & Restaurants in Raleigh

1. Fox Liquor Bar

This bar describes itself as a “subterranean drinking den” that serves both classic and contemporary drinks. Signature cocktails, wines and craft beers, and light cuisine such as sliders, are served in this elegant setting.

Website

2. The Big Easy

The Big Easy is one of the best restaurants in Raleigh. Here, you’ll find delectable food with a boisterous ambiance that won’t disappoint. Cajun culture and cuisine are combined with southern hospitality at this restaurant and bar.

Website

3. FOUNDATION Raleigh

This low-key basement pub serves innovative drinks, local craft beer, and has an industrial vibe. Moreover, FOUNDATION is among restaurants in Raleigh that serve bar food paired with their homemade libations. Certainly, one of the best places to visit in Raleigh for a contented meal.

The Ridgewood Shopping Center is an eclectic mix of independent, locally-owned shops that are best known for wide-range shopping. Moreover, its extensive selection of high-end party decorations and gift items make it among the best places to visit in Raleigh to buy a souvenir.
