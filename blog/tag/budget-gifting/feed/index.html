]]> The Christmas season is finally upon us! And we’re hooked onto some fab Christmas decoration ideas. Anyway, this year more than ever because of COVID-19, we’re all in need of some uplifting holiday cheer. And the perfect thing to do that? Beautiful Christmas gift ideas for our loved ones, of course!

The Christmas season is the perfect time to show your appreciation for your family and friends, so make sure to get them a thoughtful gift that they’ll love.

Even though it’s the most wonderful time of the year, the Christmas season can also be a source of major financial stress. So if you’re stressed about finances this year, don’t worry! We have some wonderful Christmas gift ideas that can still be bought on a budget. Also remember, your family and friends will enjoy the gesture no matter what the price tag is.

Here are 8 fantastic Christmas gift ideas under $50 that will bring everyone in your life some holiday cheer. And don’t forget to skim through some warm Christmas decoration ideas on the side too!

1. Christmas gift ideas for fragrance lovers: Scented candles

Candles are always a crowd-pleaser when it comes to Christmas decoration ideas, and you can never have too many! A scented candle is one of the best gift Christmas gift ideas for a person who’s hard to shop for. A candle is a perfect way to brighten up a room and make your home feels cozy during the cold winter months. Not to mention, candles are also great stocking fillers too!

Check out stores like TJ Maxx and HomeGoods for the perfect scented candle!

2. A thoughtful book

Do you have a family member or friend that loves to read? Browse the New York Times Bestseller List and own one this Christmas gift idea! From self-help books to cookbooks, there’s bound to be reading material for everyone! Need a little inspiration? Why not check out some of the New York Times best sellers.

3. Bath and shower products

There’s no such thing as too many pampering products, and self-care is always a winner… especially in 2020. From bath bombs to loofas, there are tons of awesome bath and shower product possibilities to make for the best Christmas gift ideas. And don’t forget, these types of products aren’t hugely expensive – even better! Check out stores like Lush and Bath and Body Works to treat your loved ones.

4. Christmas gift ideas for those who love staying in: Fuzzy socks and slippers

Stay cozy this holiday season and give the gift of cozy socks and slippers to keep warm during the upcoming cold winter months! This is one of the sweetest Christmas gift ideas that will definitely be put to good use, especially as so many people are working from home.

Check out Target and TJ Maxx for cute socks and slippers in a range of styles and colors.

]]> Giving unique gifts to your loved ones around the holiday season can be quite a daunting task. Especially, if you are operating on a tight budget. While you want to be giving and generous, you also want to buy groceries too! Just in case you didn’t know, a big price tag doesn’t always mean better. Affordable but thoughtful gifts really do exist.

So, why not prepare a gift-giving budget? You can create specific budgets for special occasions, such as a holiday gift budget that you can work towards in terms of saving. If money is super-tight, you might not have much to spend… and that’s okay!

Check out these thoughtful gifts that show your friends how much you care and hey… they won’t break the bank either.

Plants over flowers, anyday!

Sure everyone likes receiving flowers as presents, but give them a week and they’re gone. Instead, we recommend going for unique gifts like succulents, and miniature indoor plants! They’re the kind of gifts your loved ones would appreciate a lot more.

Why? because they last longer, and don’t require any active care like most other plants do!

Dinner dates with your loved one? Yes, please!

A fun dinner is always a good idea!

You can’t go wrong with a quick bite to eat on a budget… we’re thinking of a delicious bowl of ramen, a burrito, or just a classic all-American burger. All of these meals can be a thoughtful way to express your love without breaking your bank account.

Want to show you care by cooking instead? How about some some easy Christmas cooking with the roommates.

The wonders of gifting vanity to your loved ones.

These make a great gift item for both the beauty-conscious friend and the one that needs some TLC. Sometimes getting them something they might not regularly buy for themselves is a sign of a truly well-thought-out gift! These could also be thought-out products, like curly-hair products if your loved one has curly hair!

Gift baskets & bundles of joy!

This option is fun and creative, and can be pretty cheap if you strategize correctly! It’s totally up to you when it comes to choosing what you stash in your hamper, and just remember – the more personal the items, the better the gift!

Throw together some classic favorites, like candies, trendy and cute stationery, lotions, and potions, and maybe a small-value gift card to their favorite restaurant!

Game nights & board games.

The gift that keeps on giving!

Pop open a bottle (or two!) of wine and have a games night with the people you like best. By gifting a board game, you’ll make the night even more fun than simply getting the gang together. To top it off, you’ll be able to play games every time you get together. Now that’s a great gift on a budget!

Check out: Virtual Christmas Party Ideas For 2020

Make a donation in their name & give back!

One great option is to make a small donation in their name to a cause they really care about. Whether it’s providing cancer treatment to kids, saving endangered wildlife, or finding a home for cats and dogs in need, a small donation can go a long way and be a really lovely gift, especially around the holidays when it’s all about being grateful and giving back!

When you give a gift, it should be because you want to, not because you feel you have to.

And you definitely shouldn’t let gift-giving derail your budget or throw you off track when it comes to your financial goals.

Ultimately, it’s the thought that counts!
