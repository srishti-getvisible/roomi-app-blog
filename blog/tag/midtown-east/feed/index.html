Midtown New York is characterized by an intense work culture, as the skyscrapers filling the area house endless office buildings and storefronts. Besides, it’s also a midtown destination for world-renowned buildings, museums, restaurants, shopping hubs, public events, and audio tours provided in many languages.

Things to do in Midtown East NYC

We’ll help you travel and experience the unique places in Midtown East. There are so many majestic and fun places to discover in addition to the eminent Rockefeller Center, The Museum of Modern Art and MetLife Building.

Places for sightseeing in Midtown New York

Mix and mingle with the white-collar workers seething through Grand Central at rush hour, drink in the Deco masterpiece of the Chrysler Building, and shop the stores on Fifth Avenue for an unforgettable New York experience.

The Morgan Library & Museum: Midtown East, NYC

Just a ten-minute walk from Grand Central in New York City, The Morgan Library & Museum began as the private library of financier Pierpont Morgan. Today, the Morgan serves as a museum, research library, musical venue, architectural landmark, and historic site. Likewise, it’s home to an enormous collection of literary and historical works.

Grand Central Terminal

Ranked at #10 in 1,292 things to do in New York, Grand Central Terminal is the most exquisite location in Midtown East. Opened to the public in 1913, this historic train terminal with great engineering, it is a world-famous landmark in Midtown, NYC. Moreover, it is the home of Metro-North Railroad, a subway station, iconic restaurants, shops, and the world’s most alluring Apple Store.

St. Patrick’s Church

Midtown East NYC, is one of the busiest, most exciting areas in the world, the bustling businessmen and shoppers only interrupted by the calm serenity of St. Patrick’s Cathedral. Also, this church is an iconic American Gothic Revival! The cornerstone to St. Patrick’s Cathedral was laid in 1858, it is one of the most beautifully built churches across the world.

Restaurants in Midtown New York

Teppen Ramen

Counter service ordering from an array of Japanese dishes, specializing in ramen, with cozy décor is what makes this place special!

Gyu-Kaku Japanese BBQ

An interactive restaurant with self-cook BBQ. The highlight of the restaurant is that it has high ceilings and the atmosphere is relaxed with moderate lighting. Indeed, perfect for a meal after tiring travels in Midtown East NYC.

Hillstone

An upscale chain eatery featuring steak, seafood & pasta alongside specialty cocktails.

The Smith

Trendy American bistro specializing in American food & drinks. Specifically, it’s an upbeat hangout & brunch favorite.

Little Collins 3rd Ave

A stylish, warm cafe and coffee shop for pastries, all-day breakfast and lunch.

212 Steakhouse

An upscale restaurant with authentic Kobe, Wagyu & prime beef, seafood, cocktails & a long wine list. Indeed, a must try in Midtown, New York!

Fun things to do in Midtown East NYC

Shop at Saks Fifth Avenue

Whether actually purchasing something or merely window shopping, visitors can find some great, high-end goods at Saks Fifth Avenue. Moreover, during Christmas, Saks puts on a dazzling display of lights across the department store’s exterior each evening. This holiday spectacle perfectly coincides with the merriest time of the year.

FDNY Fire Zone

Rockefeller Center is home to the FDNY’s hands-on state-of-the-art fire safety learning center in Midtown East, NYC. Additionally, it features displays, multimedia demonstrations, and awesome souvenirs of the New York Fire Department.

Eden Fine Art Gallery, Midtown East NYC

Eden Fine Art has over 2000 carefully curated pieces of energetic and colorful perspective art from over 26 renowned international artists. From the world’s most famous and sought-after graffiti artists to well established contemporary sculptors, it celebrates provocative and inspiring original art.

Top of The Rock: Best View Spot in Midtown, New York

This observatory is on top of Rockefeller Center, with an ocean-liner style design, offers breath-taking views. In fact, it offers the best views of New York City skyline and the famous Central Park!
