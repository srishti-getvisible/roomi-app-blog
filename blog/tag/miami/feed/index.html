Outdoors in Miami

1. Explore Oleta State Park

Miami may not have mountains, but the running and mountain biking trails at the Oleta State Park make up for it. You can also take part in paddle activities in protected Mangrove areas!

  • Costs: Free for children under 6. Prices then range from $1 to $10 depending on the vehicle, number of people, and the time of the day.
  • Timings: 8 AM to sundown. Reservations are required for overnight stays.

2. Visit the dog-friendly Hobie Beach

If you want your furry friend to enjoy a trip to Miami alongside you, then visiting Hobie Beach is definitely one of the best things to do in Miami. Wade in the shallow waters with your pup, and watch the sun go down for a relaxing end to your day.

  • Costs: Entry is free
  • Timings: 6:30 AM to 8 PM daily

3. Enjoy the fresh air at David T. Kennedy Park

From picnic tables to biking trails, you will find it all in David T. Kennedy Park. One of the best places to visit in Miami if you want to relax outdoors with a picnic, or take a walk with your dog.

  • Costs: Entry is free
  • Timings: Sunrise to Sunset

4. Admire the beauty of the Venetian Pool

Venetian Pool isn’t a plain old water park that you would find in any other city. In fact, it is an almost century-old wonder. Created from a coral rock quarry in 1923, the Venetian Pool is famous across the globe for its beautiful views, waterfalls, and cave-like grottos.

  • Costs: Vary per season
  • Timings: Vary per month

The Perez Art Museum Miami is one of the best places to visit in Miami, not only for those with an appreciation for art, but also for those who like to admire unique architecture. With rounded walls and hanging gardens, the building is truly worthy of being called an Art Museum!

How Transit Friendly is Fort Lauderdale, FL?

For someone who is still searching for rooms for rent in Fort Lauderdale, FL, or is still in the middle of their move, a private vehicle might not always be an option. Especially if you’re a student, or moving from far away.

But luckily, there are a number of options available in Fort Lauderdale aside from private vehicles.

Broward County Transit (BCT)

BCT can take you practically anywhere you want to go within Broward County: work, schools, shopping, libraries, parks, cultural and civic activities, and other places of interest. BCT’s public transportation services cover approximately 410 square miles with a total operating fleet of 299 buses on fixed routes. With over 1600 bus stops, you’ll probably find yourself near a BCT route almost anywhere you go. Routes and schedules can additionally be found on the Broward County Transit website.

Brightline

Brightline is a privately owned and operated express train with stops in downtown West Palm Beach, downtown Fort Lauderdale, and downtown Miami. For more information and ticketing, visit the Brightline website.

B-Cycle/AvMed Rides

Broward B-Cycle, currently operating as AvMed Rides, was created through an agreement between Broward County, the Florida Department of Transportation, and B-Cycle as a means of reducing traffic congestion. Bike-sharing is an inexpensive mobility option that complements the use of public transportation, walking, and ridesharing and offers a sustainable alternative to single-occupant vehicle use. The City of Fort Lauderdale currently authorizes 16 stations within the city limits.

Riverwalk Water Trolley

Crossing the New River (FOR FREE!) is now easier than ever with the new Riverwalk Water Trolley, brought to you by the Downtown Fort Lauderdale Transportation Management Association (DFLTMA) in partnership with the City of Fort Lauderdale and Riverwalk Fort Lauderdale. Route and stop locations can be found at the Riverwalk Water Trolley website.

Sun Trolley

The City of Fort Lauderdale’s community shuttle, formerly known as Sun Trolley, is a popular transportation option. Whether you’re in Fort Lauderdale for work or play, the community shuttle is the perfect choice to get around. These shuttles provide access to world-class shopping on Las Olas Boulevard, Fort Lauderdale’s sparkling beaches, and endless options for dining and entertainment. Routes and schedules can be found on the Sun Trolley website.

Tri-Rail

Tri-Rail is a commuter rail line linking Miami, Fort Lauderdale, and West Palm Beach. It is run by the South Florida Regional Transportation Authority (SFRTA). The 70.9-mile-long system has 18 stations along the Southeast Florida coast. The system connects directly to Amtrak at numerous stations and the Metrorail in Hialeah (Miami) at the Tri-Rail and Metrorail Transfer Station.

Water Taxis

Water Taxi provides waterborne transit service throughout Broward County. For more information, including fares, schedules, and routes, visit the Water Taxi website.
