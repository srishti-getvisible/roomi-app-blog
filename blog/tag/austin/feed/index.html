Outdoor activities in Austin

1. Go hiking through a tropical forest

One of the most interesting places to visit in Austin is the Palmetto State Park. Although you’ll be surrounded by overgrown vegetation, the Palmetto plants and the scenic beauty have a Jurassic vibe to it. Take a walk along the flat trails which cut through the algae-covered swamps surrounding the palmettos, or, if you’re feeling adventurous, you can canoe or kayak across the swamps as well. As the only park in the state with tropical vegetation, The Palmetto State Park should be a mandatory stop on your Austin tour.

Costs: $3 for Adults and Free for children 12 years or under.

Timings: Open daily

2. Go for a camping trip to Pedernales Falls State Park

Want to find cool things to do in Austin? Camping at the Pedernales Falls State Park should be high up on your list! With white sandy beaches, fascinating rock formations, and the scenic beauty of the Pedernales River, stargazing at this state park is undoubtedly one of the best things to do in Austin.

Costs: Free for children up to 12 years, and $6 years for 13 years and older

Timings: Open daily, 24 hours.

3. Go on a canoeing trip with Cook’s Canoes

Located a 20-minute drive way from Austin, Cook’s Canoes is an old-school canoe rental space where you can rent canoes or kayaks for 7-mile, 11-mile or even overnight trips! What better way to explore the Lower Colorado?

Costs: For tandem boats: $50 per day. For solo boats: $35.00 per day. For overnight trips, rental rates are $75 per tandem, or $45 per solo boat.

Timings: Summer: Open from Wednesday to Sunday: 9 AM to 6 PM; Winter: Open on weekends: 9 AM to 5 PM; on weekdays open by reservation only.

4. Plan a family outing to the Lady Bird Lake

Austin tourism boasts of numerous outdoor activities, where you get to explore nature to your heart’s content. The Lady Bird Lake is surrounded by miles of hiking and biking trails, with plenty of water activity options as well. It’s one of the best things to do in Austin with your family!

Costs: Entry is free, pay for activities.

Timings: Open every day from 5 AM to midnight.

Sports in Austin

1. The first Rugby Club in Southwestern United States

Founded in 1967, the Austin Blacks Rugby Club has many “firsts” under its belt. From becoming one of the first founding members of Texas Rugby Union, to being the first rugby club in the US to purchase their own grounds.

How Transit Friendly is Austin, Texas?

Walkability

While not considered the most walkable city in the U.S. (Austin is the 29th most walkable large city in the US with a “walk score” of 41 out of 100), Austin is home to several neighborhoods that are more pedestrian-friendly than others.

Traffic

The constant influx of residents is putting a hurt on the city’s infrastructure. There are only two main arteries for commuting (I-35 and the TX-1 Loop aka MoPac), so peak hours are pretty miserable. No joke—it will take you 30 minutes to go three miles. If possible, find a job before you find a place to live and plan your commute accordingly. Those with jobs downtown will find that it is well worth it to spend the extra dough to be closer to work, as the bus system (CapMetro) is the only viable public transportation option for Austin residents.

Public Transport

Austin’s public Capital Metro Bus System serves a population of more than 1 million in its 535-square mile service area. With a system of more than 1,600 bus stops and 82 routes, Central Texas is served by local, MetroRapid limited-stop, MetroFlyer, MetroExpress, and University of Texas Shuttle routes.

Cycling

With numerous hills, elaborate trails, specialist shops, and cyclist-friendly laws, Austin is an ideal city for bike enthusiasts.

Think Karaoke — but punk. It’s everything the punk-loving amateur karaoke singer in you desires, with jams from non-chart-topping banks ranging from Crass to Sleater-Kinney.

The Moonlight Towers

Some think of these as odd, others call them romantic, but most refer to them as odd-romantic. They’re the ultimate Austin quirk, especially since it’s the only city in the world that still uses them as street lights! Here’s a very cool documentary on the moonlight towers that you can check out.

Barton Springs’ forever kind of cool.
