Getting To Know Las Vegas

Although founded in 1905, 1931 was a pivotal year for Las Vegas with Nevada legalizing casino gambling. Following WWII, Las Vegas was synonymous with gambling, big-name entertainment and high-end, lavish hotels.

Located in a basin in the Mojave Desert, Las Vegas has a rocky and desert-like landscape and is surrounded by mountain ranges. With a typical subtropical hot desert climate, the weather is hot all year round with long, hot summers and warm transitional seasons.

Top Neighborhoods In Las Vegas

The average rent for an apartment in Las Vegas is $1191 with this figure on a constant rise. Vegas has a variety of neighborhoods from super luxury to more affordable ones, usually depending on their proximity to downtown.

Summerlin:

One of the largest planned communities in Las Vegas, Summerlin is a great place to rent an apartment in Las Vegas, though the rent here is higher than the citywide average. This community is home to 230 parks and a 150-mile trail system with easy access to the Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area.

West Las Vegas:

The West Las Vegas District is a historic district located Northwest of the Las Vegas Strip. The place is lively and has strong African-American roots along with being cultural in Las Vegas.

Aliante:

Also located in the North, Aliante is the perfect place to rent an apartment in Las Vegas if you want to experience the outdoors with its proximity to Sheep Mountains and abundance of green spaces, hiking trails and the Nature Discovery Park.

How Transit Friendly is Las Vegas?

Las Vegas can be a busy city with all the tourists and conventions but has an expansive bus network and decent connectivity by monorail around the strip as well. Ridesharing apps and taxis are also an easy way to get around if you are renting an apartment in Las Vegas.

Airport:

McCarran International Airport is the only airport serving the city and is five miles from downtown Las Vegas.

Public Transportation:

While most residents rely on cars, buses are the most affordable way to travel and routes reach most places around the city. Buses run 24/7 on the Deuce route along Las Vegas Boulevard, and from 5.30am–1.30am elsewhere. The Las Vegas Monorail is also a convenient way to get around the strip.

Taxi Services:

Taxi services along with Uber and Lyft serve the area 24/7 as well. Most hotels and busy spots do sport taxi stands and it is acceptable to approach an empty cab with its light.

Night Life in Las Vegas:

Take a Night Tour:

The cool of the evening is a great time to explore the Las Vegas Strip and the Big Bus Night Tour is a great way to do this. This open-top bus will take you to all the landmarks and includes a free walking tour down Fremont Promenade as well.

Visit one of the many Casinos and Clubs:

The various Casinos and Clubs are an iconic part of ‘Sin City’ with countless options to choose from a whole host of shows, carnivals and big-name performances to entertain anyone. Make sure to check out these shows after you’ve found your apartment in Las Vegas.

Cultural Activitites in Las Vegas:

Visit the High Roller Observation Wheel:

One of the tallest in the world, this observation wheel offers some of the best views of Las Vegas as it lifts you 550 feet in the air, with the pods having an unrestricted view on all sides. One ride lasts 30 minutes and can accommodate groups as well.

See the Famous Fountain Shows:

Las Vegas is known for the famous Bellagio Fountains, and the shows are most impressive with the lights at night. Water jets and lights are choreographed to music, with shows happening every day of the week from early afternoon until midnight.

Visit the Circus Circus and the Adventuredome Theme Park:

The Circus Circus is not just a hotel and casino, but also a great place to be entertained. On the Circus Circus Carnival Midway, you can catch trapeze artists, aerial silk acrobatics, juggling, and feats of strength. The Adventuredome is contained by a giant dome and is loaded with high-adrenaline roller coasters, a roller skating rink, rock climbing, and miniature golf.
