How transit-friendly is San Mateo?

If you want to live in San Mateo, you’ll probably need a car of your own. Some commuters use public transit to get to and from work, while others walk or cycle, but driving is definitely the easiest way to get around the city.

1. Walkability

With a walk score of 65, San Mateo has average walkability, but you will probably need a car or public transport when running errands. The most walkable neighborhoods in the city are Downtown, North Central and Hayward Park.

2. Roads/traffic

The average commute time in San Mateo is about 27.8 minutes, which is slightly longer than the national average. Most people prefer to drive round the city, and if you don’t have a car, you may still be able to carpool with a driver in your community. Public transport is used by approximately 10% of commuters on the road, with fewer people opting for biking or walking.

The city of San Mateo has also undertaken projects to curb traffic in the city and implement Neighborhood Traffic Actions Plans to make roads safer for the community.

3. Taxis

Apart from Uber and Lyft, there are several taxi services you can call when you’re in San Mateo. Typically, fares for taxis start at $9.30 and go up every three miles. Dedicated airport taxi services are also available.

4. Public transportation

The San Mateo County Transit District provides public transit services to the city. Commuters can avail themselves of the CalTrain, shuttles and SanTrans bus services for their daily commute. The city has also introduced a Try Transit for Free program where commuters can take public transit for free to get to work.

5. Airports

If you’re planning to fly in or out of San Mateo, then the San Francisco International Airport would be your most convenient option. Other airports nearby include San Jose International Airport and Oakland International Airport, though they are further out.

6. Cycling

San Mateo is designated as a “Bicycle-friendly Community”, where the roads have been made safer for cyclists. The city adopted a bicycle master plan back in 2011, and as a part of the initiative, introduced better infrastructure on roads, more parking spaces for bicycles, and started a bike share program called Bay Bikes. Most bicyclists agree that spring in San Mateo is the best time to enjoy a scenic view and safer travel in the city.
