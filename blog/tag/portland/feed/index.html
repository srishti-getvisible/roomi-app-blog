]]> Moving to the “City of Roses”? Since being named this in 1888, the people of Portland continue to practice rose gardening now. This began with Leo Samuel – the first to plant and provide a pair of shears outside his garden for people to snip roses and take some for themselves.

Portland is the largest city in Oregon and the 26th most populated city in the USA. The name ‘Portland’ was decided with a flip of a coin by the city founders Asa Lovejoy and Francis Pettygrove, when they couldn’t agree on a name for their hometown. Pettygrove was born and raised in Portland, Maine and that’s how the capital of Oregon was named! Whether you are planning to move here or looking for a vacation, we’ll help you build an itinerary with the best things to do in Portland.

Explore the Outdoors in Portland

1.Washington Park

New to the city? Washington Park should be number one on your places to visit list. This flagship asset of Portland is a 410-acre park that is home to the Oregon Zoo, Portland’s Child Museum, International Rose Test Garden, Portland Japanese Garden, World Forestry Center, and Hoyt Arboretum. Washington Park enhance tourism in Portland, by attracting over 3.5 million visitors in a year!

Entry Costs:

  • No entry fee
  • $2 fee for parking which goes straight towards the maintenance of the Washington Park.

Timings:

  • 5 am – 10 pm

9. Pittock Mansion

The famous Pittock Mansion is where the elite partied back in the 1900s. This Mansion-turned-museum is a French Renaissance sensation, which was built by Henry Pittock. He worked toward bringing in all the modern amenities like controlled central heating and elevators – amongst many others. Navigate the museum and expect premium and ahead-of-its-time interiors. One of the best things to do in Portland is to imagine yourself living the life of Sir Henry! The laid out cutlery from the ancient times is on show, where you can appreciate the attention to detail at which Henry Pittock commissioned this 16,000 sq. foot bungalow!

Kids (5 and under): Free

6. How Transit Friendly is Portland?

Getting around Portland is a breeze; if you’re moving with a roommate to Portland, you won’t have to worry about solely relying on a car. Walkable bridges, reliable public transport and the cycle-friendly landscape help make Portland an extremely tourist friendly city.

1. Walkability in Portland

With Nike and Adidas’s North American headquarters located in Portland, it’s no wonder that it according to Oregon Live, Portland is the 7th most walkable city in the US. You will have no trouble trusting your feet when you want to explore the city, as many of its resident do. The city is also filled with green pastures, which make hiking a popular hobby for most residents.

2. Roads/traffic in Portland

Unless otherwise specified, cars need to adhere to a speed limit of 55 mph or below when within the city limits. Drivers should be careful about watching out for bikes, as most roads have a shared bike lane. Ensure that you familiarise yourself with specific traffic rules, such as knowing when you can turn left or right, or using mobile phones with hands-free accessories. Generally, traffic may not be a big issue, however, most people prefer to use public transport than cars.

3. Taxis in Portland

As the city is quite compact, if you plan on relying on Taxis, you won’t have to fork over a large fare. Typically, fares start from $3 with each additional mile adding another $3 to the fare. You may also have to pay $1 for each additional passenger, so keep that in mind of you’re planning on travelling with your roommate in Portland. Uber and Lyft are other reliable options.

4. Public Transportation in Portland

Rent in Portland may be expensive, but the transport isn’t. The TriMet provides reliable transportation throughout the city. From bus routes, streetcars to MAX light rail, residents have numerous affordable options to get around the city. Owning a car is not a necessity in the city, with bus routes serving the suburban areas as well. All buses are wheelchair friendly, making them the most accessible and affordable option.

5. Airports in Portland

Just 9 miles from north-east Downtown Portland, residents can access the Portland International Airport via MAX Light Rail’s Red Line, shuttles, bus routes, or via taxi. The PDX is convenient to get to, offers free Wi-Fi, great food and tax-free shopping options. Passengers from New York, Tokyo, Washington D. C. and nearly 57 other cities can enjoy a non-stop flight to Portland.

6. Cycling in Portland

Portland has over 350 miles in biking lanes and is extremely cycle-friendly. Most roads have protected bike lanes and all drivers know to watch out for bikes on the road. With several bike share companies, those who do not own a bike can take part in the fun as well. When living with a roommate in Portland, you can also take part in the Bike Events take place throughout the year, such as the Pedalpalooza Bike Festival or Portland’s World Naked Bike Ride.
