]]> El primer tatuaje siempre produce mucha expectativa sobre cómo lo experimentamos y si la recuperación de nuestra piel al cicatrizar será de lo mejor, es por eso que tener cuidados previos pueden ayudarte a que vivas tu experiencia con mayor tranquilidad.

Comienza a hidratar tu piel

Consigue una crema hidratante adecuada para tu piel y aplícala 2 veces al día, ayudará a la elasticidad y que el proceso de tatuado sea mucho menos agresivo para ella y que definitivamente tu cicatrización sea mejor. Recuerda es muy importante que el día en que vayas a hacerte tu primer tatuaje no debes usar crema y tomar en cuenta las recomendaciones de tu tatuador.

No te expongas de más al sol (¡antes, durante y después de tu primer tatuaje!)

En los días previos o mucho más cercanos a tu tatuaje, evita tener exposición prolongada al sol en la zona que has seleccionado para tatuarte ya que podrías tener quemaduras y tu sesión se debe posponer hasta que tu piel se encuentre en buen estado nuevamente. De la misma forma evita otras maneras de irritar tu piel como depilar tu piel vía láser o cera.

Exfolia para eliminar impurezas y que tu primer tatuaje quede de 10

Una parte importante de tu rutina para cuidar la piel antes de tu primer tatuaje es que tu piel se encuentre limpia, encuentra un exfoliante que sea adecuado para tu tipo de piel o incluso prueba con alguno que puedas hacer fácilmente en casa. No exfolies más de 3 veces a la semana tu carita o podrías irritarla. Checa estas ideas para armar un spa en casa con tu roomie y que queden con piel suavecita como pompi de bebé.

Por último, algunos cuidados finales que debes tener para antes de tu cita para el primer tatuaje como beber alcohol o ingerir drogas. Recuerda estar bien hidratado, tomar agua y comer frutas o verduras te ayudará a mantener tu piel en buen estado. Evita anestesia tópica o tomar analgésicos sin recetas.

Recuerda que estás a punto de vivir una de las grandes experiencias de tu vida y debes estar muy tranquilo y relajado para poder enfrentarlo, así que meditar un poco te puede servir. El primer tatuaje nunca se olvida, haz de ello una experiencia memorable.

]]> Our skin goes through a lot during the year. And as you might know, summers can be a different kind of challenging for our skin. Warm weather brings a whole slew of skincare questions, and if there was one answer to all of them, it’s the following. You have to have a basic skincare routine, and depending on the weather, you should be switching up the products based on what they’re meant to do. There should ideally be a couple of changes that one incorporated, transitioning from winter to summer. Here’s everything you should be doing in your summer skincare routine!

Related: Help! Why Is My Skin Breaking Out Even In Quarantine?

Drop the heavy weight routine.

When you’re not wearing heavy clothes in the summer, then why should your skin? In the summer, be sure to focus on lightweight products made to get the job done. The heat and humidity of summer mean you can swap out a heavier cleanser (think cream or oil cleanser) in favor of a gentle, foaming option.

Make your moisturizer dual purpose!

Combining moisturizer and SPF could be the answer you’re looking for when thinking of lightening up your skincare routine. For most areas and people, an SPF over 30 should be enough. It’s also important to note that heavier products can lead to the clogging of pores, inflammation, and acne. Especially if you have acne-prone or oily skin, losing one skin-care step with a combination moisturizer/SPF can help keep skin clear.

Related: Tips for Keeping Your Skin Healthy During the Winter Months

But NEVER skip on the moisturizing.

The extra layer of sweat that accumulates over our skin during the summer months DOES NOT count as moisturiser. Even if you’re in the most humid temperatures and your skin feels oily, you need your moisturiser.

The main purpose of a moisturizer is to replenish and re-establish the outermost layer of your skin. This then protects the skin from external pollutants that can become the cause irritation and dryness. This is where that combination moisturizer/SPF comes in: A lightweight texture will help keep that outer layer nourished without feeling too heavy on your skin.

Give your skin a solid vitamin C serum treatment.

Vitamin C is important all year round, but it becomes essential in the summers. If you have oily skin, you can pair this up with exfoliation for the pores. This doesn’t mean you go overboard with your favorite acids, but maybe try slowly increasing the number of days you exfoliate per week.

Make sure your makeup is non-comedogenic!

This is a really important one. Take a close look at your makeup product labels and only keep those that are non-comedogenic. That’s a fancy word for a product formulated without pore-clogging ingredients. Especially over the summer, try only using products that are made to suit your skin type!

Stay in the shade ?

Sun protective clothing is important, and we love wide brim hats paired with the funkiest glasses. These don’t just make fun additions to our wardrobes, but can be put to great use in the summers! Be mindful to expose as little of your body as possible to direct sunlight. That might mean sitting underneath an umbrella, wearing an extra-large hat, or avoiding the midday sun.

HYDRATE HYDRATE AND HYDRATE SOME MORE.

Water literally solves half our bodily woes. Truly. Aim for eight glasses of water a day, which can do wonders for your skin. Hydration can also help skin recover after a sunburn — but we know you’re all too smart to get those in the first place.

SPF, please!

Yeah, we kept the holy grail tip for summer skincare for the end. Everyone could do good with some 30+ SPF (Yeah, those SPF 15s don’t really work anymore). It is also important to note that sunscreen needs to be reapplied every couple of hours, so make sure to pack extra sunscreen for long summer days outdoors. You could also set reminders on your phone to remind yourself to apply sunscreen!

]]> Ever since the COVID-19 pandemic started, face masks have become an indispensable part of our lives. Be it going to work or a quick grocery run, masks are now at the top of our checklists when we leave home. Our lives have changed in many unexpected ways, and preventing the spread of COVID-19 with face masks is one of them. Breakouts and skin issues from wearing face masks have become so common that mask acne has even been dubbed ‘maskne’. As the name suggests – acne around the mouth and chin caused by wearing a face mask. Since we’re going to be wearing masks for a while, managing and preventing maskne will be our top priority! So for all you looking for maskne tips & maskne treatment you’re in the right place!

What causes mask acne?

Whether you’re blemish-prone or have more of a sensitive skin type, the problem is twofold: it’s both the humid conditions beneath your mask’s material and the physical contact of the mask itself that can cause problems. These dreaded breakouts tend to develop when pores get clogged up by bacteria, oils, dead skin cells, makeup, and dirt. This can then lead to whiteheads, blackheads, or pimples that vary in size and type.

What are some Maskne tips to prevent a breakout?

1. Step no.1 in maskne treatment is to invest in the right mask!

A cotton mask is a good option as it allows your skin to breathe while protecting others around you. Cotton is a soft, natural, and breathable fabric as opposed to synthetic fabrics such as nylon, polyester, and rayon, which are more likely to cause irritation.

A good fit will not only help to protect you and others from Covid19, but also prevent you from having to constantly readjust your mask. This reduces the spread of bacteria to your face and stops it from rubbing continuously against your skin.

2. Good skincare is a good maskne treatment

A simple skincare routine can go a long way toward keeping your skin clear. Consider mask wearing a good excuse to adopt the most basic skincare routine: a gentle non-soap cleanser and a mild, fragrance-free moisturizer. Stick to a gentle cleanser and light moisturizer, and use products that are fragrance-free, non-comedogenic, and oil-free. If you’re spending any time in the sun, don’t forget to use a sunscreen with at least SPF 30 every day. This is one of the most important maskne tips to prevent a breakout.

Break up with excessive makeup (at least temporarily!). If you put on makeup for a video call, consider taking it off when you go out.

  • Wear a soft, cotton, or homemade mask that fits your face properly.
  • Do not touch or itch your face once you wear a mask.
  • Use a protective cream or a barrier cream like sunscreen or a mild moisturizer before wearing a mask.
  • If you’re wearing a cotton mask, be sure to wash it frequently with warm water and gentle detergent. This will help remove oil and dead skin cells that can collect on the inside of the mask.

3. Avoid reusing surgical masks

So, if you keep your masks clean, follow daily proper skin hygiene, and use the appropriate products, you should be able to control any skin irritations and acne while continuing to protect yourself and others from the virus.

]]> Is your skin breaking out in lockdown? You are not alone. Quarantine should technically make things easier with skin breaking out, right? Well, is lockdown stress to blame?

What indeed is going on? Certainly, you are not using makeup or subjecting your skin to the stress of the harsh sun and pollution. You are also following a whole Instagram-worthy skincare regime, yet those darn angry bumps won’t go away.

Your girlfriends seem to be in the same fix (obviously not the lucky ones blessed with flawless skin… ugh!). Well, if you’re nodding along while reading this, here are a few reasons why skin breaking out in lockdown is still quite possible. Hint: Stress might have something to do with it!

1. Mindless lockdown snacking = skin breaking out!

Did you buy a whole stash of lockdown snacks only to eat them all in 2 days? Definitely, guilty as charged.

Binge-watching and binge eating go hand in hand, don’t they? Be careful with those lockdown munchies! Not only are you devouring significant calories while stress-eating chips and baked goodies, but you may also be clogging your follicles, which could lead to your skin breaking out.

When stressed, try consuming less unrefined sugar and more whole grains, fruits, and vegetables. Truly, you are what you eat (we all want to look like glossy donuts but it doesn’t work that way sadly).

2. Lockdown stress can be cause for frequent skin breaking out

We are in the middle of a global pandemic and have spent a majority of in lockdown. It can be a lot to take in for our mind, body, and soul. The increasing stress may be the last thing that you’re actually (consciously) stressing about. However, unchecked stress for long periods of time can cause a range of complications in one’s body, including the confusing toll on your skin.

Stress causes a chemical response in your body that makes skin more sensitive and reactive. Additionally, It can also make it harder for skin problems to heal.

Talk to your friends and family, meditate or try yoga, go easy on WFH and make time for your hobbies.

3. Maskne (aka skin breaking out due to face mask)

Acne caused by wearing masks (wow, thanks 2020). Your masks can become a breeding playground for bacteria and yeast if they aren’t washed and cleaned regularly. Along with this, factors such as rubbing, occlusion, and the mask’s material can make your situation worse.

Here’s what you can do – Keep your face clean and moisturize, get soft and breathable masks, wash your masks regularly, and avoid harsh chemicals.

4. Healthy practices to keep your #LockdownSkin in check

Follow a routine to avoid skin breaking out.

The concept of day/night has become trivial since lockdown began. Waking up late, skipping your shower, and staying in your jammies is the new normal.

While it is okay to take a break and skip makeup and hair care, you should not halt your skincare routine. Follow your cleanser, toner, moisturizer, and spot treatment routine every day without fail. Talk to a skincare specialist for advice tailored to your skin type.

Get some sun.

Along with this, take some time to get fresh air and sun (enjoy the sun before 10 am and after 4 pm). The vitamin D will not only benefit your skin but will also lighten your dark and depressed soul (it helps, really!).

Stop picking on your zits.

Seriously girl, staaaph!

Clean your phone.

Use a sanitizer to wipe the surface of your phone, and keep it squeaky clean. You can also choose hands-free to take a call instead of slapping it on your cheek whenever it rings.

Try Homemade DIY masks.

Choose ones appropriate for your skin type: what a great way to feel good!

Get enough sleep.

Netflix till 5 am < good skincare.

Drink plenty of water.

Your body will looove you forever!

5. When all else fails, stress not, and speak to a dermatologist!

If your skin is just out of control, then you need to see a dermatologist. Follow all safety measures if you are planning to visit your Derma. Don’t forget to wear your mask, carry your sanitizer, and stop touching your face to keep your skin from breaking out. If you’re anxious about stepping out, consult your doc online – thank god for technology!

No matter what – always remember that you are beautiful! These are trying times, but they won’t last forever. Keep smiling. XO
