How transit friendly is Aurora?

Aurora has a range of highway travel alternatives as well as reliable public transportation to get you where you need to go. The R Line light rail, the RTD bus system, a variety of private ground transportation, and significant ride-sharing services are all located in the city. Find out more about the various modes of transportation available below.

1. Public transportation

The RTD runs 170 local, regional, and SkyRide bus routes that connect to nine rail lines and serve thousands of bus stations. To the Denver International Airport, local one-way prices are $2.60, regional $4.50, and SkyRide $9.00.

Cultural activities in Aurora

Vintage Theatre, a small theater featuring innovative productions and a thriving youth theater program, is located here. The Aurora Fox Arts Center, a 1940s-era movie theater that reopened as a performing arts center in the 1980s (and which now offers theatrical education programs for youngsters), also hosts live theater and other acts.

The 1,100-acre Plains Conservation Center provides a glimpse into life in Colorado during the mid-nineteenth century, while the Aurora History Museum features periodic exhibits on themes such as the city’s craft beer culture and important local families. There’s no shortage of places to visit in Aurora!
