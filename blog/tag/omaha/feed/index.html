Outdoors in Omaha

1. “Bobbing” at Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge

The Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge simply referred to as “Bob,” is a floating bridge over the Mississippi River. This bridge covers 3000 feet of biking and hiking trails. Don’t forget to stand on the state line on Bob and snap a picture! This is known as “Bobbing”, and is undoubtedly one of the best things to do in Omaha.

  • Cost: Free
  • Hours of Operation: Open 24 hours

Things To Do

2. Visit the Ditmars Orchard & Vineyard

Undoubtedly one of the best places to visit in Omaha with your family, you get to pick strawberries and sunflowers during the summer at this vineyard. Also, you can walk through pumpkin patches during the winter. When visiting the Ditmars Orchard & Vineyard, don’t forget to grab a bite of their famous homemade treats.

  • Cost: Entry is free. However, you will need to pay for the produce you pick.
  • Hours of Operation: Thursday to Saturday: 10 AM – 6 PM; Sundays: 11 AM to 5 PM

Unique Eats

3. Explore Fontenelle Forest

Located along the Missouri River, the Fontenelle Forest covers nearly 2000 acres of wetland, prairie, and forest. Walk right in for a magical experience; it’s one of the few places in Omaha where you can catch a glimpse of wild deer! If you love to explore nature, then this is definitely one of the best things to do in Omaha.

  • Cost: Every day: 8 AM to 7 PM
  • Hours of Operation: $11 per adult; $10 per senior (62+); $8 per child (2-17); Free for children under age 2 and members
Website

4. Pick Berries at the Bellevue Berry & Pumpkin Ranch

Explore the countryside of the city at the Bellevue Berry & Pumpkin Ranch. Not only will you get to pick your own berries, but you also get to interact with the farm animals. And you can view farm antiques as well.

  • Cost: Prices vary per season;
  • Hours of Operation: vary per season
Website

Sports in Omaha

1. Catch a NCAA College World Series Game!

For many, the NCAA College World Series games are a bucket list event! Don’t miss out on the best of college baseball games while you’re in Omaha.

  • Cost: Varies per game; average tickets start at $150
  • Hours of Operation: Varies per game

2. Experience Ice hockey with the Omaha Lancers

The Omaha Lancers are a historic team within the United States Hockey League. With over 33 years of experience, they’ve played in almost all 50 states. Don’t miss out on watching them play on their home ice!

  • Cost: Ticket prices start at $15
  • Hours of Operation: Varies per game

Museums in Omaha

1. Visit the Durham Museum

Located at the Art Deco Union Station, the Durham Museum helps you catch a glimpse of the history of the region. Explore restored cars and catch a glimpse of storefronts from the 1940s. Or, stop by an authentic soda fountain for a malt or phosphate.

  • Cost: $11 for adults; $8 for seniors; $7 for children aged 3 to 12; free for children below 2 and members
  • Hours of Operation: Sunday: Noon – 4 PM; Monday: 10AM – 4PM; Tuesday: 10AM – 8PM; Wednesday to Saturday: 10AM – 4PM: closed during major holidays.
Website

2. Explore the Bemis Center for Contemporary Arts

Supporting contemporary artists, the Bemis Center for Contemporary Arts was founded in 1981 by artists, for artists. It has an internationally renowned artists in residence program, which helps support artists through commissions, temporary exhibitions, and innovative public programs.

  • Costs: Entry is free; you can make voluntary donations
  • Hours of Operation: Sunday to Tuesday: Closed; Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday: 11 AM to 5 PM; Thursday: 11 AM to 9 PM
Website
Events

3. Take a Trip to the Joslyn Art Museum

One of the best places to visit in Omaha with your family, the Joslyn Art Museum has something for everyone. Explore the outdoor exhibitions, which let you view over 20 artworks while walking along a garden. Or, catch the guided tours, classes, lectures, and more.

  • Cost: Entry is free
  • Hours of Operation: Wednesday to Sunday, 10 AM to 4 PM; hours are extended till 8 PM for the first weekend of every month.
Website

Culture in Omaha

1. Greet the Animals at Omaha’s Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium

Omaha’s Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium isn’t a zoo. Rather, it is a biological reserve dedicated to the conservation of animals across the globe. Here, you will find some of best things to do in Omaha, like exploring North America’s largest indoor rainforest. Also, you can explore the world’s largest indoor desert.

  • Cost: Ticket prices vary per season; free for children aged 2 and below.
  • Hours of Operation: April 1st to October 31st: 9 AM to 5 PM daily; November 1st to March 31st: 10 AM to 4 PM
Tickets

2. Get Adventurous with Flying Timber Axe Throwing!

It’s simple: grab an ax, throw it at the target and wait impatiently for your next turn. When you’re thinking of places to visit in Omaha with your friends, this is the number one spot!

  • Cost: $20 per person for one hour; $35 per person for two hours
  • Hours of Operation: Wednesday to Thursday: 5 PM to 9 PM; Friday: 5 PM to 10:30 PM; Saturday: 12 PM to 10:30 PM
Website

3. Get Closer to Nature at the Glacier Creek Preserve

Hike various trails, or simply enjoy the view at the Glacier Creek Preserve. This park is part of the extended University of Nebraska-Omaha campus.

  • Cost: Entry is free
  • Hours of Operation: Open only for daytime hiking

How Transit Friendly is Omaha?

While Omaha is a walkable city, public transit could use a little more help. It isn’t necessary to own a car to live in the city, but you would probably have an easier time if you had one. If you’re moving to the city for work, try to find rooms for rent in Omaha that are closer to your workplace.

1. Walkability in Omaha

Omaha is the 23rd most walkable large city in the US. Some of the most walkable neighborhoods are Park East, Market West and Old Market. if you like to get some fresh air by walking around the block, to look for rooms for rent in these Omaha neighborhoods.

2. Roads/traffic in Omaha

Planning to get to your appointments just in the nick of time? Omaha traffic is not your friend. While Omaha traffic has never been great, it seems to be getting worse every year. When you have to make it to events, make sure your account for traffic even during non-peak hours.

3. Taxis in Omaha

You won’t have any shortage of taxi services in Omaha. Omaha taxis charge a base fare of $3.50 with an additional $3.55 for every kilometre, and a waiting fare of $35 per hour. Most hotels in the city also offer complimentary rides to and from the airport. If you’d rather not wait to hail a taxi, you can opt for online options like Uber and Lyft. Environmentally conscious residents can also opt for ride share programs that service the city.

4. Public transportation in Omaha

Apart from rental cars, limos and shuttles, residents can also opt for the extensive bus routes throughout the city. Operated by the Omaha Metro, the bus service is Omaha’s primary mass transit system. Hence, most people opt for rental services if they are visiting the city for a few days.

5. Airports in Omaha

Eppley Airport provides non-stop service to 26 destinations and handles over 200 flights per day. Though it is a mid-sized airport, in 2016, it ranked first in customer satisfaction. Located just a 10-minute drive from Downtown Omaha, you can drive into the heart of the city in no time.

6. Cycling in Omaha

The League of American Bicyclists renewed Omaha’s status as Bronze Level Bicycle Friendly Community in 2019 for another four years. The city has continually invested in bike-friendly infrastructure to make roads safer for all. A 38-mile unifying bike trail along with Omaha’s growing bike share services has ensured that Omaha continues to improve its Bike Friendly Community status.

Historical/Cultural Spots in Omaha

The hometown of Civil Rights leader Malcolm X and US President Gerald Ford, Omaha has no shortage of cultural and historical spots. From the 20th century passenger trains that you can explore in The Durham Museum, to the Mormon Trail Centre that commemorates the struggles of the Mormon Migration and their triumph in 1846. Visit the home of General George Crook, a prominent military officer during the American Civil War, or explore the famed Boys Town founded by Father Flanagan in 1917.
