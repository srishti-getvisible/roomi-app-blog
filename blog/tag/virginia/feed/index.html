Getting to know Norfolk-Hampton Roads

Norfolk was incorporated in 1705 and saw a tobacco inspection site set up here in 1730. Thanks to its great connectivity by water and rail, it developed into the most prosperous city in Virginia by 1775.

Known today as a port city, it has a major Navy and shipping presence. It was recognized as an ‘All-America City’ in 2013 thanks to its status as a hub of the Hampton Road area. Norfolk has a subtropical climate with warm and humid summers, and mild winters. How transit friendly is Norfolk-Hampton Roads?

Downtown Norfolk offers great walkability and is best explored on foot. Many other areas of the city are also easy to travel on your own two feet. However, for longer commutes, you will need to look toward public transport and taxis. If you are looking for rooms for rent in Norfolk-Hampton Roads, jump on the many public transport options available and mingle with the locals!

1. Airports

Norfolk is primarily served by the Norfolk International Airport, the major airport in the region. Another airport that serves the city is Newport News/Williamsburg International Airport, which is usually a cheaper option that’s located 20 minutes North of the City. The Chesapeake Regional Airport, a smaller airport on the city outskirts provides aviation services too.

2. Buses

Buses in Norfolk and surrounding areas are operated by the Hampton Roads Transit (HRT). They operate a series of buses, shuttles, trollies, electric buses, and paratransit services. Check for bus stops if you are looking for rooms for rent in Norfolk-Hampton Roads. The bus routes and fares for Norfolk and other areas can be found on their website.

3. Taxis

Taxis in Norfolk are generally safe and cheaper as compared to other cities. However, you might run into difficulties finding one in some places. Hence it makes sense to book one in advance. Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are also available, which can be handy if you are looking for rooms for rent in Norfolk-Hampton Roads in an out-of-the-way area.

4. Ferries

The ferries in Norfolk are also operated by the Hampton Roads Transit (HRT) and are a great alternative way to travel. The routes and timings for the ferry can be found on the HRT website as well. The timings are divided into summer and winter timings and are subject to change. There is also a special route between North Landing and Harbor Park when the Norfolk Tides play a home game there. Other than this, there are boat taxis that will take you around the Norfolk and Portsmouth waterfronts.
