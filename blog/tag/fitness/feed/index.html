]]> In our fast-paced, consumerist world of processed food and delivery apps, it can be hard to pay attention to our minds and bodies. For many of us, spending quarantine inside our homes cut off access to gym facilities, personal trainers, yoga classes, and much more. When daily life confines you to the four walls of your home, how do you get a total body workout that nourishes you? With the help of fitness apps, a little self-discipline, and Sunny health and fitness at home equipment, a little can go a long way.

Despite what the Instagram fitness industry may try to sell you, fitness does not have a certain look or type. A healthy body helps you accomplish your daily tasks with ease.

In fact, health-related fitness comprises your cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, and body composition. A total body workout is the best way to maintain fitness across these areas.

What are the 5 components of health-related fitness you can achieve through a total body workout?

According to Verywellfit and CDC, regular physical activity boosts health-related fitness by reducing your risk of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, cancer, along with mental health benefits. Exercises can be done at home with equipment from Sunny health and fitness or using guided fitness apps.

The five benefits of health-related fitness exercises include:

  • Increased cardiovascular endurance (no panting when you climb a few flights of stairs)
  • Muscular endurance (6 shopping bags in one go, beast mode on!)
  • Higher flexibility (fewer aches and pains at the end of the day)
  • Muscular strength (easily lift your dog)
  • Better body composition (ideal body fat to muscle ratio that suits your body type)

Why choose a total body workout when you can just train the part your need to look its best?

According to Sunny health and fitness experts, a total body workout can help you achieve overall fitness, including in your target areas. We get it, chiseled abs and a sculpted butt are something we all desire. However, overtraining or undertraining certain areas can increase your risk of injury. Total body workouts, whether guided by a personal trainer or times fitness apps are easier to handle no matter your prior experience.

easy total body workouts at home (as visualized through Sunny health and fitness equipment)

1. Kettlebell magic at home

Kettlebells are one of the best options for a total body workout at home as they are easy to store and can be used to train various parts of your body

To get started with kettlebells, start with smaller and lighter weights and eventually increase both as you get comfortable. Some beginner total body workout exercises with kettlebells include wide squats, kettlebell swings, or reverse lunges.

Try these colorful Kettlebell options from Sunny Health and Fitness here.

2. Try an in-home rower

When space is not an issue, a rowing machine can give you simultaneous upper and lower body exercise. It also provides you with low-impact cardio which is a win-win situation.

Rowing helps increase muscle endurance and cardiovascular fitness. You can find a rhythm, increase resistance as you go to get both cardio and strength benefits.

Beginner option from Sunny health and fitness: SF-RW5515 Magnetic Rowing Machine

For competitive rowers: Asuna 8580 Ventus Air Magnetic Rowing Machine

3. Elliptical for the win

Ellipticals are fairly easy to have and use at home, especially when you cannot go out due to pandemic lockdown restrictions. Perhaps you simply find it more convenient to work out at home with your work-from-home schedule.

An elliptical activates muscles in your lower body while also stimulating your chest and arms. It is therefore very handy for a total body workout.

Working out on an elliptical also puts less impact on your joints and knees, making it easy to use no matter your body weight or size.

4. Suspension bodyweight trainer (TRX)

Invented by a US Navy seal (that’s how you know it’s real), the TRX is short for total-body resistance exercise.

This equipment is another great option for limited space and storage at home. All you need is your body weight and gravity to attain a total body workout.

To use them, all you have to do is secure the straps to a sturdy spot and the rest of the exercises involve your body suspending in the air partly, leaning back- essentially providing resistance for every kind of workout.

5. HIIT total body workout (only gym mat needed!)

For a quick total body workout when all you need and maybe can afford (it’s alright, we understand) is a gym mat, HIIT is a useful solution. HIIT stands for high-intensity interval training.

Try this HIIT workout from Sunny health and fitness that targets building strong lean muscle in your upper body, lower body, and core while burning calories.

You can also use fitness apps like Interval timer (Download Android, iOS) or Freeletics (Download: Android, iOS) to complete a HIIT total body workout at home.

]]> Confirmado: es bueno tomar cerveza después de hacer ejercicio

Amantes de la cerveza, ¡llegó nuestro momento! 

Si te gusta tanto la chela como a nosotros, te va a encantar esto que tenemos que platicarte:

Un catedrático de la Escuela de Medicina de la Universidad de Granada en España se puso más creativo de lo normal y probó que el consumo moderado de cerveza clara después de hacer ejercicio en ambientes muy cálidos y que implican sudar mucho, permite hidratarse casi como si se bebiera agua. Además de que para muchas personas es más placentero y puede ser un buen estímulo para hacer ejercicio. 

“Su alto contenido en agua, el equilibrio de sus componentes (hidratos de carbono, bajo contenido en sodio y ausencia de grasas), su bajo aporte calórico (una cerveza de 200 mililitros contiene 90 kcal y si se trata de cerveza sin alcohol, 45 kcal) y su baja graduación alcohólica (4º-5º), hacen de la cerveza una bebida interesante para el mantenimiento de los márgenes cardiosaludables del deportista”, afirmó el profesor tras llevar a cabo su investigación.

Este es un estudio que se realizó ya hace algunos años, pero siempre es buen momento para retomarlo y correr la voz.

Para hacer la investigación pusieron a correr a varias personas hasta agotarse físicamente, sin beber nada mientras hacían la actividad física. Aquéllos que se rehidrataron con cerveza saciaron su sed más rápido y sintieron que se recuperaron antes que los que tomaron agua. 

Además de esto, el máximo beneficio al hacer ejercicio se obtiene cuando nos resulta placentero, ¿y qué es más placentero que una chela helada después de sudar?

Por supuesto que esto no significa que siempre debes echarte una cerveza después de hacer ejercicio o que corras a terminarte todo el barril de tu bar más cercano. Pero es bueno saber que de vez en cuando te puedes dar una chelita reconfortante post workout. 

Beneficios de tomar cerveza: ¡por esto amamos la chela!

La verdad es que no necesitamos de muchas razones para amarla y ahora menos sabiendo que es bueno tomar cerveza después de hacer ejercicio. 

Aun así, para que tu amor por ella crezca aún más, acá te dejamos algunos beneficios de tomar cerveza que nuestros amigos de National Geographic en español nos comparten:

  • Disminuye el riesgo de diabetes
  • Ayuda a la digestión
  • Evita el envejecimiento porque tiene antioxidantes naturales
  • Fortalece los huesos
  • Las cervezas obscuras y negras pueden reducir los ataques al corazón

Por supuesto, estos beneficios de tomar cerveza se dan en consumos moderados. Tampoco creas que vivir en una eterna borrachera de chela te los dará. 

Pero igual, lo que te hemos platicado en este artículo lo puedes tomar como pretexto para ir corriendo por un six y brindar con tus roomies por lo buena que es la chela con la humanidad.

]]> Maintaining a workout regimen is already a challenge in your busy life and now you’ve taken on the delight that is summertime moving. That’s like 2 week of packing and then moving, settling & unpacking. With all of this you will most likely miss out a month’s workout. NOT! Especially when you have us to help you with fitness hacks & home exercises to stay fit come what may.

Fitness Hacks That Will Make Packing More Fun & Help you Stay Fit!

1. Make box hunting a part of your jog or run

Everyone needs boxes for their move, and no one wants to pay for them if they can at all avoid it. While in the packing phase, movers are always on the prowl for boxes from retail places who need to get rid of them. Go for a nice cardio boosting run or jog, and weave your path through retail heavy areas that aren’t so far from home so if you are successful in finding boxes, you don’t have to carry them for a too far. There a fitness hack you thought you didn’t need but actually did. You’re welcome!

Make a fun playlist

Whether it’s podcasts or music, nearly everyone at the gym is listening to something as they work for those often elusive gains. Working out while packing should be no different. Make a cool Pandora station based on motivational songs like Survivor’s “Eye of the Tiger”, or use any of these one hundred song playlists on Spotify found from Fitness Magazine. Your “moving mix” will help keep the blood flowing and apply all the home exercise hacks we have for you here!

Use proper workout form when packing

No one ever got anywhere by skipping leg day, this is known around the world, but if you’re trying to work out while moving, how can you make sure everything stays firm and tight? When packing the kitchen where you have empty cabinets of pots, pans, and canned goods, use a proper squatting technique. Do one item at a time, leaving the box on the floor, keeping your back straight. Now you’re applying one of the best fitness hacks out there and successfully doing squats while packing.
Roomi’s Fitness Hack: Increase the weight with more items if you’re looking to build or many lighter reps to just help you maintain.

Home Exercise Hacks? Reimagine Chores!

Do reps every time you fill a box or bag! Topping off your moving box and taping it up means it’s time for a HIIT (high-intensity interval training). Consider the weight of the box before you select your workout- safety first and make sure the items packed aren’t fragile. Work your arms and shoulders with an upright row by lifting the box and winging out your elbows. Overhead tricep extensions can be done by lifting the box behind your head, arms at a 90-degree angle, elbows at your ears, and slowly lift the box straight up.
Roomi’s Fitness Hack: Bags with handles make for an easy dumbbell curl. If you typical work muscle groups to failure, try not to, you’re still on a mission to finish packing.

With these tricks, fitting a workout in while packing for your move is entirely possible. Look for opportunities every time you have to lift or bend and you’ll be well on your way to staying fit while meeting your move out deadline.
