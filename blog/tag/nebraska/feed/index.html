Getting to know Lincoln

Originally a prairie covered with buffalo grass, Lincoln was first inhabited by the Pawnee tribe. It was founded in 1856 as the village of Lancaster and became the county seat of Lancaster County. Renamed after President Abraham Lincoln, it is also the seat of government for the state of Nebraska. Lincoln experiences a humid continental climate as it is far from any major water bodies. It was also designated as a refugee-friendly city due to its stable economy, educational institutions, and industries.

Cultural activities in Lincoln

University of Nebraska State Museum:

This is the state’s premier museum of natural history and a University-based research museum as well. The museum aims to discover and disseminate knowledge about the natural world. This is a must-visit for those with an interest in natural history when looking for rooms for rent in Lincoln.

Nebraska State Capitol:

This building is home to the state legislature of Nebraska and an example of Art Deco architecture. The interior art provides a representation of the history of Nebraska, its government, and its people. There is also an observation deck on the 14th floor that provides views of the entire city.

Sunken Gardens:

Sunken Gardens was a Depression-era project, built in 1930. Originally known as the ‘Rock Garden’, it is home to over 416 trees and shrubs. This is a great place to visit for a weekend nature getaway if you are looking for rooms for rent in Lincoln.
