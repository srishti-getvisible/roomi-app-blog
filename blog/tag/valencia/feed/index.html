Getting to Know Valencia

Valencia was founded as a Roman colony by Decimus Junius Brutus Callaicus in 138 BC and named Valentia Edetanorum. Later occupied by Moroccan and Arab Moors, it was the capital of the Taifa of Valencia and an agricultural hub. The city also served as the capital of Spain when Joseph Bonaparte moved the Court there in 1812, and again between 1936 and 1937, during the Second Spanish Republic.

Valencia has a subtropical Mediterranean climate with mild winters and hot, dry summers. Thus, if you are moving to Valencia, you can expect some of the best weather in the world all year round!

How transit friendly is Valencia

1. Taxis

Taxis in Valencia are generally safe, and cheaper than in other major cities. However, you may run into difficulties finding one in certain places, especially late at night. Ride-sharing apps like Cabify are usually a cheaper and better option to get around. Also, it can be handy if you are looking for rooms for rent in Valencia that are situated away from the city center.

2. Trains and Buses

Trains in Valencia are operated by Ferrocarrils de la Generalitat Valenciana (FGV). FGV runs the Metrovalencia which is an urban rail system including light railway, metro, and several tram operations. Thus, the Metrovalencia is one of the best ways to get around if you are exploring rooms for rent in Valencia. Buses in the city are also operated by the FGV.

3. Airports

The city is served by Valencia Airport, located 9 km west of the city center. This international airport has regular services to airports across the country and Europe. Alicante Airport is the next nearest major airport but it is considerably further away. Additionally, if you are flying in to find rooms for rent in Valencia, be sure to book in advance and avoid the hordes of tourists.

4. Cycling

Valencia has a bicycle sharing system named Valenbisi to cover short daily routes within the city. Moreover, the fleet currently has over 2750 bicycles and 275 stations to lend and return them. However, to use them, you’ll need to set up a yearly membership.

Cultural Activities in Valencia

  • City of Arts and Science: Located on the southern end of the Jardines del Turia, this place is famous for its architecture by Santiago Calatrava. Named as one of the 12 Treasures of Spain, this cultural and architectural complex is one of the most important places to visit in the city. It’s certainly a must-visit if you are looking for rooms for rent in Valencia!
  • Valencia Cathedral: This Church is officially known as the Metropolitan Cathedral–Basilica of the Assumption of Our Lady of Valencia. Furthermore, it was consecrated in 1238 by the first bishop of Valencia, holding an important place in history. With a mix of Renaissance, Baroque and Neo-Classical elements, the church is one of the best places to visit in the city.
  • Mercat Central: Over 5 times larger than Mercat Boqueria in Barcelona, this market opens at 7:30 am and closes at 3 pm. If you are on the lookout for cheap rooms for rent in Valencia and want to shop like a local, this is the place to go. Here, you’ll also find fresh produce, meat, fish, olives, and souvenirs in this huge market area.

Dining in Valencia

  • Bar Ricardo: This bar is a classic Valencian establishment and has hardly changed since it opened in 1947. In fact, the tellinas (wedge clams) and ensaladilla rusa (Russian salad) here are local favorites. If you’re exploring rooms for rent in Valencia, be sure to stop by here for some great local snacks or a refreshing drink.
  • La Pascuala: La Pascuala is a local bodega that has everything from snacks to filling and delicious local meals. Also, be sure to try the sepia (cuttlefish), calamares (fried squid) or bocadillo (stuffed baguette).
  • Casa Montaña: This tavern is a favorite rendezvous for politicians, intellectuals, and artists in Valencia. Additionally, with a timeless and charming feeling to go with the great food, this eatery is one of the best in the city. Hence, if you are looking for a room for rent in Valencia, be sure to give this place a visit.
