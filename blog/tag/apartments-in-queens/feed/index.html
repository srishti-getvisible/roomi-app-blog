Getting to know Queens

The first settlers in the area were Dutch in 1635 who established the colony of New Netherland. However, in 1664 the area was captured by the English who renamed and took over the area. Queens was incorporated as a borough of New York City in 1898 by a vote of the State Legislature. Queens and the rest of New York City have a humid subtropical climate with partial shielding from the Appalachian Mountains. The area also benefits from the moderating influences of the Atlantic Ocean. Also home to the John F. Kennedy International Airport and LaGuardia Airport, it is a major hub. With this connectivity, getting there by air is never a problem if you plan to find studio apartments for rent in Queens and live there.

How transit friendly is Queens?

Queens can be easy to navigate by walking or cycling. With rush hour traffic, it can often be faster to just bike or walk if your commute is short and allows it. 3. Taxis

Taxis are easily available in Queens and can be useful if you are looking for apartments for rent in Queens in areas that are a bit out of the way. Ridesharing apps like Uber and Lyft are also great options to get around.

Regarding taxis, here are some things to keep in mind.

  • Minimum metered fare is $2.50, which increases 50 cents every fifth of a mile or every minute, depending on how fast you’re traveling.
  • All taxis accept cash and credit, debit and prepaid cards.
  • Tip 15–20 percent at the end of a trip; bridge and tunnel tolls are extra and added to the metered fare.

4. Ferries and water taxis in Queens
