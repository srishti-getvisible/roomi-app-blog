Is Milan safe?

It’s important to always be vigilant when you’re traveling and staying in another city. In Milan, the overall risk is low, but due to the city’s size it’s best to keep your wits about you at all times.

In case you encounter any emergencies, these hotlines will come in handy. 112 can be dialed to report any issues and the operator will direct you to the service that you need, but knowing the direct number to call will ensure help arrives faster, especially where time is critical.

The 911 equivalent in Italy is 113. This number connects you to the state police who can assist you in an emergency. In the case of fires, missing persons, rescues, and first-response operations, dial 115 to reach the fire service (firefighters). For any medical emergency, dial 118 for an ambulance.

Take note of the word, Aiuta/o (a·iù·ta/a·iù·to) which means “help”.

Public Transit in Milan

1. Walkability

Milan is a walkable city with a mix of both historic and modern architecture. The famous landmarks and tourist spots can easily be reached by walking. And the city’s efficient metro and tram can easily help you to your destination for when your feet get tired.

2. Roads & Traffic

The roads in Milan are some of the most congested in the country, which is why driving in the city’s center is not recommended. The good news is that with the city’s great metro and tram system, you don’t need a car to get around and find cheap rooms for rent in Milan.

3. Trains

Milan has a great metro system and a tram network, making it super easy for you to find rooms for rent in Milan. The Milan Metro has four lines and 111 stations and is the longest metro line in Italy.

  • M1 (red) stops at the Duomo and San Babila
  • M2 (green) stops at Milano Centrale
  • M3 (yellow) stops at the Duomo and Milano Centrale (the perfect line for tourists)
  • M5 (purple) is combined with regional trains

4. Buses

Milan has around 80 bus local bus lines and a few trolleybus lines (electric buses with overhead wires), making this is a really easy and convenient way to go around the city. Fifteen night travel routes are also available from midnight until 6am, and they depart every 30 minutes — meaning you can get home from your big night out easily and safely.

Museums in Milan

7. Museo Nazionale Scienza e Tecnologia Leonardo da Vinci

Named after polymath Leonardo Da Vinci, this is Italy’s largest museum dedicated to science and technology. Housed in a beautiful 16th-century monastery, the museum charts global advances in science and technology with over 13 interactive workshops and displays. This makes for one of the best things to do in Milan for all ages.

  • Costs: 10€
  • Timings: Tuesday – Friday: 10 am – 6 pm; Saturday – Sunday: 10 am – 7 pm
Visit website

8. Villa Necchi Campiglio

If you’re interested in architecture or interior design, one of the best places to visit in Milan for you is Villa Necchi Campiglio. It is an interwar masterpiece that was designed in the early 1930s by architect Piero Portaluppi for his sisters. And now it’s open to everyone to view the beauty inside and out with its own greenhouse-style garden cafeteria.

  • Costs: (Children) €4 – (Adults) €14
  • Timings: Wednesday – Sunday: 10 am – 6 pm
Website

9. Armani/Silos

This place hardly needs an introduction, so if you’re a fashion enthusiast looking for the best places to visit in Milan, this might be the very first thing you’ll want to do. Grouped thematically over four floors, the permanent exhibition at Silos highlights the points of inspiration that have influenced Armani’s creative approach throughout his career.

  • Costs: 6€ – 12€
  • Timings: Wednesday – Sunday: 11 am – 7 pm; Thursday – Saturday: 11 am – 9 pm
Website

Culture in Milan

10. Fondazione Prada

Who doesn’t love immersing themselves in art? If you’re hoping to be inspired, going to the Fondazione Prada is one of the best things to do in Milan. Designed by Dutch architect, Rem Koolhaas, the place has 10 buildings with one tower covered in shimmering gold leaf! Alongside all of this art is a cafe designed by film director Wes Anderson.

  • Costs: 10 am – 7 pm
  • Timings: 15€
Website

11. Visit Sforza Castle

A revered cultural center since the unification of Italy in the 19th century, this fortress is a vast complex that includes numerous museums and a great collection of art, including Michelangelo’s last sculpture!

  • Costs: 5€
  • Timings: 7 am – 7:30 pm
Website

12. La Scala Opera House

An architectural masterpiece commissioned by Empress Maria Theresa of Austria in the 1770s, this place was renovated after being bombed during WWII. And of course, they host regular performances by world-class musicians, singers, dancers and actors, making it one of the best places to visit in Milan, culturally.

Just like Milan, Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II is one of the most stylish shopping malls. Often referred to as il salotto di Milano, or Milan’s living room, due to its regular use by residents as a meeting spot, going here is one of the best things to do in Milan if you want to live like a local!

Located in the Navigli district, Mag Café is one of the best places to visit in Milan. Quirky collectibles and memorabilia line the cafe, and the outdoor seating is a perfect spot to catch the skies over the canal.

Timings: 7:30 am – 1:30 am

With two Michelin stars, this family restaurant is one of the best restaurants in Milan. The tasting menu allows you to sample the cuisine that includes subtle Italian flavored dishes. So for a literal taste of Milan, head straight here!
