How transit friendly is Miami?

According to the 2016 American Community Survey, 72.3% of working city of Miami residents commuted by driving alone, 8.7% carpooled, 9% used public transportation, and 3.7% walked. So here’s all you need to know about intercity transit when looking for rooms for rent in Miami, FL.

1. Walkability & Cycling

Although Miami is a dangerous city for pedestrians, owing to the number of cars in the city, bicycling is a fairly considerable option for transit. In 2010, Miami was ranked the 44th-most bike-friendly city in the US according to the Bicycling magazine.

2. Traffic

The fact is, Miami traffic is the worst! The Dutch navigation and mapping company TomTom, in its annual report compiled from 14 trillion GPS reports, declared Miami the seventh-most congested city in the United States. So stay prepared with Waze when you hit that road.

Cultural activities in Miami

  • Take an art walk in Wynwood arts district: Taking place every second Saturday of the month, you can head over to Wynwood for gallery viewings with free drinks and music! Take in the street art and refreshing cocktails while being entertained by brilliant performers on every street corner.
  • Participate in the Viernes Culturales in little Havana: Viernes Culturales or Cultural Fridays is an event that celebrates the best of Cuban culture. Think live music, dancing, Cuban art viewings, and of course people rolling and smoking Cuban cigars!
  • Take a fascinating tour of the erotic art museum: Truly one of a kind, and the only museum devoted to fine erotic art in the U.S., this is a must see when in Miami. If not for the art, go for the spirit of ambition that resided in a Jewish grandmother (Naomi Wilzig) who traveled the world collecting erotic art pieces, amassing one of the largest collections in the world!

Historical spots in Miami

  • The Freedom Tower: This historical spot in Downtown Miami played a major role in the Cuban refugee crisis. In the past, the Miami News used the building as its headquarters too! The freedom tower is now used as a museum for contemporary art.
  • The Barnacle Historic State Park: Go here to visit the oldest house in Miami, aka, the Barnacle. Home to an American yacht designer (founder of Biscayne Bay Yacht Club), it allows you to enjoy the scenic Biscayne bay while you’re there!
  • The Kampong: Garden lovers, here’s your little paradise! Started by horticulturist Dr. David Fairchild with his collection of tropical species from all over the world, it is now a full blown botanical garden. You can also view the historical photographs of the variety of flora you can find here. It now features a laboratory, a herbarium and an education center as well! And if you’re wondering, ‘Kampong’ means village in Malaysian.
