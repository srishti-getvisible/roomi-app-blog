]]> If you’re a fan of the show, How I Met Your Mother, then you’re familiar with the gang’s primary hangout spot: MacLaren’s Pub. This bar was even based on a real life bar in NYC known as McGee’s. So, if you liked seeing Barney Stinson and friends having quality time at their ‘pub’, then here are some pubs in NYC that may be a good fit for you.

Pubs in NYC: The Bronx

1. McDermott’s Pub

If you’re living in NYC, you’ve most likely heard of McDermott’s Pub in the Bronx. This is one of the bars in NYC that is certainly not lacking in customers. It has proven to be a great place to watch sports, play bar games, eat food that will tingle your taste buds, and enjoy beer and cocktails. This pub is a perfect meeting spot for groups, just like the one Barney Stinson and the gang enjoyed in How I Met Your Mother.

Timings: 11 a.m – 4 a.m

Make A Reservation

2. Rambling House

Known as one of New York’s premier restaurants, this Irish pub is almost always buzzing! They offer music throughout the week, from pop to rock. And we can’t forget about the menu! Find the best Irish meals you can think of, served on hot platters for your dining pleasure. Their new greenhouse —Rambling GreenHouse— also has beautiful decor that makes it one of the best New York pubs.

Timings: 12 p.m – 10 p.m

Website

3. Try the Bronx Alehouse

For lovers of free WiFi, this place is for you. Enjoy a variety of pub staples in this craft beer bar that offers not only kerbside pickup and outdoor seating but also free WiFi to accompany your visit. This pub organizes great events throughout the year, and you can even host private events here. In the Bronx Alehouse, you can be sure of great food with fast service that makes this one of the best bars in NYC.

Timings: Sunday to Thursday: 11 a.m – 12 a.m.
Friday and Saturday: 11 a.m – 2 a.m

Website

Related: Fun things to do in Manhattan in a single weekend

Pubs in NYC: Manhattan

1. Have the full HIMYM experience at McGee’s Pub

This Irish Restaurant & Pub is located in 240 West 55th Street in Manhattan and is the inspiration for the MacLaren’s Pub in the popular comedy series, How I Met Your Mother. This is one of the best New York pubs for you to try, especially if you’re a fan of the show. This bar has 22 HD TVs, an internet jukebox, an impeccable sound system, and a Symphony Room where various events take place. Their specials include How I Met Your Mother-inspired cocktails, and they work with On Location Tours, where they give a special 15% discount for the show’s customers. If you’re a fan and you’re living in NYC, then you have to take advantage of this.

Timings: Sunday to Thursday: 11 a.m – 11 p.m.
Friday and Saturday: 11 a.m – 12 a.m

Website

2. Connolly’s Pub & Restaurant

Enjoy live music with your meals at one of the best bars in NYC, with two locations: Connolly’s Time Square and Connolly’s 47th Street. The lineup of live music and meals which, according to their official website, is an “elusive, cuisine-defining balance of sweet, salty, and sour” makes this a must-try pub in NYC.

Timings: 10 a.m – 2 a.m

Website

3. Molly’s Pub: The most traditional bar in NYC

Just like McGee’s, Molly’s Pub and Restaurant Shebeen is an Irish pub, so How I Met Your Mother fans may enjoy this place too. If you’re living in NYC or you’re a tourist, it won’t hurt to visit this traditional spot. It also operates as a bar, and is known for its shepherd’s pie, hamburgers and steaks.

Timings: Monday to Friday: 4 a.m – 10 p.m.
Saturday and Sunday: 12 p.m – 11 p.m

Website

4. Carragher’s Pub and Restaurant

This sports bar/restaurant is perfect for sports fans living in NYC, screening matches of Liverpool F.C,  It serves burgers, sandwiches, and drinks, and has a shop where you can purchase clothing items including the Retro NYC Kopites Shirt. Real soccer fans can even book their birthday and other events here!

Timings:
Monday: 11 a.m -12 a.m
Tuesday to Friday: 11 a.m – 4 a.m
Saturday and Sunday: 10 a.m – 4 a.m

Website

Pubs in NYC: Staten Island

1. Staten Island’s Joyce’s Tavern

Dubbed by its official website as ‘Staten Island’s Popular Meetings Place’, Joyce’s Tavern is a local favorite. You’ll find many events here such as live music bands, Reverse Happy Hour, Country Night Thursdays, Spring and Summer BOGO, and much more. Just recently, they organized a ‘Free Britney’ Party in support of Britney Spears. With a recently opened back patio deck, you can enjoy your meal below the NYC sky.

Website

2. Flanagan’s Tavern

Every day is St. Paddy’s Day in Flanagan’s Tavern. This is a fully-fledged Irish-themed bar, which How I Met Your Mother fans may love. This is also great for sports lovers, as they screen games every Sunday while serving great food and drinks. Live music entertainment is also on offer, so what are you waiting for?

Website

3. Unwind in Steiny’s Pub

Not many bars in NYC are located close to the Staten Island Ferry, which is why this is one of the coolest New York pubs. They offer great service and beer, and even have a jukebox! Events like Martini Madness, Happy Hour, and Karaoke night will be sure to spice up your night.

Timings: Monday to Saturday: 11 a.m – 3 a.m.
Sunday: 12 p.m – 3 a.m

Website

]]> When in NYC, you’ve got to dress like a New Yorker! If you’re planning a trip to visit New York over the Holiday Season, gurl – bundle up! New York’s infamous winter chill can literally quote, unquote, freeze you if you’re not packing mindfully. Especially for someone who’s coming from a place where it doesn’t snow. Personally, I feel winter is like the best time to visit NYC! Why? Because NYC Christmas is like no other, and everyone should witness it at least once in their lifetime. However, if you’re not prepared for the cold, you will be unable to experience its true beauty. So without further ado, here’s a survival guide with NYC winter essentials to prep you for the sweater weather.

Open your suitcase and add the following NYC Winter essentials!

1. Warmers

Layering is key! Especially if you want to survive the NYC winter! However, layering can be quite difficult if your sweaters are all thick & heavily knit. I mean the last thing you want to do is end up looking like a fine lookin’ pumpkin on your 2021 vacation. Unless that’s the look you’re going for. So do this instead – invest in good quality warmers. Not only do they keep you super snug, but you can also slide them under your favorite outfits with ease.

2. Winter Jacket

Now that you have your warmer, the next item on our list of NYC winter essentials is a warm winter jacket. Invest in a coat or a parka with a hoodie that comes till your mid-thigh(at least). You can get these coats online from Macy’s, Everlane, Nordstrom, Uniqlo, and Amazon. Our personal favorite coats are from Orolay, Canada Goose, Uniqlo & The North Face

A survival guide: What to look at when buying a winter jacket for NYC?

  1. A hood
  2. Body shell
  3. Lining
  4. Filling: Down works the best!
  5. Wool
  6. Waterproof & Water-resistant: Makes sure that you’re ready for NYC snow & rain

3. Good kicks

I can’t stress enough how important owning good pairs of kicks are to survive the NYC winters. Here’s what you need:

Comfortable sneakers

Ask any New Yorker what their favorite mode of transport is, and they will say ‘walking’! No, seriously, that & the subway. I know that Carrie Bradshaw had us believe that we could walk anywhere in the six-inch heels. But it’s not a practical choice. So you want to pack sneakers that allow you to stay comfy around the clock.

NYC Winter Essentials: Boots

No winter survival guide is complete without the staple winter boots. You need a functional pair that’s waterproof & comfortable. Please skip bringing any cutesy ballerinas, heels, or fancy suede footwear! They will end up getting wet & dirty in the snow, leaving you to fend for yourself barefoot. Try the snow boots from Sorell, Dream Pairs & Hunter Boots.

Roomi Tip: Add woolen & shearling socks too, they will keep your feet warm & snug

4. A comfortable bag

To enjoy NYC for all its beauty & traveling within the city you need a bag to add all your travel essentials. New York is an expensive city, so you want to carry things that don’t let you exceed your budget and helps you explore the city comfortably. I personally like to carry a backpack that can accommodate my camera and other NYC winter essentials like:

Water bottle

Stay hydrated on the go with a leak-proof water bottle. Additionally, I also like to carry an insulated water bottle for my caffeine fix. It helps me save on the crazy coffee charges that can go anywhere between $5 to $20.

Lip Balm

Cold winters and chapped lips are like that cringe couple that loves to PDA. It’s hard to break ’em up unless you have a lip balm that believes in third-wheeling like a boss.

Portable Charger

Don’t you hate it when you see a beautiful scene, and before you can capture it, your battery dies. This honestly has happened to me(even as a local) way more than I’d like to accept. Anywho, as a traveler, you need it more than anyone – for beautiful snaps + google maps. There’s nothing worse than getting lost in a new city w/o a map. Don’t you agree?

What is the cost of living in NYC for an international student?

]]> Does living in NYC and zero waste living really go hand in hand? Well, yes.

There are New Yorkers who have done and documented it. Meet Béa Johnson. Her family of four generates only a small jar of waste each year. I know, we can’t believe it either. If this gets a little part of you excited to try it out too in your shared apartment, then you’re in the right place. Read on for a realistic New York guide to start your zero waste journey.

“Refuse what you do not need; reduce what you do need; reuse what you consume; recycle what you cannot refuse, reduce, or reuse; and rot (compost) the rest.”

Bea Johnson on zero waste living in NYC

The key is to start slow and continue steadily. Take it a step at a time rather than trying to do zero waste life at once. The ultimate aim is a lifestyle that is both sustainable and comfortable to live. If you find it inconvenient, you are unlikely to stick to it in the long term.

To start out with, Roomi recommends the following tips.

Take stock of your buying habits

Carrying your own bag is a great start. The next step is being mindful about what you buy. Try bulk buying, so there’s no plastic packaging. Farmer’s markets often have products available that aren’t wrapped in plastic. Living in NYC also gives you access to some zero-waste stores, which makes sustainable shopping more straightforward.

Packaging takes up a lot of room in our waste-paper bins. So just by choosing products sold loose or with reusable packaging, you make a big difference!

Once you successfully practice zero waste shopping with your groceries, you can branch out. There’s a New York guide for almost everything, so it would be easy to find the right stores for sustainable shopping. Think clothes, cosmetics, and even vehicles.

This could list could be useful: Stores In New York City For The Sustainable Shopper

Sign up for the NYC composting program

Living in NYC is turning eco-friendly. The city has committed to reducing overall landfill waste by 2030. We have the largest composting program in the country, open to over 3.5 m residents.

The city has also partnered with non-profit organizations to redistribute pre-loved and reusable items. Instead of ending up in a landfill, second-hand products reach those in need through donateNYC or Housing Works.

Alternatively, you can compost at home for your plant babies! Composting is easy-peasy and can be done in any space. Even in a shared apartment or a condo. Trust us- your plants will thank you for it! A detailed guide to Composting in a Small NYC apartment.

Enjoy delicious zero-waste meals

There’s a common misconception that those who practice sustainability are somehow ‘missing out’ on common experiences enjoyed by others. This may have been true to some extent at the beginning of the movement but no longer. With more and more people seeing the value of a mindful, sustainable lifestyle, even restaurants in the city are looking toward zero-waste functioning. So, when it’s safe to go out again, you can take a break from your shared apartment (finally!) and enjoy a meal out without compromising your chosen lifestyle.

New York Guide Alert – Edible Manhattan takes you through 8 Zero Waste Dishes Now On Restaurant Menus

As we said at the beginning of this handy zero waste New York guide, this is just a practical place to begin. There’s a lot more you can do. Everyone’s zero waste journey looks different.

We’re sure once you get used to thinking about the sustainability of your habits, you’ll automatically be more mindful of your choices. Good luck to you!

]]> If you’re like the average person stuck quarantining in the pandemic, you are probably on the hunt for fresh content to keep your mind engaged. Especially when living in NYC, self-isolation can be difficult. Whether you have hit a Netflix overdose, or simply need fresh perspectives to get you back in tune with the vibrancy and bustle of New York City— look no further. Podcasts are here for the win!

In case you are new to the world of podcasts, they can be a wonderful source of information, humor, advice and everything else in between. Think of it like reading or conversing with your eyes closed. (Or open, we can’t tell you what to do!)

As a matter of fact, 3 out of 4 podcast listeners shared that they specifically enjoy tuning into podcasts to learn new things and gain new perspectives.

Luckily, we have compiled a list of the quirkiest podcasts that remind you why living in NYC is worth the trouble. Read on for the ultimate New York Guide to podcast listening, curated for you by your favorites at Roomi –

1. Freakonomics Radio

Freakonomics radio is the brainchild of New York economist Steven D. Levitt and journalist Stephen J. Dubner, authors of a best-selling book series and film of the same name. Join the hosts each week as they make you put on your thinking caps by telling you “things you never thought you wanted to know (but do)”. No New York Guide would be complete without a mention of the beloved magazine “The New Yorker” and its host of podcasts. The talk radio segments run along selected topics ranging from the best of literature, news, and politics to poetry. For the literature lovers living in NYC, “ The New Yorker Fiction” segment digs into the archives of the magazine to find selected stories from legendary authors, followed by a discussion with an expert guest. You can find the latest episode featuring a short story by Steven Millhouser here.

The Modern Love Podcast is based on the popular New York Times column “Modern Love”. The episodes feature the best of the real, raw, and complicated love stories from writers living in NYC and beyond.
