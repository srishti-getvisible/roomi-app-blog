]]> San Diego is known for its pleasant climate, 70 miles of pristine beaches, and a myriad of world-class family attractions. Indeed, there’s no shortage of places to visit in this city. With its unique lifestyle to boot, it’s no wonder it’s such a great place to live in, and experience as a tourist! Gear up and read on to discover the best things to do in San Diego!

Must do outdoor activities in San Diego

1. Paraglide over the Coast

A tandem paragliding trip over the beautiful coastline is an amazing experience that everyone will enjoy. Unless, maybe, you’re terrified of heights. San Diego tourism typically provides more than 300 flyable days per year, boosting your chances of finding the perfect conditions during your stay.

Visit the website

2. Sunset Cliffs

If you’re on the lookout for the finest experiences in San Diego, Sunset Cliffs are ideal for hiking and recreation. The magnificent sandstone cliffs and pristine greenery, as well as the breathtaking ocean views, make for an amazing day or night stroll. There are a few quiet beaches nearby, too, and just getting to them can be an adventure in itself!

  • Timings & Fees: Open 24/7 and FREE to the public 365 days a year!
Know more

3. Mission Beach and Pacific Beach

This beach is a popular spot when it comes to San Diego tourism, particularly for surfing. It is perfect for beginners as well as seasoned surfers. You’ll find numerous water sports equipment rental shops strewn around the neighborhood, offering everything from jet skis to kayaks.

  • Timings & Fees: Open 24/7 and Free!

Sports activities in San Diego

1. Kayak the La Jolla Sea Caves

The La Jolla Ecological Reserve is located just offshore from the small seaside community of La Jolla. It is one of California’s most ecologically varied ecosystems and one of the cool things to do in San Diego. San Diego tourism thrives of this location – an awesome place to visit if you’re searching for cool things to do in San Diego.

Visit the website

2. Petco Park

Petco Park is often regarded as one of the best baseball stadiums in the country. It is a must-see for Padres fans or anybody who wants to see a baseball game. This spot checks off the list of cool things to do in San Diego, while taking in the stunning skyline.

  • Timings & Fee: Hours and Games prices vary; tours $20 for adults.
Visit the website

3. Hike Torrey Pines State Natural Reserve

If you want to cover all of the best things to do in San Diego, then the hiking paths in this reserve are worth visiting. They are generally short loops with stunning views of the ocean. The Guy Fleming Trail, a 2/3 mile circle with two ocean-view viewpoints and the Parry Grove Trail, a 1/2 circle with a native plant garden, are two of the most popular.

Visit the website

Must visit museums in San Diego

1. USS Midway Museum

The USS Midway Museum has been preserving the historic USS Midway, the twentieth century’s longest-serving aircraft carrier, since June 2004. If you want to explore some of the fun things to do in the city, this museum is a must-visit! You’ll get access to locations that aren’t usually open to the public, such as the crew’s sleeping quarters, the engine room, the primary flight control room, and the ship’s jail!

2. San Diego Natural History Museum

This museum, located in Balboa Park, developed out of the San Diego Society of Natural History, which was formed in 1874. This makes it the oldest scientific organization in Southern California and the third oldest west of the Mississippi.

Events

3. San Diego Museum of Man

The Museum of Man, as the region’s only anthropological museum, provides a detailed program that includes cannibalism, beer history, and human-animal relationships. It definitely ranks high on our cool things to do in San Diego list!

  • Timings: Wednesday to Sunday, from 10:00 am – 5:00 pm.

Soak in the culture of San Diego

1. Old Town

The Old Town is a renowned tourist destination that falls under the top places to visit in the city. Locals enjoy going there on a daily basis, while tourists like its old-world charm. The historic structures and the architectural combination are undoubtedly intriguing. The Old Town has preserved much of its fascinating past via its architecture and culture.

Visit the website

2. Balboa Park

Home to the renowned San Diego Zoo, this 1,200-acre park is the city’s cultural hub. Located downtown, Balboa Park is a great place for a stroll, bike ride or picnic. It’s a one-stop wonderland helping the city tourism thrive!

Visit the website
Buy Tickets

3. Cabrillo National Monument

Cabrillo National Monument is located on the southernmost tip of Point Loma. Juan Rodriguez Cabrillo, the first European explorer to cross the Californian coast in 1542, is depicted in the sculpture. The breathtaking panoramas are the major reason people travel to the monument, making it a part of the most popular tourist spots here.

  • Timings: 9:00am – 5:00pm

Explore restaurants, bars & shopping centers in San Diego

1. The Gaslamp Quarter

The Gaslamp Quarter is a city district where you can find anything from restaurants to retail malls to different forms of entertainment.

Plan your visit

2. George’s at The Cove

One of the best places to visit in the city with stunning panoramic views of La Jolla Cove from nearly every seat in the house. The upscale cocktail menu features all the summery drinks you’d imagine, from margs and martinis to mojitos and micheladas!

Visit the website

3. Morning Glory

Morning Glory’s design hits you with a checklist of Instagram hits—rose-gold accents, tiled floors, Millennial-pink booths, wallpaper galore, and an aesthetic interior. Certainly, one of the best places for a fulfilling breakfast (and a quick photoshoot).

Visit the website

4. Seaport Village

Here you can spend the afternoon watching ships float in and out of the harbor while sipping coffee or shopping for souvenirs. Seaport Village is one of the best places to visit in San Diego as the 14-acre village is home to more than 50 shops and 13 dining outlets.

Visit the website

Though the rent isn’t as scary as other parts of California, it’s far from cheap but certainly tight on space. In fact, while the average rent for a two-bedroom apartment in the city was around $1,900 as of February. This number is far less than the $2,400+ price tag in Los Angeles. And a whopping $4,100+ rent price in San Francisco, where housing has been in high demand. Like many U.S. metros, San Diego’s vacancy rate has been dropping rapidly in recent years.

Only 3.62 percent of San Diego’s rentals were vacant in 2014, nearly 2 percent less than in 2011. When you compare this to the national vacancy rate average of 6.32 percent, San Diego proves to be as hard as many other large metros to find affordable housing. The good news is that San Diego residents may be able to rent up for a lower cost than elsewhere. That is if you know where to look. We talked to some local residents who offered their advice on how to snag luxury rentals. All while using a roommate finder and some San Diego know-how!

What’s the Deal in San Diego?

The Affordability of San Diego Rentals

Local renters like Walter Meyer are concerned about the rising rents in his city. But they recognize San Diego’s relative affordability when considering the amenities and space to share. On average, renters spent less than 25 percent of their median household income on median monthly gross rent in 2014. Which means the average San Diego is not rent-burdened, by definition. Meyer says he feels fortunate enough to live in a city where he can afford shared housing. That too in a nice part of town, even while the rents continue to rise.

“The neighborhood in which I live, Hillcrest, is considered one of the most desirable in San Diego — actually, the country — it has made national lists of ‘most walkable’ or ‘best neighborhoods,” he says.

“There are a lot of bars, restaurants, theaters, gyms, coffee houses, etc., and we are walking distance from Balboa Park and have easy access to all of the major freeways. We pay a price for this convenience — our rents do seem to be climbing faster than other areas.”

Another local renter, Daeli Parker, agrees that if you have some help (like a good roommate finder), you can find the right place in San Diego and live a comfortable life.

“The first place we rented was nice, as far as luxury goes: Rooftop pool, valet parking, etc.,” she recounts. “We were right in downtown so everything was within walking distance, and that was a plus, including the trolley, which went as far east as El Cajon.”

She continues that downtown San Diego sports a variety of options, many of them small and expensive, but with a few luxury high-rises which offer the amenities she had. But in these areas, the rent is high, and climbing, and the choice apartments might already be taken.

Expenses and Rent Rise

Though the median renter isn’t rent-burdened, many in San Diego do feel the weight of rising rents, and the city is getting too expensive for some — making the need for a good roommate finder greater than ever.

Jessica Whitt* is sad to say it, but she’s jumping ship later this year for greener (cheaper) pastures. Whitt, who rents alone and is fortunate enough to work from home (no need to commute), says she has no reason to pay high prices to stay put, though she won’t be happy to say goodbye.

“I love it here, but the rental market has gotten out of control,” she says. “Every year they raise the rent, and it’s impossible to negotiate because if someone new moves in, they can get more money from them. The attitude I encountered in general is that they’re doing you a favor, which really bugs me because I’m making them money.”

It’s a common complaint, even in other major cities.

Pro Tip: Use a Roommate Finder in San Diego to Rent Up

Whitt and Meyer had some advice for renters trying to score a rental (or keep one) in San Diego. Whitt recommends if you already have a place, hang onto it, especially if it’s within your means.

“Renew your lease — if possible — during the ‘low’ season, which is September to March in San Diego,” she offers. “In my experience, they’re more likely to negotiate during that time. Also, if you know you want to stay there, renew for more than a year.”

She also points out it’d be easier to afford rising rent with the help of a roommate, as long as communication is solid. Average renter household sizes have increased nationwide, and it’s no coincidence. As rental markets get more expensive and more competitive, renters are making room for hopefuls. It’s a way for renters to create more housing and lower cost for everyone without relying on developers and city officials to build affordable housing.

Meyer says the best roommate finder is a local renter in San Diego. So ask around, make friends, and get the word out there!

“I hear stories all the time of people who called about an apartment and by the time they ran over to see it 20 minutes later, the place had been rented,” he says. “But if you have a friend or a friend of a friend who is renting, perhaps you can get it before they go public with the rental notice.”
