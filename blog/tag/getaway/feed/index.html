]]> Looking for a fun weekend getaway? Connecticut could be the perfect spot for you! This state is filled with beautiful scenery and quaint towns, offering locals and tourists a range of weekend activities including boutique shopping, hiking, scenic ocean views, and delectable meals. Additionally, there’s plenty places to visit in Connecticut and things to do in CT for everyone!

Connecticut is located in the southwestern corner of New England bordering Long Island Sound, New York, Massachusetts, and Rhode Island. And there are many places to visit in Connecticut with all sorts of landscapes – from beautiful beaches and rolling hills to small homey towns. Here are 5 places to visit in Connecticut for a perfect weekend getaway!

1. Westport

If you’re looking for a cute beach town to while away the hours, Westport – located in Fairfield Country, CT – could just hit the spot. The town center in Westport consists of numerous shops and restaurants. Shop for your favorite brands such as Lulu Lemon and Urban Outfitters then finish the day off with a delicious dinner. And one of the best places to visit in Connecticut for the love of Mexican cuisine is Bar Taco. Transport yourself to a tropical beach in South America while you tuck into mouth-watering tacos.

Westport is also home to Compo Beach. Since the weather is getting cooler, take an evening stroll with friends or a loved one along the beach!

2. Ridgefield

Love small towns? Then one of the best places to visit in Connecticut would be Ridgefield for an authentic small town experience. Ridgefield is a small town treasure of Connecticut – home to numerous boutiques and delicious restaurants, such as Bailey’s Backyard and Luc’s Cafe.

Bailey’s Backyard offers a range of farm to table dishes, and Luc’s Cafe is a French restaurant that offers its customers an extensive authentic cuisine. Bailey’s Backyard and Luc’s Cafe both offer outdoor patio seating, so you can enjoy some alfresco dining. Bon appetit!

3. Fairfield

Fairfield is another charming beach town located on the shoreline of Long Island. It’s home to Jennings and Penfield beaches, as well as a heap of cool restaurants and shops in town. Try a few of Fairfield’s restaurant hotspots such as Geramino’s Tequila and Wine Bar or The Sinclair. You’ll come across a few local bars that are ideal for a drink after dinner. Additionally, Fairfield University is just down the road from downtown Fairfield. Talk about the definition of a perfect college town!

4. Kent

One of the more scenic towns located in Litchfield County, Kent is one of the must see places to visit in Connecticut. It has a small town center as well as a range of outdoor treasures, making it the perfect town for lovers of the outdoors. Looking for things to do in CT? Look no further! Kent is home to numerous hiking spots such as Kent Falls, Macedonia Brook State Park, and Bulls Bridge.

Tired from a long day of hiking? Head into the town of Kent for a delicious meal. Try the Kingsley Tavern for classic pub food or Ore Hill & Swyft for a farm to table meal. There’s something for everyone in this beautiful town!

5. Greenwich

Greenwich is another town located in Fairfield County, and one of the must see places to visit in Connecticut. Moreover, it’s one of the oldest towns in Connecticut and is full of history and art, making it a great choice for those looking for a culture kick. Greenwich is home to parks, gardens, museums, shopping, and restaurants. Looking for things to do in CT? Visit Caumsett State Historic Park Preserve or the Bruce Museum for art, science, and natural history exhibits.

And you can end the day in downtown Greenwich with a perfect meal. Try L’escale Restaurant for Mediterranian cuisine or Pasta Vera for authentic Italian food.
