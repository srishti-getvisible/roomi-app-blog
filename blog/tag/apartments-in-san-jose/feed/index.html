Getting To Know San Jose

‘San Jose’, which is Spanish for ‘Saint Joseph’ became the first chartered city in California in March 1850 after many periods of rule under different empires. San Jose had established itself as a major trade depot with railroad connectivity in 1864 improving the trade capabilities and enabling the shipping of produce from farms. Top Neighborhoods in San Jose

  • Willow Glen

Willow Glen is a charming neighborhood in San Jose known for shopping, indie cloth boutiques, specialty food stores, and an all year round Saturday Farmer’s Market with fresh local produce and foods. With regular art and wine festivals and great options for dining, Willow Glen and its tree-lined streets are a great place if you are planning on renting in San Jose.

  • Almaden Valley

Located in a valley and nestled between the Santa Teresa Hills to the east and the Santa Cruz Mountains to the west, Almaden is a great place to find a room for rent in San Jose. Known for being a great school district, Almaden offers a peaceful and safe environment to rent a studio apartment in San Jose. It is also home to many technology and research companies along with the IBM Almaden Research Center.

  • Evergreen

Evergreen is known for its stunning views of the foothills and many parks, green spaces, and recreational and sports areas. Groesbeck Hill Park is also located in the heart of Evergreen with basketball courts, baseball fields, playgrounds, and hiking trail. These make this a great neighborhood to find rooms for rent in San Jose if you are active and enjoy an outdoor lifestyle.

How transit friendly is San Jose?

Downtown San Jose is easily walkable with streets arranged in a grid format making it relatively easy to navigate and locate addresses. Beyond the downtown area, however, things are more spread out and you will need to look towards driving or using public transportation.

1. Subway

The local VTA light rail system connects downtown to Mountain View, Milpitas, Campbell and Almaden Valley. A VTA day pass lets you use buses and Light Rail lines all day which can be a great option if your commute requires an interchange.

2. Buses

The local Valley Transport Authority buses are another great way to get around the city. VTA buses cover most areas and you can easily check the routes to make sure your area is serviced before deciding to find rooms for rent in San Jose.

Cultural activities in San Jose

Being a hub of technology, finance and culture, there is plenty to see and do in San Jose with several museum of different genres and lots of outdoor activities as well.

The Tech Museum of Innovation: A great place to learn how technology affects our daily lives with an IMAX Theater, a cafe, and a retail store.

San Jose Museum of Art: An established museum known for its contemporary collection of modern art, focusing on the West Coast as well as other national and international pieces.

Downtown San Jose Public Art Walk: An ever changing collection of street and public art of all kinds that provides a 3-dimensional depth to the city.
