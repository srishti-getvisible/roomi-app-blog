]]> College is an extremely pivotal point in a young person’s life. Therefore, it is important that you take the time to pick the right school for you! New York is home to some of the top liberal arts schools in the country. It is also home to numerous liberal arts schools. If you’re looking for a smaller college, choosing a liberal arts school may be the best fit for you! If you’re looking for some small liberal arts colleges in New York, this list could be very helpful!

What’s special about liberal arts schools in New York, or elsewhere is that they have fewer students compared to public universities. The class sizes are smaller and as a result, students get more individualized attention from professors. Even though liberal arts colleges are smaller than universities, they still have all the traditional aspects of college such as clubs and Greek life.

Searching for a college and planning for your future career can be overwhelming. So, here is a list of some of the top liberal arts colleges in New York State to help narrow down your college search!

Marist College

Marist College is located in Poughkeepsie, New York, with a student population of around 5,000 and a student acceptance rate of 46%. This liberal arts school has been recognized by the Princeton Review and has been ranked as one of America’s top colleges by Forbes.

Marist is well known for their Florence study abroad program as well as their Fashion Merchandising major. This school is located right on the Hudson River and has scenic campus views that will make anyone fall in love with this school!

Check out Marist’s Princeton Review page here!

Hobart and William Smith Colleges

Nestled in the center of the Finger Lake Region, Hobart and William Smith Colleges is located in Geneva, New York. This liberal arts college has a student population of around 2,000 with an acceptance rate of 57%. Hobart and William Smith (HWS) sits right on Seneca Lake, which is one of the seven Finger Lakes. The campus is breathtaking and there is plenty to do around Geneva, including going to countless wineries and sailing on Seneca Lake during the warmer months.

HWS has one of the top study abroad programs in the country. In fact, the majority of students end up studying abroad at least once at HWS. HWS has been featured in the Princeton Review and the school is known for its outstanding professors.

Check out Hobart and William Smith’s Princeton Review page here!

Bard College

Bard College is a liberal arts school situated in Annandale-on-Hudson, New York, with a little less than 2,000 undergrad students. Their student acceptance rate of 65%. This school has been recognized by the Princeton Review as well as Forbes.

Bard is known for thriving in the arts and film courses; however, they also offer a range of other majors including language, literature, science, mathematics, computing, and social studies. Bard’s curriculum is rigorous, yet flexible at the same time.

Check out Bard’s Princeton Review page here!

Skidmore College

Located in Saratoga Springs, New York, Skidmore College has around 2,5000 undergrad students with an acceptance rate of 27%, making this college a little bit more difficult to get accepted to. There’s plenty to do both on and off-campus, including exploring downtown Saratoga and visiting the Saratoga Race Track. Saratoga Springs is truly the perfect college town!

Skidmore College has over 60 possible majors and is known for its excellent visual and performing arts programs. Skidmore is the perfect fit for any creative individual. Not to mention, Skidmore is also a Princeton Review recipient.

Check out Skidmore’s Princeton Review page here!

Union College

Based in Schenectady, New York, Union College is home to around 2,000 undergrads. Union has a student acceptance rate of 39%, therefore making it a selective school. Featured in the Princeton Review, Union College is a liberal arts school that offers 58 majors, including engineering.

With over 100 clubs and 26 varsity and athletic teams, there’s something for everyone at Union College. Not to mention that it’s a short journey away from major areas such as NYC and Boston.

Check out Union’s Princeton Review page here!
