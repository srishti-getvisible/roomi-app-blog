Getting to know Santa Barbara

With a colorful past, as vibrant as its geographical climate, Santa Barbara has been inhabited for over 13000 years. First colonized by native Americans, and then taken over by Spanish explorers (who gave the land its current name of the Mission of Saint Barbara,) it was destroyed by a tsunami and earthquake in the 1800s.

Rebuilt in the late 1800s, the Presidio of Santa Barbara survives to this day. After the Spanish reign ended, and a brief Mexican occupancy, the land was won over by the Americans.

During the Gold rush years of California, the town became a haven for bandits, gamblers, turning it into a dangerous and lawless area.

After the war though, the town had the world’s largest movie studio during the era of silent film – Flying A Studios, a division of the American Film Manufacturing Company.

How transit-friendly is Santa Barbara?

To find cheap rooms for rent in Santa Barbara, the one thing you need to know well is how to get around, right? So here’s the info on how to get around the city!

1. Walkability in Santa Barbara

The communities in Santa Barbara have been improving their pedestrain routes, and continue doing so. In 2011, the Pedestrian and Bicycle Information Center (PBIC) designated Santa Barbara a Gold Level Walk Friendly Community for its walkability initiatives and programs.

2. Cycling in Santa Barbara

A silver level city in bicycling as per the League of American bicyclists, Santa Barbare has a well planned network of bike trails and other resources for enthusiasts.

If you’re looking for good climbs on your two wheeler, there are many famous climbs to cover such as the Gibraltar road, painted cave, etc.

If you’re a tourist in the city when looking for rooms for rent in Santa Barbara, opt for bike rentals to get around the city. You’ll love the pace, take in the vibe, and save on money too!

3. Airports in Santa Barbara

You’ll find all kinds of commercial flights at the Santa Barbara Municipal airport. And if you’re one of the rich ones (we envy you,) you can find private jets and charters at the Santa Barbara Aviation.

4. Public transport in Santa Barbara

Amtrak’s rail service offers trains at the Santa Barbara and Goleta train stations to get around the city.

And if you prefer buses to get around , the Santa Barbara Metropolitan Transit District offers local bus services all around the city. There’s also Clean air express buses, VISTA buses, and Santa Barbara Air buses to get around when finding rooms for rent in Santa Barbara.

Ganna Walska Lotusland

Hanna Puazk, a.k.a, Ganna Walska, a famous opera singer, bought this land and renamed it Lotusland (after naming it Tibetland in hopes of turning it into a retreat,) in honor of the sacred Indian lotus growing in one of the ponds on the property.

And then, she fell in love with gardening. She created a beautiful botanical garden of rare plants which is now on display for everyone. If you’re a plant lover, this is something you shouldn’t miss out on, while you’re here.
