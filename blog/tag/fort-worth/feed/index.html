]]> Moving to Texas’s Fort Worth? Did you know that, even though Dallas and Fort Worth are usually referred to as a single entity, they’re two completely different cities in Texas? And now that you do, let’s get to know all about cowtown! Whether you’re moving here or simply visiting, you should know some of the best things to do in Fort Worth. Here’s your guide to getting to know the city and its culture better. We’ll be covering some of the best places to visit and some of the cool things to do in Fort Worth.

Let’s get started!

Explore Outdoor Activities in Fort Worth

1. Cowtown Cycle Party

Great for individuals and big groups alike, the cowtown cycle party will get you moving through the city’s attractions – and you’ll make some cool friends on the ride. You can rent the cycle party for a two-hour tour with a group of up to 16 people.

Costs:

From $399

Timings:

Full Bike Tours

Monday – Saturday: 10am – 10pm

Sunday: Noon – 9:30pm

Make New Friends Mixer Tours

Thursday: 8 – 10pm

Friday: 8 – 10pm

Saturday: 12:30-2:30pm

Sunday: Noon – 2pm & 2:30 – 4:30pm

Book Tickets

2. Airfield Falls

If you’re looking for cool things to do in Fort Worth, head to Tarrant County’s largest natural waterfall! A gorgeous view with great native wildlife and beautiful monarch butterflies, you should definitely put this one on your ‘places to visit in Fort Worth’ list!

Costs:

Free

Timings:

5am – 10pm

Event Calendar

3. Mountain biking at the North Shore Trail

If you like mountain biking, you’ll love the North Shore trail! One of the most popular hiking and biking trails, the trail runs for about 10 miles from Rockledge Park to Twin Coves Park round the edge of Lake Grapevine.

Entry costs:

Free from Murrell Park and Twin Coves Park entry points; $10 for Rockledge Park entry point.

Trail rules & amenities

Enjoy Sports in Fort Worth

4. Get in the water at Backwoods Paddlesports

Exploring the Trinity River with Backwood Paddlesports is another cool thing to do in Fort Worth. It offers a wide range of water sports, with paddleboards, kayaks, and canoes available for hire! And if you’re a beginner, you can give their beginner classes a go.

Costs:

$18 per hour, per person (life jackets included)

Timings:

Monday – Friday: 10am – 7pm

Saturday: 10am – 7pm

Sunday: 10am – 7pm

Book Tickets
Events

5. Zip Line (and more) at DFW Adventure Park

Speed through the woodlands of Texas at the DFW Adventure Park with zip lining! And don’t worry if you haven’t done it before. With zip lines ranging from 250 – 450 feet in length, there’s something for everyone here.

Costs:

$40 per person

Timings:

Open Saturdays

Book Now
Detailed Costs

6. Learn Urban Fishing in the Trinity River

Ever thought of trying your hand at fishing? If yes, then this is one of the places you must visit in Fort Worth. The temperate climate of the area makes the Trinity River fish-able almost all year round! And beginners can take lessons by local fishermen with free classes every Wednesday.

Costs:

$550 per day (package deal, including flies, tackle, & equipment)

Timings:

All day

View Requirements

Take a Tour of Museums in Fort Worth

7. Modern Art Museum of Fort Worth

If you’re looking for places to visit in Fort Worth, put the oldest museum in Texas in your list. The Modern Art Museum is a beautiful structure that houses tours, lectures, art camps, workshops, and more, given by prominent figures in the art world. Moreover, they also host films and partner with other local arts organizations to offer music, dance, and live theater shows.

Costs:

$10 – $16

Timings:

Monday: Closed

Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday & Saturday – Sunday: 10am – 5pm

Friday: 10am – 8pm

Book Tickets

8. Kimbell Art Museum

If you love architecture, the Kimbell Art Museum will delight you. The building designed by Louis I. Kahn is one of the outstanding architectural achievements of the modern era. It is home to over 350 works that are pillars of aesthetic beauty and historical importance.

Costs:

$14 – $18

Timings:

Monday: Closed

Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday & Saturday: 10am – 5pm

Friday: Noon – 8pm

Sunday: Noon – 5pm

Event Calendar

9. Fort Worth Aviation Museum

Home to a collection of 24 warbirds and a US Navy Blue Angel Hornet in display, the Fort Worth Aviation Museum is a must for aviation enthusiasts visiting Forth Worth. The museum’s indoor displays include the B-36 Peacemaker and Forward Air Controllers Museums, a T-38 cockpit simulator and computer flight simulators.

Costs:

$5 – $10

Timings:

Wednesday: 9am – 4pm

Saturday: 9am – 5pm

Sunday: 11am – 5pm

Book Tickets

Indulge in the Culture of Fort Worth

10. Fort Worth Opera Festival

The Fort Worth Opera, as well as being a place to showcase and create new American operas, also offers education programs for all ages to inspire and instil creativity. So if you’re a fan of all things opera, this is one of the cool things to do in Fort Worth for you!

Timings:

Monday – Friday: 9am – 5pm

Event Calendar

11. Fort Worth Classic Guitar Society

Ever wanted to learn the guitar? If yes, then this is the place you must visit next time you’re in Fort Worth. You can enjoy musical performances and learn classical guitar here too. And if you’re a musician, you can also apply to perform in concert here!

Costs:

Admission ranges from free (or donation based) to $150, depending on the event.

Learn at the Guitar Camp

12. Will Rogers Memorial Center

If you’re on the hunt for cultural places to visit in Fort Worth, the Will Rogers Memorial Centre could be the place for you. This 120-acre event facility hosts all kinds of events, from corporate and educational, to agricultural and sporting events, and more!

Timings:

Monday – Friday: 8am – 5pm

View Brochure

Eat, Shop & Be Merry in Fort Worth

13. Ellerbe Fine Foods

What was once a gas station is now a place where fine dining meets farm-to-table cuisine! You can choose to dine inside in one of several dining rooms or on the outdoor patio. The food here is unique: think garden edamame ravioli and Maw Maw’s bread pudding, which makes it worth a visit on its own. Additionally, they have a selection of great wines for having a good time when you’re in Fort Worth.

Costs:

$31 – $50

Timings:

Tuesday to Thursday: 5 – 9pm

Visit the website
Event Calendar

14. Thirty Eight + Vine

Wine by the ounce, anyone?

This wine bar offers 42 rotating wines dispensed by the ounce at the touch of a button! When looking for cool things to do in Fort Worth, this unique, self-serve concept wine bar will allow you to sample, discover, and then, of course, drink your favorite wine to your heart’s content. With so many amazing wines on offer, though, it might be hard choosing just one!

Timings:

Tuesday & Thursday: 4 – 10pm

Friday: 10am – 2pm

Saturday: Noon – 10pm

Sunday: 1 pm – 8pm

Event Calendar
View Menu

15. Sundance Square

And when you’re done with all the places you must visit in Fort Worth, why not stop by the Sundance Square, where there are 35 square blocks of shopping, dining, and entertainment establishments for you to enjoy?

Timings: 6am -11 pm
