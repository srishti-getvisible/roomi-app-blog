How transit friendly is Porto

1. Trams

The Porto tramway network is an integral part of the public transport system of Porto. It is operated by the Sociedade de Transportes Colectivos do Porto (STCP) and is an affordable way to get around the city, with 3 routes to choose from. Why not try a tram ride when you’re looking for rooms for rent in Porto?

2. Metro

Metro do Porto an extremely efficient system to get around the city at an affordable price. The signage throughout stations and on trains is very clear and helpful. This makes it easy for tourists to get around with limited Portuguese language skills. There are also tons of shops and restaurants at stations to enjoy while you wait for your train.

3. Buses

The bus system in Porto is an excellent option for tourists, students, and locals. There are two types of buses in Porto, urban and interurban. Buses are an extremely affordable and efficient way to reach your destination and they make looking for cheap rooms for rent in Porto simple!

Cultural Activities in Porto

One of the key things to do in Porto is to take advantage of everything the city has to offer. Whether you’re looking for fun or culture, there’s no shortage of incredible tourist attractions and events to keep you busy. There’s always something exciting happening in the area!

1. Visit Livraria Lello Bookshop

Livraria Lello is a unique bookstore in Porto, Portugal. It’s a stunning example of detailed architecture and decor from another era. The store originally opened in 1881 and it’s become one of the most popular bookstores in Portugal thanks to its beautiful aesthetics. This bookshop is nothing less than a paradise for book lovers looking for rooms for rent in Porto.

2. Visit Casa da Música

The Casa da Música is a striking multi-purpose arts center that combines art and music within one spectacular space. With its unique architecture and in-house symphony orchestra, it has become a symbol of both culture and music in Porto.

3. Visit the Foz do Douro

The river Douro is a remarkable place if you are looking to spend some time off the beaten track. Escape the crowds of the city center and explore this relaxing waterfront with views over the coast. Especially scenic is the part known as Foz do the Douro, or “Foot of the Douro”.
