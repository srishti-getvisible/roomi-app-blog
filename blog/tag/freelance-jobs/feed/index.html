]]> When you have a passion for writing and can even make money out of it along the way, it sounds surreal. Well, of course, making it happen isn’t always simple. Finding freelance jobs for writing can be challenging even for writers with prior experience, and breaking into the business is downright puzzling. Some writers start out by searching for small freelance writing jobs online. Others work in a related business, like public relations, and have enough of a portfolio to approach past clients, and contacts.

One of the biggest obstacles for writers attempting to get paid for their work is finding legitimate, paid, online writing jobs. It’s all too easy to stumble across freelance writing “gigs” that offer little more than exposure — which doesn’t put money in the bank. To end up with good freelance jobs let’s get down to the basics.

What are freelance jobs

It’s pretty simple, freelance writing is the practice of writing for money. Importantly, working on one’s own and not being employed by a company or organization forms as a core aspect of freelance jobs. Freelance writers produce written content to suit the needs of their clients, either working from home or in a rented office space.

The advantage of having flexibility and a range of clients to work for, makes freelance job interesting. Writers can have many different clients, or, one very large client with a lot of steady, ongoing work. In freelance writing jobs, you can work as a generalist and cover topics as diverse as lifestyle or personal finance. Or, you can specialize in one specific area, such as cryptocurrency or architectural design.

Roomi Tip: Work on your portfolio and develop your niche as specialists tend to find more work and thus, earn more than generalists.

The 4 most profitable freelance writing niches

It’s vital to analyse where you fit in and decide what kind of writer you want to be. There are hundreds of different niches and different writing styles to choose from depending on your skill set. From copywriting and reviewing to non profit freelancing opportunities, the choices in freelance writing jobs are endless. Many of these niches can give you an edge as they are more rewarding than others. Here are some of the most profitable freelance writing niches.

Digital Marketing: Trending in freelance jobs

Businesses and entrepreneurs are cybernating the majority of their business. This means they’re relying on digital marketing companies, such as email newsletter companies, optin services, online accounting software, online hosting, and more to boost sales in the digital world.

Technical Writing

Technological knowledge are key for technical writing. These freelance writing jobs, in particular, are among the most high paying. That’s because they involve a significant amount of technical know how, yet writers must still be able to present the information in a way that is simple for the common person to understand. For instance, blockchain, software development, supply chain management, and AI.

Financial Writing: One of the highest paying freelance jobs

Finance is another industry where writers are paid a premium for excellent content. While it’s not a new niche, it’s still an important industry since finance and money-saving tips can change as the markets evolve. Topics covered in this niche include credit cards, personal finance, taxes, and insurance.

How to get freelance writing jobs

Where should you look for freelance jobs for writing?

FlexJobs

One of the top job boards for remote work, FlexJobs facilitates you to create a custom job search profile to meet your needs. Select your categories under “Writing”, your preferred work schedule, your experience level and more to narrow down your search results to those that best fit what you’re looking for.

A subscription is $14.95/month or $49.95/year, and you can save up to 50% off by using code “JOBS” at checkout.

MediaBistro

MediaBistro is a great resource for media freelancers of all stripes, offering online courses, tools and information that can help you navigate your career.

Human Jobs

Human Jobs offers freelancing jobs humanities and liberal arts graduates, for example, individuals with degrees in English or history. The site features freelance opportunities as well as full-time jobs. Created in 2020 by a history PhD student at Oxford, it includes plenty of writing, content and communication roles.

Behance Creative Jobs

Behance allows freelancers to upload your past projects to quickly create a visually-pleasing online portfolio. This makes it a great resource for writers without a website. It has its own job board which you can browse to find your next career move or freelance gig!

Content Writing Jobs

This site with freelance writing jobs board includes remote, freelance, contract and full-time jobs. To examine these hand-picked writing opportunities, visit the site online, sign up for daily job alerts. Or else, subscribe to its paid newsletter that shares brand new openings once per week for $10/month.
