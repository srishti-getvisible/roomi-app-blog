]]> Santa Monica is a beachfront city in California, bordered by Los Angeles and Santa Monica Bay. Originally inhabited by the Tongva people, today the many attractions have made it a popular tourist city. With a coastal Mediterranean climate, the city has great weather and sunshine almost all year round. If you are planning a visit, read on to learn about the best things to do in this city!

Related: Find Rooms For Rent in Santa Monica

Best things to do in Santa Monica: Outdoor activities

1. Santa Monica Pier

One of the most iconic sites in California, a visit to the pier has to be on your list of things to do in the city. Also a popular photo spot, the Pier is lined with shops, musicians, and of course, the famous Ferris wheel at the end.

Costs and Timings: Accessible to the public. Store timings and costs vary.

Visit Santa Monica Pier

2. Palisades Park

This palm and eucalyptus tree-lined path offers stunning views of the ocean and the city. Mountains. Stretching across the coastline, Palisades Park is a big tourist attraction for anyone visiting the city.

Costs and Timings: Accessible to the public.

Visit Palisades Park

3. Santa Monica Mountains

Located not far from the city, a hike in the mountains is one of the most popular activities for outdoorsy folk. Also, from the things to do in the city, there is nothing like the stunning views and peace that feels far removed from the bustle and noise.

Costs and Timings: Accessible to the public.

Visit Santa Monica Mountains

4. Santa Monica Beach

One of the biggest attractions has to be the Beach. Stretching three and a half miles, the beach is a hub for both locals and tourists looking to have fun. Additionally, with areas for picnics, sports, bike rentals, water sports, and much more, it is one of the best places to visit in the city when you want to chill out.

Costs and Timings: Accessible to the public.

5. Santa Monica Farmers Market

Offering the freshest variety of local produce, the Farmers Market takes place at multiple locations throughout the week. Hence, a visit to the Wednesday Farmer’s Market along Arizona Avenue needs to be on your bucket list.

Costs and Timings: Vary according to market and stores.

Visit Santa Monica Farmers Market

Best things to do: visit the Museums

6. Aero Theatre

This Art Deco-styled tourist attraction has been around since the 40s and is owned by American Cinematheque. Screening classic films and contemporary independent movies, it is one of the best places to go in the city for film buffs.

Costs and Timings: Check the website for shows and timings.

Visit American Cinematheque

7. Museum of Flying

One of the biggest attractions for aviation enthusiasts, this museum is dedicated to documenting the history of flight. Additionally, The Museum of Flying features exhibits from aviation companies like Boeing, Hughes Aircraft, Lockheed, North American, and Northrop.

Costs:

  • Adults: $10
  • Seniors / Students: $8
  • Children ages 3-12: $6
  • Children 2 or younger are free

Timings: Wed-Sun: 10 AM-5 PM

Visit Museum of Flying

8. Heal the Bay Aquarium

This Aquarium is should be on your list of things to do in the city when you’re visiting the Pier and Beach. Located conveniently under the Pier, it is dedicated to understanding and conserving the Southern California coastal waters.

Costs: Open to Public

Timings: 12:30-5:00pm

Visit Heal the Bay Aquarium

9. California Heritage Museum

Located in a restored 1894 house, this museum was originally home to the son of the founder of the city. Also a big tourist attraction for history buffs, it captures the history of the state with rotating exhibitions.

Costs:

  • Adults: $8
  • Seniors: $5
  • Students: $5
  • Children under 12: Free

Timings:

Wednesday to Sunday: 11 AM-4 PM

Visit California Heritage Museum

10. Camera Obscura

An offbeat tourist attraction, this place offers a unique optical experience using rudimentary camera-like technology. Also, you can view a live projection of the beachfront in this unique contraption, away from the crowds.

Costs: Open to the Public

Timings:

  • Mon-Fri: 9 AM to 3 PM
  • Sat: 11 AM to 4 PM
Camera Obscura Art Lab

Best things to do: visit the Restaurants

11. BOA Steakhouse

If you love steak and seafood, a meal here should be on your list of things to do in the city. Serving world-class meats plus options for vegans and alternative diets, this place is sure to delight anyone.

BOA Steak Website

12. Del Frisco’s Grille

Overlooking the pier, this restaurant is one of the best places to go in the city for great local food. And the locally sourced ingredients and great decor make for a great vibe that you need to experience yourself.

Eat at Del Frisco’s Grille

Though the nightlife in Santa Monica may not be the highlight of the city, you’ll never have a boring evening in the city. Compared to the numerous things to do in nearby Downtown Los Angeles, the city may not seem like a party town, but the upscale lounges and bars in Santa Monica provide plenty of entertainment for all those who love an interesting nightlife. So, which bars in the city are worth exploring? Let’s look at Roomi’s guide to one of the best things to do in Santa Monica (and anywhere, really)… exploring the city’s best bars!

What is Santa Monica Nightlife Like?

The laidback beach city vibe permeates every aspect of Santa Monica. Hence, it’s not surprising that the nightlife in Santa Monica is not as energetic as in Hollywood or nearby Los Angeles neighborhoods. Here, you get to enjoy a mix of entertainment options, from cozy, dimly lit lounges, to rooftop bars in Santa Monica that offer a friendly ambiance. You’ll find your fair share of options to explore while you’re in the city.

Bars in Santa Monica You Can’t Miss Out On

Santa Monica isn’t a party town by any means, but its nightlife sure isn’t lacking. Want to enjoy a great evening with friends, or planning your bar-hopping route for the weekend? Roomi’s got your back with the list of the best bars in Santa Monica that need to be on your radar.

Head to the Basement Tavern, a historic venue

From the 90s themed Sunday nights, to live Karaoke bars on Wednesday nights, there’s always something going on at the Basement Tavern. The Basement Tavern brings one-of-a-kind energy with its speakeasy-style atmosphere and unique location at the basement of the aptly named Victorian, a Victorian-era-style house that has been converted to an events venue. While cocktail bars in Santa Monica aren’t exactly a rarity, you won’t want to miss out on this enchanting joint.

Get the true dive bar experience at the Gaslite

Favored by the locals, the Gaslite is known to be one of the best joints in Santa Monica where you get an authentic dive bar feel. Skip long lines, and don’t worry too much about missing out on an exciting nightlife experience when you’re at this joint. From a dance floor that let’s you show off all your moves, to free popcorn and delicious drinks, you cannot miss out on heading to this bar when you’re in Santa Monica.

Upscale your evening at the Elephante Beach House

Among the list of the best things to do in Santa Monica, exploring the Elephante Beach House has got to be near the top of the list. Founded by Nicholas Mathers, who is not only an experienced restauranter but a designer as well, the vibe of this place cannot be compared to other rooftop bars in Santa Monica. Once you enter, you won’t just be marveling at the custom furniture, but the extensive list of wine and Italy-inspired cocktails they offer as well. On any given night, this place is buzzing with people – a great way to expand your circle when you’re new to the city.

Enjoy a swanky evening at The Room

The Room is undoubtedly one of the most famous bars in Santa Monica, and for good reason. Though the place looks upscale, you’ll find that the cozy booths make it more of a hideaway lounge. With a regular roster of DJs and delicious drinks, this is the place to head to when you want a memorable night with your friends. You’ll need to opt for a ‘smart & stylish’ dress code, but be sure to put on something that you can dance in!

Don’t miss the happy hours at the Misfit Restaurant & Bar

Good food, booze, and service, what else could you want from one of the most frequented cocktail bars in Santa Monica? Famous for their summery cocktails like Sangria, Basil Gimlet, and other concoctions, you can enjoy your fill during the lengthy happy hours in the joint. What do the locals recommend, aside from the delectable drinks? Surprisingly, it’s their salted chocolate chip cookie, but we ain’t complaining!
