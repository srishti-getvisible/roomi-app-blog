]]> The holidays are here! That means parties, fun, parties, family… did we mention parties? They’re undeniably one of the best parts of the festive season. It’s a great opportunity to connect with friends and family and get into the holiday spirit. That being said, planning a holiday party that runs smoothly can be stressful. This year, you don’t have to fret, because Roomi’s here to help! Stay tuned, as we list out some holiday party ideas and tips to help you make the most of this festive season.

Holiday Party Tips

1. What’s on the menu?

When planning your menu, remember to mix things up. You can include a variety of dishes, drinks, desserts, and bite-sized snacks.

Plan your menu according to the estimated number of guests, but leave room for more just in case. Use an online menu maker tool to design beautiful menu cards to let guests know what they’re eating.

2. Create your guest list and choose a date that works

Do you want to stick to close family members or will you invite all your friends, neighbors, their pets and the local line-dancing society?! Draft up your guest list early on and liaise with key guests to choose the best date.

3. Send out invites early

Once you’ve set a date for the party, you’ll need to send out invites. It’s advisable to send them out a few weeks – or even a month – before the due date. Mail them in the post, old-school style, or send out online invitations.

4. Venue

Selecting a venue depends on the number of people you’re inviting. If the party can’t be hosted at home, consider booking a bigger venue. If this is the case, then book the venue early to avoid anything spoiling your plans.

5. Choose your decor

Decorating the venue is a sure-fire way to capture the spirit of Christmas at your party. The kind of decorations you’ll use largely depends on the theme you’ve chosen. Let’s say you’re going for a classic dinner party. You’ll need decorations like candle holders, or even flowers. We’ll get into great theme ideas shortly! For your decorations, you can get a little holiday party inspiration from a movie or a local craft store.

6. Curate a Christmas playlist

Nothing like good music to give some holiday party inspiration, and stir up the festive mood at your party! Make a playlist of fun and popular holiday songs —both old and new. Use platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube to create a festive playlist.

7. Choose a theme

A great theme sets the atmosphere and tone for your party. It also determines the decorations, lighting, and color scheme. Here are some themes you can try:

  • Classic dinner party
  • Pajama party
  • Ugly Christmas Sweater
  • Secret Santa
  • Tree Trimming
  • Winter Wonderland

8. Plan parting gifts to give to your guests after the party

Don’t let your guests go home empty-handed. Include cool parting gifts in your budget while planning your party. You can buy to-go boxes for guests to take home yummy homemade desserts and treats.

Holiday Get Together Ideas

It’s okay if you’re stumped on ideas for your party. Here are some fun holiday party ideas you can try to make that day memorable:

1. Fun games

When choosing games, consider your guest list. How many people are coming and what mix of personalities can you expect? This will help you plan games and even prepare some prizes to spice things up.

  1. Gift wrap game: This could be an individual or team game. Test your guests to see who can wrap a gift the fastest – with only one hand!
  2. Holiday movie trivia: This is a fun holiday get-together idea. Have your guests answer questions on classic and modern Christmas films.
  3. 2 Truths & a Lie: In turns, let guests give 3 statements, and have the others try to spot the lie.
  4. Jingle Bell Toss: See which of your guests has the best aim. Hot-glue cups to a surface, and toss jingle bells in them to score a point.
  5. Christmas Limbo: Find out how low your guests can go in a Christmas-themed limbo challenge. You can use a candy cane or a Christmas scarf to set the bar.
  6. Charades: Make your party more entertaining by having your guests act out their favorite holiday movies.

Remember to choose the ones that will suit the kind of guests you’re inviting!

2. Christmas Karaoke

Another great holiday get-together idea is having people sing along to Christmas songs and carols. Sing songs like Feliz Navidad, Deck the Halls, Jingle Bell Rock, etc. It’s time for Christmas Carol-oke!

3. Watch holiday movies

Watching holiday movies is one of the most fun holiday party ideas ever! Offer your guests a great cinematic experience by sharing popcorn, sweets, or drinks.

4. Cookies and tree decoration

A Christmas tree decorating party is one of the most traditional Christmas parties there is. Make provisions for colorful Christmas ornaments for your guests to place on the tree.

Buy cookie decorations beforehand and let guests try their hand in decorating delicious cookies. Everyone can then swap their masterpieces after.

5. Pictures!

Picture taking is one of the best holiday party ideas because you get to save the fun party moments forever. You could even set up a quaint homemade photo booth to let your guests take beautiful holiday photos.

]]> The jolliest season of all is here! Christmas party is all about celebrating with friends and family, eating delightful feasts and of course, the annual viewing of Elf! However, taking on the responsibility of hosting a Christmas party at home can be stressful. To make your Christmas party a memorable one, we have some easy Christmas party ideas that go beyond just decoration or a Christmas menu.

So, whether you wish to have a glamorous Christmas party in a small house with friends and family, or are celebrating with roommates, we have the holiday party guide to make the big day worth remembering!

Hosting Christmas? Don’t Forget The To-Do List!

The holidays are the busiest time of year for most people, but planning ahead can ensure everything runs as smoothly as Santa’s Workshop when you’re hosting a Christmas party at home!

  • [ ] Mark a date: It’s important to pick a date for your celebration so it doesn’t clash with anyone’s holiday plans.
  • [ ] Guestlist: Design your guest list according to your apartment size and the number of people it can comfortably cater for. Even if you’re hosting Christmas in a small house, you can still have a stellar guestlist!
  • [ ] Plan out the budget: Work out a budget and stick to it! Short on cash? Plan a potluck party or a short and sweet festive menu, with just enough food to fill up everyone’s stomach.
  • [ ] Entertainment and games: You want a fun, lively party, and one way to get everyone involved while hosting Christmas is with some classic holiday games! Some games we love are: a) Charades b) Pictionary c) Trivia d) Christmas dance freeze e) Bring Me
  • [ ] Favors: Nothing makes for a memorable party more than a party gift. Some cute Christmas party favors include:
  • Socks
  • Personalized keychains
  • Handmade holiday cookies
  • Hot chocolate sticks of various flavours i.e. mint chocolate, orange chocolate, dark chocolate etc.
  • [ ] Send out invitations: The earlier you send invites, the better! Now that everything is ready, it’s time to let your loved ones know that you are hosting a Christmas party.

It’s Time to Decorate!

The sound of the crackling fire, the smell of cedar tree and freshly baked cookies, and the beauty of falling snow, Christmas is a holiday that appeals to our every sense!

Want to recreate this natural essence in your home? We’ve got some easy Christmas party ideas to get your guests’ holiday senses tingling!

Theme

It’s time to flex your decorating muscle. Now, it could be tempting to decorate the corners of your place in random Christmas garb. But we suggest you pick a theme, especially when you are hosting a Christmas party in a small house.

Consider a color theme, such as silver and red or gold and green. Consider asking your guests to dress up to match the theme, for some unmissable photo ops!

Playlist

Hosting a Christmas party at home without music isn’t really a party, so plan out your playlist in advance. Ask your friends and family if they already have a go-to festive playlist, or take song requests prior to the party.

Choose some classic tunes that people just can’t help singing along to. Everything from The Pogues to Mariah Carey is acceptable here.

Set Up a Self-Service Table

While your friends are gossiping about some hot news in their workplace, you don’t want to be too busy serving beverages to catch up.

Prepare a self-service table where you keep the drinks and snacks. Place some pre-made signature Christmas party cocktails in beverage dispensers, and guide guests to the fridge to help themselves to wine and beer.

For guests that don’t drink alcohol, add a section of non-alcoholic Christmas speciality drinks

like Cranberry-Ginger Shrub or super delicious Slow Cooker Pear Cider.

Decor

Now comes the best part…decor! Even if you’re feeling short on creativity, we’ve come up with a few easy Christmas party decor ideas for your home over the festive period.

Decorative Ladders

Decorative ladders are a stylish and smart way of revamping your small space for a Christmas party. Adorn it with fairy lights, bows and other embellishments.

Downsize The Tree

When you are dealing with a small apartment while hosting a Christmas party, downsizing your tree can save a lot of space. Whether it’s fresh or fake, size doesn’t matter when it’s sparkly and beautiful!

Scented Candles

Scented candles can create a festive mood in no time! We love gingerbread, pine tree, cinnamon and berry this time of year.

Pinecones

Pine cones are surprisingly cute mini Christmas trees. This super low budget decoration with a star on top won’t take long to become the star of your Christmas party.

Get Creative with Walls

Fortunately, wall decorations aren’t too heavy on your pockets. You can buy hooks and use them for hanging hand wreaths, pictures of Christmas celebrations, and some quotes. If you want to get more creative, you can use fairy lights to adorn them.

Budgeting Tips for Hosting Christmas

Hosting a Christmas party at home can be financially stressful too. From buying gifts to planning decorations, Christmas celebrations are often a stretch on the budget. But they don’t have to be

Here are some pocket-friendly tips:

  1. Set a dollar amount limit per gift
  2. Hand-make Christmas cards
  3. Give group gifts
  4. Buy pre-loved gifts
  5. Thrift your gifts
  6. Grow plants to give
  7. Host a Christmas potluck
  8. Donate gifts in someone’s name instead of giving them a gift
  9. Re-gift
  10. Thrift your decorations or re-use the ones left from last year
  11. Invite people to your Christmas party via email instead of using printed invitations

Voila! You’re ready to host a memorable party. Christmas is a time of celebrating bonds, stuffing desserts in your face and dancing to the Christmas tunes – make sure you relish every bit of it.

]]> The holiday season is upon us! And now all of the troubles and turmoil this year has come with seem to be at bay,. At least as long as the tree is up, fuzzy socks are hung, and Mariah Carey is being played. So to round it all off? Curling up with the best Christmas movies on Netflix (and a warm cocktail) is one of the most time-honored traditions.

And we want to help you figure out where to start the holiday indulging. So we have made a list of our favorite Christmas movies on Netflix!

1. Christmas in the Heartland

Think The Parent Trap, but set during the Christmas season. And it makes for one of the cutest Christmas movies on Netflix! So as the story goes, Kara Gentry (Sierra McCormick) and Jessie Wilkins (Brighton Sharbino) meet on an aeroplane headed to the same town. Furthermore, the identical girls decide to switch places, and naturally, drama ensues.

2. Santa Girl

Being Santa’s daughter definitely comes with its own set of challenges. In brief, she’s all set to marry some pre-approved guy and follow into the family business. Consequently, Cassie Claus (Jennifer Stone) is able to attend a semester of college in the “real” world. So safe to say, this is one of the most enigmatic Christmas movies on Netflix.

3. Christmas With My Father

In brief, comedian Jack Whitehall and his father, Michael, go on adventures around the world in the Netflix show Travels With My Father. So in this Holiday special, the father-son duo in their goofs and grumpy moments apply their signature chemistry. Equally important, the cherry on the top? The Fab Five of Queer Eye help the duo with their bond.

4. Get Santa

What do you think would go down when Santa shows up in your shed? This is one of the most adorable British Christmas movies on Netflix! And it follows the unfolding of events when a family goes through this same exact thing. So this film has a hapless Santa – played by Jim Broadbent, a staple in the other quintessential British kids’ movies, Paddington.

5. Holly Star

In brief, a broke, down-on-her-luck puppeteer returns home for the holidays. She gets caught up in an exciting treasure hunt with her paintball-obsessed best friend. Along with her tango-dancing grandmother, and a childhood sweetheart. To summarize, this is one of the quirkier Christmas movies on Netflix. And this one’s bound to make you long for love! ?

6. A Cinderella Story: A Christmas Wish

Since the 2004 Hilary Duff hit, the Cinderella Story series has been around like Santa’s elves. The fifth and most recent installment, starring Laura Marano, is about an aspiring singer-songwriter who gets a shot at stardom and love during the Christmas season. Nevertheless, this is one of the few musical Christmas movies on Netflix you can sing along to!

7. This Christmas

In brief, This Christmas is a Christmas movie on Netflix that centres on the Whitfield family, whose eldest has come home for the first time in four years. In fact, it is inspired by Donna Hathaway’s 1970 song of the same name. And the family gets together to deal with everything left unsaid, for this intense family Christmas special.

8. Angela’s Christmas

The author of Angela’s Ashes, Frank McCourt, is known for writing honest, moving stories about his family. In brief, Angela’s Christmas turns an incident in McCourt’s mother’s childhood in Ireland into one of the most beautiful and kind-hearted animated Christmas movies on Netflix that can be heartily enjoyed by the entire family!

9. A Very Murray Christmas

This is one of those Christmas movies on Netflix that feel too good to be true. It’s what the industry calls a star-studded Christmas Special, which is about the making of a star-studded Christmas Special! In brief, Sofia Coppola directs the hour-long show, which stars Bill Murray, George Clooney, Amy Poehler, Michael Cera, Chris Rock, Rashida Jones, Miley Cyrus, and many more.

10. Drunk Parents

To summarize, Alec Baldwin and Salma Hayek play a couple hiding their dwindling financial situation from their college-aged daughter. With holiday season approaching, so does the daughter’s time to come back home – and the parents’ actions are just about to catch up with them. While this could be the premise of a drama, it’s actually a star-studded Ch, even if its that of an acquired taste kind.
