How Transit-Friendly is Melbourne?

1. Walkability

In Australia, Melbourne is counted as the second most walkable city with friendly, culture-filled neighborhoods. Errands can be done on foot, but with the city’s efficient and free City Circle line, it’s easy to catch a tram if you prefer.

2. Roads & Traffic

The city has well-designed roads with car lanes, tram lanes, and bike lanes — making your visits to restaurants in Melbourne super easy!

You can also check this website for any road closures, road works, and traffic issues in the city.

3. Trams

Trams are a major form of transport in Melbourne — in fact, the city is famous for its trams! It has 1,763 stops and 24 routes and is hailed as the largest operating tram network in the world. Without a doubt, the best way to get around Melbourne is on the 35 City Circle route which covers lots of the city’s tourist attractions and is free to ride.

Another tram system in Melbourne is the City Loop — a mostly underground and partly surface-level subway system that goes around the CBD. The tram has five underground stations: Southern Cross Station, Flagstaff, Melbourne Central, Parliament, and Flinders Street. Using the tram makes it easy and convenient to look for rooms for rent in Melbourne, so be sure to utilize the service when you’re there.

If you’re looking for rooms with an average rent in Melbourne, using the tram is a great convenience.

4. Bus

Just like trams, buses are a major form of public transport in Melbourne, with 346 routes in operation around the city. They also have a Night Network bus system with 10 routes that operate every Friday and Saturday night. For regional areas, Victoria’s V/Line service provides routes to regional areas across the state.
