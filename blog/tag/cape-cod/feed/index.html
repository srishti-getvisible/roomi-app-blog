How transit-friendly is Cape Cod?

1. Ferries

Bay State Cruise Company and Boston Harbor Cruises offer seasonal, high-speed ferry service between Boston and Provincetown. While the Woods Hole to Martha’s Vineyard ferry can accommodate cars, there’s no need to take a car. Both islands have excellent public transportation service, so take your bicycle instead!

2. Airports in Cape Cod

Cape Cod thrives on tourism, so there’s no surprise that it has excellent airports for travel. Some of the airports in Cape Cod are:

  • Martha’s Vineyard Airport (MVY)
  • Nantucket Memorial Airport (ACK)
  • Barnstable Airport (HYA)

Extensive inter-town public bus system makes frequent stops at the MVY airport. Moreover, regularly scheduled daily flights connect from Boston to Hyannis and Boston to Provincetown.

3. Trails and bike paths

Cape Cod National Seashore and the surrounding communities have many miles of paved and unpaved hiking trails and bike paths available. While the Cape is separated from the rest of the state by a narrow body of water, this stretch of land is easily accessible from Boston and Providence thanks to canal bridges.

4. Buses

If you don’t want to drive in Cape Cod, there are public transit options available. Sit back and relax on one of the Cape’s buses. Some buses can take you around the Cape, while other buses connect Cape Cod to Boston, Brockton, and Providence.

Traditional Architecture in Cape Cod

Generally, Cape Cod homes have a steep roof, shingled exterior, symmetrical façade, and large chimney in the middle. While these houses can have multiple floors, most residences are single-storied. Additionally, Cape Cod house style is notoriously free of any excessive decoration, making them versatile enough for a variety of homeowners.

Nowadays, Cape Cod house style falls into one of three categories: a full Cape, three-quarter Cape, and half Cape. While full Cape houses boast a picture-perfect symmetry that is commonly associated with the style, three-quarter and half Capes are smaller, not to mention their entryways and chimneys are slightly offset.

Outdoor activities in Cape Cod

Cape Cod has many beautiful and pristine landscapes, so it’s an ideal setting if you like to explore off the beaten track. The weather is relatively warm for much of the year—well, warm by New England standards. This means you can spend more days outdoors, taking in the scenery.

There are hikes to be taken, and bike trails to cycle. If you’re feeling adventurous, go for a swim. While Atlantic cod aren’t as plentiful as they used to be on Cape Cod, there is still a variety of fish to catch. Buy or rent fishing gear and enjoy a peaceful afternoon out on the water. Kayaking opportunities abound, and kayak rental companies are spread out all over the Cape.

Sailing and boat trips are also available around the Cape, and if you’re lucky, you might even spot the occasional whale.
