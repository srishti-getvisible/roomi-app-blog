]]> Heading to the ‘mile high city’? First, you should know that Denver earned its nickname because of its elevation of 5,280 feet, or one mile above the sea level. And when you visit, you’ll instantly know that Denverites take pride in the name, and they take it seriously. From stadiums, to restaurants and more, you’ll hear it and see it across miles within the city. (See what we did there?) However, being at such an elevation does pose some downsides if you’re visiting for the first time. So do prepare for adjusting to its higher altitude if you’re traveling from a lower altitude. Now once you’re here and well acclimatized, whether you’re on vacation, or to start a new life, here’s your guide to the best things to do in Denver, Colorado.

Explore the outdoors in Denver

1. Denver Botanic Gardens

When looking for stuff to do in Denver, start here. This is a 23 acre land with 7 diverse theme gardens (including a Japanese garden of wind and pines, Monet Pool and more) with plants from around the world. They even host beautiful exhibitions in the space. Need we say more?

Entry costs:

  • Adults: $10 – $15
  • Seniors (65+), Military & Veterans (w/ID): $7 – $11.50
  • Children (3-15) and Students (w/ID): $7 – $11
  • Children 2 and under: Free

Timings:

  • 09:00 am – 08:00 pm

2. Mount Evans Scenic Byway

Looking for cool things to do in Denver? Here’s the perfect thing for you! The highest paved road in North America, and one of the highest in USA, this scenic highway spans across 28 miles and reaches a height of 14,130 feet! Drive through breathtaking views, with alpine lakes, massive granite walls and strands of twisted, ancient bristlecone pine.

Entry costs:

  • All sites: $15 per vehicle
  • Entering Summit Lake Park: $5 per vehicle
  • Motorcycles: $3 per person
  • Mount Evans Season Pass: $25
  • Bicycling/walking/driving through: Free

Timings:

  • Closed during the winter season.

3. Red Rocks Amphitheatre and Park

When looking for cheap things to do in Denver, this place will surprise you. An open air amphitheatre to attend amazing concerts and performances or even enjoy history or enjoy a day out in the park. There’s also film screenings and Red Rock’s Ship Rock Grille for you to grab a bite to eat while you’re here.

Entry costs:

  • Free entry

Timings:

  • Park and trails open one hour before sunrise till one hour after sunset.

7. Forney Museum of Transportation

If you’re looking for cool things to do in Denver with love for all things transport, head to Forney Museum! View over 600 artifacts related to historical transportation, including buggies, steam locomotives, carriages, rail equipment, bicycles, toys and more on display!

9. Wings Over the Rockies

Located on the former Lowry Air Force Base in Denver, Colorado, United States, the museum preserves the history of its operations from 1938 to 1994 in its collections, archives and research library (with over 15,000 books on aviation, aerospace and ballooning.) So if you’re looking for cool things to do in Denver, this museum will be worth your while.

12. Denver Performing Arts Complex

There’s nothing that tops this when looking for cool things to do in Denver. A four-block, 12-acre site containing ten performance spaces with over 10,000 seats connected by an 80 feet tall glass roof! Catch performances by the Colorado Symphony while you’re here!

How transit friendly is Denver?

While Denver is not the best city in terms of walkability, you can definitely get around walking downtown and in some neighborhoods. This may be something to keep in mind while finding rooms for rent in Denver. The streets that run from East to West are numbered and streets running from North to South are named. However, Downtown with its diagonal streets can be more confusing. When it comes to public transportation, here are your options.

1. Light rail in Denver

The Denver light rail can get you to and from most metropolitan areas. Make sure to check routes while looking for rooms for rent in Denver. There are also commuter rail lines to Arvada, Wheat Ridge, Westminster, and Denver International Airport.

2. Buses in Denver

The RTD Buses are the backbone of Denver’s transportation system. They are $2.60 for a one-way local trip. Local routes are denoted by a number and crisscross the city at regular intervals. There are also ‘Limited’ buses that stop less frequently on major arteries like Colfax and Colorado Boulevard.

3. Taxis in Denver

Taxis are easily available in Denver and can be useful if you are looking for apartments for rent in Denver in areas that are a bit out of the way. Ridesharing apps like Uber and Lyft are also great options to get around. The basic fee for a taxi is $3.50 and the kilometer price is $2.80. For standing and waiting time, $30.00 is charged per hour.

4. Cycling in Denver

The League of American Bicyclists has rated Colorado as the sixth most bicycle-friendly state. Denver has a large network of bike trails leading all over the city. Trails are usually marked and are sometimes dedicated or run alongside traffic. Cycling can be great to save and cut commute time if you are looking for rooms to rent in Denver.
