How transit-friendly is Huntington Beach?

The Transportation Division of the Department of Public Works provides a safe and efficient transportation system in Huntington Beach. There are plenty of opportunities to walk around neighborhoods, go on drives, or even hop on a bike. While most commuters prefer to drive, alternate commute options are available.

1. Walkability

With a walk score of 55, Huntington Beach has average walkability. Depending on where you find rooms for rent in Huntington Beach, you can enjoy exploring the city on foot. Some of the most walkable neighborhoods in the city are Downtown, Oak View, and Yorktown.

2. Roads/traffic

The average one-way commute time in this city is 28.9 minutes, which is slightly higher than the national average. Driving is the preferred commute option, followed by carpooling. Only 1.1% of commuters opt for public transit, as more people prefer to bike or walk. While other cities may have worse traffic, you should try to find rooms for rent in Huntington Beach close to your workplace. Traffic can pile up fast!

Major highways in the city include the Pacific Coast Highway, which connects the beach cities throughout California; the San Diego Freeway, which connects with all other major freeways in Southern California; and the Beach Boulevard, which runs directly through the heart of the city.

3. Taxis

Numerous taxi services are available in Huntingtin Beach, including Uber and Lyft. Fares start from $3.50, with an additional $2.75 for every additional mile and a waiting fee of $32 per hour.

4. Public transportation

While only a few commuters opt for public transportation, there are still some options available. The Orange Country Transportation Authority provides nine bus routes on its regional bus service. Additionally, Trailways and Greyhound are the two other major bus route providers in the city.

5. Airports

The closest airport to the city is the John Wayne Airport in Santa Ana. For those flying via JetBlue Airways, the Long Beach Airport will be the best option. However, if you’re still looking for more options, you can opt for the Los Angeles International Airport.

6. Cycling

Huntington Beach has over 10 miles of paved biking paths near the beach, offering the most beautiful views. Actually, the city is quite bike-friendly, with plenty of bike rental services as well. Although several roads have proper bike infrastructure, cyclists should still bike with caution in mind.
