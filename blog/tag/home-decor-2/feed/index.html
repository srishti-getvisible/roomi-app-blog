]]> Are you still dating your documents with 2021? Well, this is just an official reminder that 2022 is here, and you need to get out of the 2021 loop and move forward with the new year.

Many things have changed from last year (even though some have not). The decor trends last year were more about neutral shades and minimalism.

This year is quite different. From bold colors to natural textures- there is no dearth of house design trends to embrace. If you are not sure where to start, we have a comprehensive list of 2022 decor trends to get you started.

And guess what? These decor hacks are perfect for renters as well.

Let’s admit it, we don’t want to live with the same decor trends year after year, but we also do not want to get into trouble with our landlord. So, go ahead and personalize your rented house without the fear of breaking the terms of your lease. Another plus, these ideas do not require half of your salary or hours to implement.

14 Easy Interior Design Trends 2022 You Must Try

Ready to try some easy-breezy home decor trends 2022 for your house? Let’s get started.

1. Bring Nature Indoors

With work from home becoming the general norm, we are spending more time indoors than we could have imagined. It is no surprise that we want to ensure our connection with nature by bringing the outdoors inside and using nature-inspired decor trends.

Experiment with natural surfaces like wooden boards, grass mats, etc. Style up your small space with planters, and vases, and bring more greenery to your space.

2. Add Colors with Removable Wallpapers

Removable wallpapers are an affordable way to play around and add a new vibe to your house. Painting your rented house is not a landlord-friendly hack and may result in a fee upon move-outing.

So, go the easy way by using removable wallpapers and personalize your space without making any permanent changes.

And ohh don’t worry, wallpapers are not going out of style, at least not anytime soon. Choose from colors and prints that reflect your personality, and create a gorgeous looking apartment.

3. Decor Trends with Curtains and Drapes

Changing the window coverings is an easy way to add a little spark to your rented apartment. Ditch the boring vertical blinds and switch to colors, prints, and patterns for your window coverings.

Style your drapes in a quirky way can make a big difference in how your space feels. Plus, you can take your new curtains with you when you move to another place. Find your dream house now.

4. Multifunctional Spaces for Small Home Office Ideas

In the last two years, our living area has also doubled up as our work area. Some creative small home office ideas for dual use: add a beautiful room partition, and use your bedroom for work as well…or stack your dining room walls with wine and files together. Phew! A big plus for WFH.

5. Temporary Flooring

A key element of interior design trends in 2022 is beautiful flooring.

But ugh, will my landlord allow it? Most definitely, YES.

A landlord-friendly home decor hack is to go with temporary flooring. There are plenty of options from floor stickers to mountable tiles, all of which can give an amazing spark to your rented house.

Ohh, another thing, it can save you a lot of money and time! You can thank us later.

6. DIY Headboard

If you are looking for creative 2022 home decor trends, build a DIY headboard for your small space decor. Get rid of your old headboard and give it a new life with a nice headboard.

Reuse some old wooden pallet, give it a good base coat of your favorite color, and use a stencil and your second favorite color to add some accents. Tadaa, your headboard is up and ready to be placed.

7. Closet Creativity

We know how much you love to shop and how badly you need more space in your closet. An easy and creative way to optimize your space – is to stack similar clothes in one section; divide the drawer into sections for different clothes; add a chain hanger set that can easily hold six sets of clothes together. Apply these apartment living room ideas to your new rented house.

8. No Drill Hooks

Still craving more storage? Another landlord-friendly apartment living room ideas for storage is to add no-drill hooks.

Upgrade your storage without the stress and expense of drilling holes in your walls, instead of using stick-on hooks.

Want to make it more organized? Color code the hooks for each member of the family.

9. Switch up your Space

A little change is always good to add a new look and feel to your house. Rearranging the layout of your space is an easy decor hack that can make a big different.

For example: shift your couch to another wall or flip the layout of all of your furniture. A new layout will add a refreshing vibe to your house and make it look like a new one.

10. Change the Lighting

One way to transform your space is by changing the lighting in your apartment. You can switch out the light bulbs, and install color-changing LED light bulbs that will allow you to change the color of the lights, and the mood of your apartment, whenever you want..

You can vary the amount of light by adding floor lamps and table lamps throughout your apartment. For more overhead or wall light, you can add temporary stick-on battery-powered LED light fixtures. For more subtle light, you can add some twinkling fairy lights.

11. Striking Artwork & Statement Pieces

Eyeing for the best home decor trends 2022? Then you cannot miss out on adding an amazing artwork collection to your house.

For any small space, it is important to have something that sparks a conversation. It could be a vintage painting, a signature showpiece, or a nomadic style print. This is an excellent landlord-friendly hack to beautify your house.

12. Touch of Seasonality

An upcoming interior design trend in 2022 is to keep your house full of seasonal vibes. Add a wool rug during winters or style up with colorful flowers during spring- this touch of seasonality can make you excited about the changing seasons and also add a quick change to your house without much effort.

13. Inviting Place Settings

One of the key things for small space decor is to keep your house pleasant and appealing. Organize your house, keep it clean and curate a thoughtful place setting for an attractive look.

For example, you can add some fresh flowers to your dining room table. Or you can add a table cloth and napkins to create a cozier dinning area.

14. Add the Pantone Vibe

If you like to stay updated with the latest interior design trends of 2022, you must add the Pantone color of the year to your house. The color of the year is Very Peri, which is a mixed shade of blue, violet and red. Display this color in your walls, table decor, artwork, to add a chic vibe to your house.

Roundup of Rented Home Decor Trends 2022

We can expect 2022 to give us some major small space decor inspirations that our landlords will not really mind.

Even if you do not want to change your entire house, use some of the 2022 home decor trends and change the look of your house.

Go ahead, give these decor trends a try and add fresh life to your rented apartment.

]]> So you’ve moved into a brand new off campus housing and you want to know how to decorate your apartment on a budget. Rent isn’t cheap by any means and you want to find affordable furniture if your off campus housing is unfurnished. However, you don’t need to spend a ton to get your student housing looking amazing. Read on for some tips on making yourself at home and adding your unique taste to your off-campus housing!

Getting started

Before you step out and spend those precious dollars, here are a few things to keep in mind. You will need to shell out some cash to decorate your off-campus housing. Hence, be sure that what you buy is going to fit your student apartment.

Budget

It can be easy to get carried away while decorating your off campus housing. Dorms and campus housing may have restrictions when it comes to decoration or modifications. However, off campus housing is a lot more flexible. Be sure that you set a budget and do not spend on unnecessary items or furniture. Remember you can always buy something later if you still feel that you really want it.

Available space

Do you really need that 50 inch T.V. or that huge fluffy rug? Don’t get carried away when you shop. And be realistic. Make sure what you buy will actually fit in your off campus housing, and is practical. There is still a lot you can do if you are working with a small space. However, be sure you don’t overdo it and end up with a cluttered apartment.

Pets

Having a pet is a commitment that you need to take seriously. Thus, be sure that you have your furry friend in mind while decorating and furnishing your off campus housing. Make sure that you are creating an environment that is safe and comfortable for your pet. Also, keep in mind that you need a place for your pet to sleep and relieve themselves! Shedding can also be something that you need to plan for, to avoid a mess. Fragile decorations on open shelves can also be a disaster. Ask yourself if the things you are buying are going to be a problem.

Aesthetic

It is easy to see something pretty and spring for it in the spur of the moment. However, do consider if what you are buying will actually fit your aesthetic and that of your off campus housing. The last thing that you want, is to buy something and then realize that you really don’t like it that much. Hence, be sure to set the vibe you want to go for!

Practicality and mindfulness

While you may really think that fancy ceramic shot glass set will really up your house party game, hold on. When you’re on a budget, be careful of your temptation to spring for fancier and more pricey versions of things. Don’t fall for marketing and end up going beyond your means! However, this doesn’t mean you should always buy cheaper and low-quality items. Being mindful is the key here. Before buying something, ask yourself. Is this going to add value to me or my space?

Buying furniture

If you are moving into an unfurnished off campus housing, then the first thing you’ll need to buy is furniture. Get started with a list of things you will need to furnish your off campus apartment. Make sure that you are getting everything and not less, and definitely not more. Again, remember, that mahogany shelf may look great in the corner, but do you really need it?

What to get?

Let’s start off with the essentials. You will need a bed and mattress to start off. A solid and comfortable study desk and chair is also a good investment. Beyond this, it really depends on the size and nature of your off campus housing. If you are living in a studio apartment, you may want to keep it limited and only get a small couch or coffee table. However, if you are moving into a bigger space and sharing it, you may want to get a larger couch and table for your common spaces.

Where to get it?

For furniture, you may want to check out IKEA. This basically your one-stop shop for all your furniture needs! Other than this, you can visit local stores and look for deals as well. Buying used furniture online is also a great option. You can often find great deals on used furniture that can save you a decent buck. Remember, these are things that you will use regularly and need to last you a while. Hence, take your time and do your share of window shopping.

Buying applicances and decoration

After you’ve got yourself the essentials with a full set of furniture, appliances and accessories are next. This is the place where you should exercise your fair share of caution. It can be easy to splurge too much on these items. Be sure you are getting what you need, and that it adds value to your space and life.

What to get?

Again, this can widely vary depending on your lifestyle and the size of your off campus housing. If you are going to be cooking at home, a fridge and stove are going to be essential. Beyond this, you can get a microwave, blender or oven depending on the kind of cooking you do. If you are going to be eating out or in your college mess, you may want to skip this all. Ask yourself if you need a television and if you and your flat mates are actually going to using it. When it comes to decoration, keep all the earlier tips in mind, and consult with your roommates if you have any.

Where to get it?

You can look to Walmart or Bestbuy to find appliances and other decorations. They usually have a large selection and decent prices. You can also look to Amazon and easily find great deals online as well. Try Etsy for handmade and more personalized decor and art. Again, be sure to check with local stores for deals.

]]> When you share an apartment with someone, you don’t always agree on everything. Maybe you like minimalist decor, while they’re a hoarder. Differences in home decor preferences can be really annoying. But coliving means you have to accept your differences and try to find common ground. But that doesn’t mean you have to be unhappy with your apartment. Changing up your home decor can be great for your mental health.

But in order to revamp your home decor, you need to declutter first. I’m sure you’ve seen enough Marie Kondo videos to know what I’m talking about. So how do you do it? How do you declutter when you share an apartment with someone? You can’t just get up one day and throw away half the stuff in your house because you want to take your home decor to the next level. You and your roommate need to be in this together- that’s what coliving is about.

This is why today, we’re going to show you how to declutter when you share an apartment with another person!

See also: Why Natural Sunlight Is Apartment Decor & Therapy In One

Share an apartment, Share the burden

When you’re coliving with someone, you need to divide up chores evenly, it’s only fair. You can’t share an apartment with someone and not do any of the work (and vice versa). So if you’re dividing up everything else, then why should decluttering be only one’s person’s job? Get your roommate with you one weekend and just start sorting through all your stuff.

Decluttering can be really difficult for some people, so having another person to do it with is actually a great idea. Pick one day where you declutter your part of the house and another day for your roommates. You can sort through your common items one afternoon, it probably won’t take a lot of time.
