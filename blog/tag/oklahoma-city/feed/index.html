How transit-friendly is Oklahoma City?

One thing you should know about Oklahoma City is that most people drive around here. It was once called the least walkable city in America. While things are better now, it’s best to have your own car if you’re going to be living here. But of course, there are some other great modes of transportation here as well. Let’s take a look!

1. Walkability

While things are improving around here in terms of walkability, the walk score of Oklahoma City is still 34. This means that for most of your day-to-day errands, you’re going to have to drive. But the good news is that the city is becoming more and more walker-friendly by the day!

2. Roads/traffic

While Oklahoma City is pretty car-reliant, driving around here is surprisingly pleasant. In fact, Oklahoma City is the sixth most drivable city in the United States. Additionally, the city is positioned right at the crossroads of I-35, I-40, and I-44, making it extremely accessible for everyone.

3. Taxis

Taxi services are available in Oklahoma City, especially around the airport. Of course, Uber and Lyft are always at your disposal. Another option you have here is getting a rental car if you’re in town for a few days.

4. Public transportation

The Central Oklahoma Transportation and Parking Authority (COTPA) or EMBARK are responsible for public transportation in Oklahoma City. They have plenty of options for you such as EMBARK Ferry Transit, EMBARK Bike Share (Spokies), OKC Streetcar, and EMBARK Parking. You won’t have trouble finding your way around town with all these services at your disposal!

5. Airports in Oklahoma City

There are three airports in and around Oklahoma City:

  • Will Rogers World Airport
  • Clarence E. Page Airport
  • Wiley Post Airport
