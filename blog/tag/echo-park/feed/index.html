Getting to know Echo Park

If you’re a film buff (and you have to be if you’re living in LA), you’re going to love this one. Echo Park is significant to the early Hollywood silent films. Then known as Edendale (now split into Echo Park, Los Feliz and Silver Lake), was where the first silent film studios were set up. In fact, Charlie Chaplin used to live in Echo Park!

Since then, Echo Park has been the backdrop of the counterculture movement in LA and a hub for scholars, writers and artists from the world around. Today, the trendiest cafes, bars, shops are all here. And with the quintessential LA Mediterranean climate along with a beautiful Lake, Echo Park is one of the best neighborhoods in LA to live in. How transit-friendly is Echo Park?

1. Walkability

Echo Park is one of the most walkable neighborhoods in LA. Most areas in Echo Park have a walk score of 84 on an average. That means, you don’t really need a car for most of your day-to-day errands. However, you should remember that other parts of LA are not known for being particularly walker-friendly, and most people do need a car to survive in LA. Remember to factor that in when you’re making a choice.

2. Roads/traffic

It’s no secret that traffic in LA is pretty bad. In fact, it’s one of the most congested cities to drive in. Echo Park is near the 101, the 2, and the 5 freeways. So, while driving in Echo Park itself is quite a pleasant experience, it’s still part of LA. So, be prepared for spending some time stuck in traffic on the regular.

3. Taxis

If you want to avail taxi services in Echo Park, LA City Cab has got you covered. Whether you want to go to the airport or just for a night out, LA City Cab is a reliable and reasonably priced option. Of course, Uber and Lyft are always just a click away!

4. Public transportation

If you’re worried about traveling in and out of Echo Park, there’s no need! The Neighborhood is quite public-transport friendly. Whether you want to travel by bus, train, or subway, Echo Park is extremely accessible! Here’s a helpful guide when traveling to Echo Park:

  • Bus: look for route number 10/48, 2, 4, 92.
  • DASH Train: take the San Bernardino Line
  • Subway: take B Line (Red)
