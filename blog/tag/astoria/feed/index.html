]]> The Astoria neighborhood has tons to offer for the price. At first glance, it might seem like a quiet, middle class neighborhood. And while that’s part of Astoria’s awesome personality, don’t let it fool you into thinking this place is boring. Delicious microbreweries, astounding Greek food, beautiful parks, and more await you in this incredibly diverse neighborhood. With so many designated bike paths, getting around town is easy if you don’t have a car. Here’s Roomi’s Astoria neighborhood review for you!

Astoria neighborhood review: Greek food lover and microbrewery enthusiast paradise

Can’t get enough Greek food and you’ve never met a microbrewery you didn’t like? Then the Astoria neighborhood offers more happiness in a few city blocks than all of paradise combined. Beer gardens and little mom-and-pop Greek restaurants are plentiful. Don’t have a specific destination in mind? Just taking a stroll through your own neighborhood will reveal all manner of delights.

For Greek food, head on over to Tavern Kyclades. It isn’t just a top destination for Greek food within Astoria neighborhood. It ranks as one of the absolute best Greek restaurants in all of New York City. The interior has art based on the famous island chain in Greece. And it’s complete with the distinctive blue and white homes and idealistic beaches.

For 25 microbrews on tap and full kitchen service until 1 AM, try Sunswick 35/35. The beer covers a range of options, from hoppy to mellow so everyone can find something they like. Hearty pub fare is on the menu. But so are more unusual options like wild mushroom fritters for the more explorative amongst you. They’ve also got trivia and bingo! Which is always a fun way to introduce yourself to the locals when you first arrive.

Accessible and interesting art galleries and museums

Beyond its culinary experience, the Astoria neighborhood is a really educational place. Your first stop should be the Socrates Sculpture Park. Not only are there a bunch of outdoor sculptures for you to enjoy, but there are even art classes. They’re divided into adult, teen, and kids courses. So whether you go with friends or family, you’ll have something you can enjoy. Music, festivals, farmers markets, and yoga are also available there.

Visit the Museum of the Moving Image in the Astoria neighborhood. It teaches everyone about the different aspects of movies, film, television, and other digital media. They educate people about how digital media is made and how it affects our society. Their exhibitions change regularly as they examine different people, shows, and technologies.

The Kaufman Arts District in the Astoria neighborhood is very supportive of emerging artists. The RaR Bar has showcased a lot of previously undiscovered performers over the last nine years. Enjoy discovering new music? Then living in Astoria will give you access to a steady stream of up-and-coming talent.
