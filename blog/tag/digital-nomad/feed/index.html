]]> Ever since the pandemic hit, more people have ditched their full-time jobs to work from home. But after a point, even WFH-ing becomes boring, right? And that’s why becoming a digital nomad could be a great step forward.

A digital nomad is any traveler who earns from the internet. Between 2018 to 2020, the digital nomad population in the US doubled up. And as the pandemic made people realize that work is not restricted to their offices, they’re choosing to make the most out of it! Want in? This blog will tell you how to become a digital nomad with some extra special tips and advice!

Related: How Do You Convince Your Boss to Let You Work Remotely?

Why Becoming a Digital Nomad is so Thrilling in 2022

Once upon a time, it was a fantasy to get to work and travel. With the modern internet facilities available today, living remotely is practical and fun. Becoming a digital nomad is super exciting but it also comes with its own challenges. Still on the fence? Here are some of the top reasons why becoming a digital nomad in 2022 could be an amazing move for you:

  1. There’s something new to explore every day! The chance to visit new places and try new things means no two days are ever the same.
  2. You will meet new people and also develop unique skills. You may even make lifelong connections and have some unforgettable experiences.
  3. As you explore the world and new people, you will grow as a person, become more adaptable, and adopt a positive view of the world in general.

Feeling pumped up? Now, let’s look at how you can achieve an amazing lifestyle as a digital nomad in 2022!

How to Become a Digital Nomad in 4 Steps

Let’s make it clear that there is no one roadmap to a successful digital nomad lifestyle. Becoming a digital nomad is a unique journey for everyone. Your personal skills and the country you move to will shape your route into this life. To help, here are 4 key steps to help you achieve a more comfortable journey towards your digital nomad lifestyle.

1. Join a community of digital nomads

Living in the era of Instagram and Facebook, you’ve probably already got some digital nomads in your network. The first step is to follow their YouTube channels and start making notes!

You can also find digital nomad communities on Facebook groups and Reddit. Join pages and groups that are specific to your preferred locations. This will give you some practical tips and advice to use in your journey. Online communities will really help in finding specific information- like where to live on a budget and where to find healthy, cheap food.

Related: Travel Books That Take A Virtual Journey Through United States

2. Get a location-independent job

Unless your boss allows you to work remotely, it’s time to find a new job! Working as a digital nomad, you will rely completely on the internet. With no fixed location or hours, you must develop the skill of self-discipline to stick to work. Having said that, you must find a job that won’t restrict you to one place. Here are some tips and ideas to help you start:

  • Identify your skills – Whether you’re a recent graduate or a working professional, working remotely while traveling can be so liberating. So, figure out the top skills that you can monetize online while becoming a digital nomad. Writing, planning, teaching, or managing – anything can come in handy. You can be a content creator, a virtual assistant, an online teacher, and so much more!
  • Start freelancing – A lot of work can be done as a freelancer. Whether you are a graphic designer, a photographer, or a content writer, freelancing is one of the best ways to earn online. Create your profile on freelance websites like Upwork or Fiverr. Building a good portfolio will help you get better-paying clients. That means more money to do cool things as you travel!
  • Create an independent business – If you love the thought of being your own boss, why not start your own online business? Whether you’re launching a dropshipping business or building a content agency, almost everything can be managed online. You could hire freelancers online from all over the world and scale up your business too.

Related: 6 Must-Have Skills For Individuals Seeking Remote Jobs In 2021

3. Plan the logistics of your nomadic lifestyle

Living remotely and working on your own hours all sounds great. But with so much flexibility, there are always chances for things to go wrong. Planning the logistics of a nomadic lifestyle can involve a variety of things:

  • Banking, taxes, and finances– When becoming a digital nomad, you must keep a tab on your finances and banking methods. Choose a good credit card company that can help you no matter where you are. When you are paying taxes and traveling between nations, you may have to hire a good accountant to avoid blunders.

Related: How to Manage Financial Risks as a Young Entrepreneur

  • Wifi, VPN, and mobile facilities– If you are working remotely while traveling, fast internet is a must. Whether you choose to live in an Airbnb in a remote area or visit a popular cafe, the wifi should be a primary concern. Get a good VPN provider so that you can access different networks irrespective of your actual location. Virtual mobile numbers are also very useful to stay connected.
  • Emergency backups and insurance– When you are visiting new places, you cannot ignore that things can go wrong once in a while. Travel insurance is very handy (and usually mandatory) when you’re traveling abroad. Get good health insurance that offers good security as a digital nomad. Do set up an emergency fund that can help you in any type of crisis, too.
  • Getting around and finding accommodation– Finally, research the places that you are moving to. Look up all the details about transportation networks and good accommodation. As a digital nomad, you will mainly rely on the public transportation system or rented vehicle companies. Wherever you choose to live, it should ideally be well-connected and include the amenities you need to live comfortably.

4. Pick your first destination and go!

When becoming a digital nomad, you must be careful about picking your locations. Don’t just close your eyes and point to a map! Whether you want to stay in your country or go to some exotic location, think realistically.

Start by making a list of all the locations that you want to visit. Find out the cost of living, transportation, and accommodation options. Don’t forget to make a note of the climate and seasons. If everything fits your plan and budget, go ahead and book your tickets before you change your mind!

]]> Recently, more and more people have begun to work from home or venture into freelancing as their primary source of income. In turn, the idea of being a digital nomad in today’s digital world is becoming a reality for many. And that means sampling life in other cities and countries! Who would have thought that working remotely while traveling is possible, huh?

Before we cover the best cities for digital nomads, make sure you know the basics you’ll need to make this awesome lifestyle work for you.

What you need if you’re working remotely while traveling

  • Reliable internet connection
  • Affordable cost of living
  • Good health care

Being a digital nomad, these three are non-negotiable! For one, it’d be challenging to work in an area with an unstable internet connection. Plus, a part of working as a digital nomad is knowing how to budget correctly, so make sure to stay at a decent and affordable place. Lastly, never skip on good health care! With all these considered, here are the 10 best cities for digital nomads.

10 Best Cities for Digital Nomads

1. Lisbon, Portugal

Thinking of going to Europe to live your life as a digital nomad? Lisbon is the place to be! With a relatively low cost and high quality of living, working remotely in Lisbon is ideal. Their beautiful beaches are within a short drive, plus public transport is easy! And when you close your laptop for the day, you’ll find the nightlife is as lively and vibrant as the city.

  • Internet speed: 26mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: $665.09
  • Visa: Schengen Visa of up to 90 days
  • Useful apps: Lisboa Cool

Related: Find Rooms For Rent In Lisbon, Portugal

2. Chiang Mai, Thailand

Known for its many co-working spaces and affordable accommodation, Chiang Mai is one of Asia’s best cities for digital nomads. Living in Thailand exposes you to mother nature, beautiful landscapes, and cultural and historic scenery! If you’re looking to practice meditation, work on your spirituality, and discover more about the sacred wisdom of Buddhism, Chiang Mai can give you that.

The place is perfect for solo travelers, as working remotely while traveling is convenient here. You get fast internet and enough cool coffee shops to work in.

  • Internet speed: 30mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: 536.90$
  • Visa: No visa required if staying for less than 30 days
  • Useful apps: Tuk

3. Tenerife, Spain

Island life for a digital nomad is possible in Tenerife. With a crowd of tourists and nomads, the community in Tenerife is constantly growing. It also has the best weather in Europe! Mostly sunny with a spring feel, balancing work and leisure on the island is easy. You can even get into watersports!

Accessing Wi-Fi around Tenerife is simple, with their streets and public areas offering free public Wi-Fi. There are also a lot of co-working spaces you could go to!

  • Internet speed: 21mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: [678.16$]
  • Visa: Schengen Visa of up to 90 days, Student Visa, and Temporary Residence Visa
  • Useful apps: Tenerife.buzz

4. Buenos Aires, Argentina

Called the “Paris of South America,” Buenos Aires has everything a digital nomad could ask for! Reliable internet connection, good food, buzzing nightlife, and even a convenient metro system, Buenos Aires is a fantastic city. Socializing is easy, and the food is excellent – what more do you need? Don’t forget the city’s minimal cost of living! Buenos Aires is definitely one of the best digital nomad destinations.

  • Internet speed: 26mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: [748.74$]
  • Visa: Tourist Visa of up to 90 days
  • Useful apps: Cómo Llego, Pedidos Ya

5. Madrid, Spain

With an average internet speed of 34mbps, working remotely while traveling around Madrid is easy! As a big city, Madrid has plenty of pueblos (towns) to live in, amazing food, and buzzing nightlife. More than that, as one of the best cities for digital nomads, Madrid has a friendly community. There are a lot of parks you can visit (or work in!) and you can go on a day trip to the city’s many landmarks when you have some spare time.

  • Internet speed: 34mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: 1,579.14$
  • Visa: Schengen Visa of up to 90 days and long-stay national visa
  • Useful apps: Citymapper, Glovo

Related: Find Rooms for Rent in Madrid

6. Austin, United States

Known as “Silicon Hills,” Austin is a duplicate of Silicon Valley with a countryside theme. Home to tech startups and hip neighborhoods, working as a digital nomad in the city is great, especially if you’re into music and art! There are many museums, live concerts, and bars with live performances all around Texas. Check out some of the best things to do in Austin while you’re at it!

  • Internet speed: 77mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: 2,195.85$
  • Visa: Tourist visa
  • Useful apps: CapMetro

7. Krakow, Poland

Living in Krakow, Poland, with an internet speed of 43mbps, is a dream for digital nomads. Not to mention, the city has one of the lower costs of living in Europe with an exceptional quality of life! (Plus, it’s extremely safe for solo female nomads!) You wouldn’t have to worry about work since there are WiFi hotspots scattered around the city and many cafes you can work from.

  • Internet speed: 42mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: $1,053.29
  • Visa: Schengen Visa of up to 90 days
  • Useful apps: Jakdojade, Pyszne.pl

8. Melbourne, Australia

Melbourne, Australia, has a relatively high cost of living paired with a high quality of life. It’s an eccentric city known for being one of the most livable cities in the world that is famous for its art and culture. Not only that, the public transport in Melbourne is highly convenient! They even have a free-of-charge tram route (35 City Circle) that covers most of the city’s attractions.

Melbourne’s also known for its great student atmosphere, which is great for digital nomads. Being surrounded by all that youthful productivity is beneficial for you too!

  • Internet speed: 52mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: $1,982.23
  • Visa: Tourist Visa for up to three to six months, Australian Working Holiday visa
  • Useful apps: Public Transport Victoria, TramTracker

9. Mexico City, Mexico

When working remotely while traveling in North America, Mexico City should be high on your list! The city is filled with mind-blowing food and epic nightlife! Taking a break from work and roaming the streets of Mexico is what being a nomad is all about.

  • Internet speed: 20mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: $950
  • Useful apps: Rappi, Mexico City Metro Map & Route Planner App

10. San Diego, CA

One of the best cities for digital nomads is San Diego in California. Although in its early days, the digital nomad’s community in the city is expanding. Working remotely in San Diego is made possible thanks to emerging co-working spaces and cozy coffee shops. During your downtime, turn to some natural spots in the city.

  • Internet speed: 77mbps
  • Average Cost of Living: $2,722
  • Useful apps: San Diego Offline Street Map
