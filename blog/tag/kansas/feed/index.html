How transit-friendly is Kansas City?

1. Buses in Kansas City

The Metro bus service is helpful to get around the urban core and most of the tourist destinations. The MAX (Metro Area Express) buses also help you easily get around downtown and other major spots. The standard fare is $1.50/trip with transfers available up to 2 hours. A day pass can also be purchased for $3 and is good for local service. If you are looking for rooms for rent in Kansas City you can check the availability of buses online.

2. Taxis in Kansas City

You can easily use taxis in Kansas and they are generally safe and inexpensive. However, you can find difficulties finding one in some places. Hence it is advisable to book one in advance. Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are also available. They can be great if you are looking for rooms for rent in Kansas City in an out-of-the-way area.

3. Streetcar in Kansas City

The streetcar in Kansas City is free but slow to ride. It runs from north to south and links Union Station with the popular River Market district. Streetcars run every 10-15 minutes from morning to evening, with extended services on weekends. Routes and timings can be found on their website.

4. Walking in Kansas City

Most areas of Downtown and the tourist areas are accessible on foot. If you live in one of these areas or are out exploring, you can easily make it around without transportation. The city is fairly easy and safe to explore and you can scout areas if you are looking for cheap rooms in Kansas City.

Cultural Activities in Kansas City

  • One Kansas City Place: The tallest building in the city and the state, and a popular attraction. Located downtown, the building walls are entirely black glass and the top has a light ring visible at night. You can find the best views of downtown and the city here!
  • Union Station: The second largest train station in the country and an Amtrak train stop. The Union Station houses a visitor center, theatres, restaurants, shops, and more. An architectural marvel and hub of activity.
  • U.S. National WWI Museum and Memorial: The country’s leading museum dedicated to documenting, interpreting, and understanding the Great War. Great for history buffs and a must-visit if you are looking for rooms for rent in Kansas City and are settling here.
