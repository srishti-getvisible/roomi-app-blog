Getting to know Jersey City

Jersey City is located on the peninsula known as Bergen Neck, with a waterfront on the east at the Hudson River and New York Bay and on the west at the Hackensack River and Newark Bay. Being one of the major port of entry for immigration to the U.S., as well as a major employment center, Jersey city is one of the most ethnically diverse cities in the world!

Jersey City is also home to the tallest buildings in New Jersey, including the Goldman Sachs tower, which tops out at 781 feet tall. Additionally, the major international ports like port Jersey and the International Matex-Tank Terminal has given way to redevelopment, making it one of the largest centers of banking and finance in the United States. This has led to the district and city being nicknamed Wall Street West too!

How transit friendly is Jersey City?

About 40% of Jersey City’s population prefer public transit, making it the second highest public transit friendly city after New York City and Washington, D.C.

1. Trains

  • Hudson-Bergen Light Rail is one of the most popular forms of transportation in the city. Of the 24 stations that connect its three terminus points, 13 are located in Jersey City.
  • PATH is a 24-hour rapid transit system with four stations in Jersey City.
  • Hoboken Terminal will help you get around the city’s northeast corner.

2. Ferries

  • NY Waterway ferries operate between Harborside, Paulus Hook Ferry Terminal, Liberty Harbor and Port Liberté to Manhattan at Battery Park City Ferry Terminal, Pier 11/Wall Street, and West Midtown Ferry Terminal.
  • Statue Cruises provides service to and between Ellis Island and Liberty Island
  • Liberty Water Taxi operates ferries between Liberty Landing Marina, Warren Street and the World Financial Center.

3. Buses

The Journal Square Transportation Center, Exchange Place and Hoboken Terminal are major origination/destination points for buses.

Additionally, a cheaper alternative to travel within the city, locally known as dollar vans are also available.

4. Cycling

The Hudson River Waterfront Walkway, Hackensack RiverWalk and the Grove Street Lanes are all bicycle friendly.

Historical Spots in Jersey City

  • Ellis Island Immigration Museum: History buffs can learn about people who immigrated from different parts of the world and entered America, from the late 1800s to the mid-1900s.
  • Liberty Science Center: Located by Liberty State Park, it’s an interactive museum that features various permanent exhibitions. Fascinating ones include the Touch Tunnel, an 80-foot (24.3-meter) dark path that will force you to use all of your senses except your eyes!
  • The Landmark Loew’s Jersey Theatre: Performance art lovers can head to this space for live concerts, theatre and other performing arts. Moreover, the Loew’s shows classic films throughout the year as well!

Shopping Centers in Jersey City

  • Another Man’s Treasure Vintage Store: You can find everything here, from 1920s lingerie to 1990s acid washed denim to jewelry, accessories, vinyl records and even luggage!
  • Newark Avenue Pedestrian Plaza: Whether you want to shop, eat and drink, or enjoy a night stroll, the Newark Avenue is the perfect place to go to. All thanks to it being closed to cars and being turned into a pedestrian mall in 2015!
