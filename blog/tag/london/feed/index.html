Outdoor Activities in London

1. Have a picnic in Hyde Park

This is one of the best things to do in London if you want to avoid from the crowds. Fun fact: King Henry VIII was known to have hunted wild boar and deer on these grounds during the 16th century.

Timings: Daily, from 5am – 12 midnight

2. Follow a London cycle route

If you’re having a hard time choosing places to visit in London, why not hop on a bicycle and explore the city on your own or on a guided tour? Choose from the many different options that let you take in the sights you’re most interested in.

Sports in London

1. Catch a game of cricket

Cricket’s a popular sport in England, so watching a match is one of the best things to do in London. Check out the upcoming fixtures at either of the city’s major cricket grounds—Lord’s or The Kia Oval.

The Kia Oval Website

2. Watch the annual Oxford-Cambridge Boat Race from the River Thames

Join the thousands of people every spring who watch the Oxford-Cambridge boat race by the banks of the River Thames. The two universities have been racing each other since 1829. This is one of the best things to do in London if you want to feel like a local.

Website

5. Witness the sheer brilliance of tennis legends

Home to the oldest tennis tournament in the world, London is the best place to enjoy this sport as a spectator. Expect some upgrades to the All England Club’s Centre Court as it turns 100 years old in 2022. Wow!

Museums in London

1. Appreciate art and artifacts at the British Museum.

If you’re on a budget, then you can visit the British Museum for free. The museum has nearly 100 rooms, so make sure you plan your visit ahead of time to avoid missing anything.

Timings: Daily from 10am – 5pm

2. See masterpieces up close at the National Gallery

Feast your eyes on Vincent Van Gogh’s “Sunflowers,” Claude Monet’s “Bathers at the La Grenouillère,” or Rembrandt’s self-portrait. Measures are in place for your safety, so you can enjoy your visit without care.

Timings:

  • Sunday – Thursday: 10am – 6pm
  • Friday: 10am – 9pm

Cultural Locations in London

1. Take a selfie at the Buckingham Palace gates

An iconic landmark, Buckingham Palace is definitely one of the best places to visit in London. Tours are available so you can visit staterooms and ooh and ahh at all the exquisite furniture and artwork. Plus, watch out for the changing of the guard every day at 11 am.

Website

9. Relive the old days of theater at Shakespeare’s Globe on Bankside.

Theater and literature buffs, this one’s for you. Seeing Shakespeare’s plays performed at his reconstructed Globe has to be one of the best things to do in London for bardolators!

Shopping Centers, Bars, & Restaurants in London

1. Check out the Portobello Road Market

If you’re looking for a cool souvenir or collectible, then you should head on over to Portobello Market in Notting Hill (yes, that Notting Hill). You might just score a beautiful piece of jewelry or artwork from one of its 1,000+ merchants. Definitely one of the best things to do in London, hands down.

Timings:

  • Monday – Wednesday: 9am – 6pm
  • Thursday: 9am – 1pm
  • Friday: 9am – 7pm (antique stalls)
  • Saturday: 9am – 7pm

Note: Shops and cafes remain open throughout the week.

Website

2. Drink with a touch of whimsy at Mr. Fogg’s House of Botanicals

This bar features the botanical library of Phileas Fogg, the protagonist in Jules Verne’s Around The World In 80 Days. With its Victorian horticulture theme, you’ll feel as if you’ve actually stepped into the novel’s timeline. For foodies and book lovers, this should make it onto your personal list of some of the best things to do in London.

Timings:

  • Monday: 4pm – 1am
  • Tuesday – Thursday: 5pm – 11:30pm
  • Friday: 3pm – 12am
  • Saturday: 12pm – 1am
  • Sunday: 3pm – 1am

3. Snap an IG Pic At Circolo Popolare

With its hanging trellises and delicious Italian food, this spot is the most queued-for restaurant in the city. It’s one of the best things to do in London if you want to enjoy an unbeatable meal.

Timings:

  • Monday – Wednesday: 11:45am – 3:15pm; 4:45pm – 10:45pm
  • Thursday: 11:45am – 3:15pm; 5pm – 10:45pm
  • Friday – Sunday: 11:45am-10:30pm

How transit friendly is London?

1. Public Transportation

The public transit system is excellent, but it can be costly, so take advantage of any available discounts. If you’re going with a group on an overground train, make sure to inquire for family tickets (not singles for everyone). Traveling during rush hour (7-9:30 a.m. and 5-7:30 p.m.) is best avoided since crowds are dense and ticket costs are significantly higher.

Avoiding the subway altogether if you simply need to travel around central London can save you money. For most routes, buses are significantly less expensive and virtually as speedy. Similarly, a bicycle rental system is also available. It costs £2 to start and then £2 per 30 minutes (the first 30 minutes of each journey are free).

Roomi Tip: Getting a Family & Friends Railcard (£30) saves a third on all adult journeys and 60% on kid’s travel.

2. Taxi

Taking a trip in one of London’s iconic black taxi taxis is thrilling; it’s a convenient and safe way to get around. However, black cabs come at a high cost. Likewise, a two-mile journey will set you back around £15. Uber is a popular mode of transportation in the city since it provides significantly lower prices.

A full day hunt for cheap rooms for rent in London is made easy with Uber, as they charge £3 to begin with, then £1.20 each mile after that. This typically works out to be significantly less expensive giving you the opportunity to have exciting conversations with the driver.

3. Car rentals

If you want to get around the city by car, Hertz, Sixt, and Enterprise are just a few of the rental companies available. Cultural activities in London

1. Visit the British Museum

The British Museum is enormous. You can spend a day getting lost in its galleries, checking out antiquities from ancient Rome, Greece, and Egypt. Moreover, explore relics from Easter Island, Japanese porcelain, and much, much more. Admission is free of charge and it’s open 7 days a week, so don’t miss one of the world’s greatest museums.

2. Visit London’s Smaller Galleries

Art lovers should head to major institutions like the Tate, but don’t neglect the city’s lesser-known galleries. Experimental contemporary work is always on show at the Whitechapel Gallery (77-82 Whitechapel High St). Similarly, the Esoterick specializes in Italian modern art (39A Canonbury Square). Likewise, White Cube also hosts radical new talent (25-26 Masons Yard).

3. Take a Cruise on the Thames

London grew up around the River Thames, and the river is the ideal vantage point to take vacation photos of waterfront attractions. Namely, the Houses of Parliament, the Tower of London, and Tower Bridge. Though City Cruises offers guided tours, you can also take river bus services using your Oyster Card. They run from Westminster Pier to Putney in the west and Woolwich in the east.

Living an affordable lifestyle in London

London is well-known for being one of Europe’s most pricey capitals. You can significantly save money on food and groceries at supermarkets such as Sainsbury’s, Morrisons, and Tesco. Likewise, sandwiches at Waitrose and Marks & Spencer are a touch more upscale, but they’ll still only cost around £2. If you want any electrical products, go to Tottenham Court Road, which is home to a number of electronics stores.
