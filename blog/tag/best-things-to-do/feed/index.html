]]> Moving to the “City of Roses”? Since being named this in 1888, the people of Portland continue to practice rose gardening now. This began with Leo Samuel – the first to plant and provide a pair of shears outside his garden for people to snip roses and take some for themselves.

Portland is the largest city in Oregon and the 26th most populated city in the USA. The name ‘Portland’ was decided with a flip of a coin by the city founders Asa Lovejoy and Francis Pettygrove, when they couldn’t agree on a name for their hometown. Pettygrove was born and raised in Portland, Maine and that’s how the capital of Oregon was named! Whether you are planning to move here or looking for a vacation, we’ll help you build an itinerary with the best things to do in Portland.

Explore the Outdoors in Portland

1.Washington Park

New to the city? Washington Park should be number one on your places to visit list. This flagship asset of Portland is a 410-acre park that is home to the Oregon Zoo, Portland’s Child Museum, International Rose Test Garden, Portland Japanese Garden, World Forestry Center, and Hoyt Arboretum. Washington Park enhance tourism in Portland, by attracting over 3.5 million visitors in a year!

Entry Costs:

  • No entry fee
  • $2 fee for parking which goes straight towards the maintenance of the Washington Park.

Timings:

  • 5 am – 10 pm
Website

Accomplish your daily step goal with Willamette River Walkway. This is a great spot to run, jog, bike or just sit down to watch the sunrise or sunset! The walkway sits in the heart of the city, so can fit in nicely around your schedule, as you find other places to visit in Portland!

Costs:

Free Timings:

Open all year round

Welcoming over 3,50,000 visitors a year, PIR’s biggest attraction is the collection of show cars on Wednesday night “Beaches Cruise-in”. This is your chance to put your car on the track with popular Late Night Drags, making this spot one of the best places to visit in Portland for adrenaline junkies.

Museums in Portland

7. Portland Art Museum

When you build your itinerary for tourism in Portland, don’t forget to add Portland Art Museum. The modestly-sized museum makes it a great option if you’re looking for a bite-sized chunk of history! Here you will find everything from modern art to priceless Greek and Roman articles.

When visiting the Oregon Historical Museum, look out for the permanent exhibit, “Experience Oregon”. Time travel with the stories of Oregon exhibited here in different mediums! The Doors fan? Don’t miss the “Portland Psychedelic”, a vast collection of autographs and local posters from the thriving music scene of Portland back in the 1960s.

9. Pittock Mansion

The famous Pittock Mansion is where the elite partied back in the 1900s. This Mansion-turned-museum is a French Renaissance sensation, which was built by Henry Pittock. He worked toward bringing in all the modern amenities like controlled central heating and elevators – amongst many others. Navigate the museum and expect premium and ahead-of-its-time interiors. One of the best things to do in Portland is to imagine yourself living the life of Sir Henry! The laid out cutlery from the ancient times is on show, where you can appreciate the attention to detail at which Henry Pittock commissioned this 16,000 sq. foot bungalow!

Costs:

Adults: $11

Kids (6 to 18): $8

Kids (5 and under): Free

Discounts available for Senior Citizens and Students

Timings:

10:00am – 5pm

Visit Website

Culture in Portland

10. International Rose Test Garden

Portlanders take the nickname “City of Roses” very seriously. An initiative taken over 133 years ago by Leo Samuels, it’s still going strong! The International Rose Test Garden since 1917 is home to over 600 rose varieties. The garden has about 10,000 individual rose bushes that bloom the pride of the city! Listing down some highlights of tourism in Portland? Jot this one down!

Costs: Free

Timings: 10am- 4pm

Website

11. Willamette Valley Wine Tour

One of the best things to do in Portland for those with a taste for fine wine, try our personal favorite, ‘Willamette Wine Tour’. Portland has a plethora of tours on offer, for visitors that want to get a little boozy!

Costs: Starting from $150

Timings: As per the tour you choose

Website

12. Portland’s Underground Donut Tour

One of the city slogans is “keeping it weird” and the underground donut tour claims to be doing just that! A donut trip across the city filled with good times and a sugar rush. Where else can you get an experience this sweet?

Costs:

Adult: $30

Kids (10 and under): $15

Timings: 2 hours duration

Must Do Outdoor Activities in Seattle

1. Discovery Park

This is the city’s largest park of 534 acres, located on the tranquil shores of the Puget Sound in Seattle’s Magnolia neighborhood. There are twelve miles of walking trails leading to coastal bluffs, rocky beaches, serene meadows, and wooded groves, but the 2.8-mile Loop Trail provides a best-hits list if you’re tight on time.

  • Timings: 4am–9:30pm

2. Olympic Sculpture Park

Olympic Sculpture Park proves a perfect downtown spot for strolling the grounds. Moreover, you can admire fascinating large-scale works on display within a picturesque nine-acre green space. It makes up for one of the best places to visit in Seattle!

  • Timings & Fees: Open and FREE to the public 365 days a year!

If you’re looking for cool things to do in Seattle, go to Woodland Park. It’s a multi functional park and leisure center located southwest of Green Lake, famous for its excellent zoo. Walking trails, multiple playfields, tennis courts, lawn bowling, horseshoe pits, and a skatepark are all available in Woodland Park.

Don’t miss out on the Sports Scene in Seattle

1. Catch the Seattle Mariners at T-Mobile Park

T-Mobile Park, the Mariners’ retro-style ballpark, is one of the best places to visit in the country. It’s also one of only a few stadiums with a retractable roof (which opens and closes in 10-20 minutes), allowing the Mariners to play on real grass without fear of being rained out.

Located in the city’s SoDo neighborhood, it is the home field for the Seattle Seahawks (National Football League), and the Seattle Sounders FC (Major League Soccer).
