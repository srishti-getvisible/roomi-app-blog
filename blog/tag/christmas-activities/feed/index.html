]]> Can you believe that Christmas is less than a week away? We’re full of the festive spirit, and hopefully, you are too! But we know how busy you must be with all the last-minute preparations. So, let’s get the party started with some festive drinks that will help keep out the winter chill. Whether you’re hosting a low-key Christmas celebration or a huge get-together, you can’t do it without these Christmas cocktails.

Our Favorite Christmas Cocktails

Challenge your inner bartender with these easy Christmas cocktail recipes. Each of these recipes is flavorful and perfect for spreading holiday cheer. Whether you have a sweet or sour tooth, this carefully curated list of Christmas drink recipes won’t disappoint you. So, let’s get the holiday spirits flowing!

Holly Jolly Christmas Citrus Cocktail

Is vodka your go-to drink? Do you love the taste of citrus? Allow us to present the first Christmas cocktail recipe of the season – the Holly Jolly Christmas Citrus Cocktail. This tangy, Christmassy cocktail will help you relax, unwind, and get in the holiday spirit. The drink is made with vodka and orange juice and topped with pomegranate seeds and a sprig of fresh mint. Sound good? Try it out for your upcoming Christmas Eve soiree.

Get the recipe here.

White Christmas Martini

Spread festive cheer with a round of these Christmas cocktails. They’re made with vanilla vodka, crème de cocoa, and white chocolate liqueur. And this recipe is so easy, it’s perfect for any surprise holiday guest. Finish up the drink with a coarse sugar garnish to make it sparkle. You’re surely going to earn some praise for your bartending skills with this super easy recipe.

Get the recipe here.

Grinch

If you love Dr. Seuss’s classic holiday character, the Grinch, you must try this Christmas cocktail! It got its name because of its distinct green color. It’s a simple melon liqueur sour, so if you love melon, this drink will put a smile on your face. Plus, you can stir it up in a jiffy, making it the perfect drink for a last-minute addition.

Get the recipe here.

Toasted Marshmallow Cocktail

If there’s one drink that can get the whole family to cozy up around the firepit, it’s this one. This sweet, boozy, adult treat is topped with toasted marshmallows. And it can double up as a dessert, too! A non-boozy hot chocolate with marshmallows will be just as much of a hit with the kids.

Get the recipe here.

Spiced Silver Bell

This tequila-based drink is flavored with winter spices and a touch of citrus, making it the perfect holiday drink. This cocktail is a smooth blend of lime juice and tequila with a dash of cranberry spice syrup. Top it off with ginger beer for a sparkling finish. This is an offbeat Christmas drink that everyone is sure to love.

Get the recipe here.

Holiday Mai Tai

If you’re looking for fruit-flavored Christmas cocktail recipes, then this one is a must-try. Toss together rum, pineapple juice, orange juice, and amaretto, and your cocktail is ready! It’s sure to bring sunshine to any holiday party, even if it’s snowing outside!

Get the recipe here.

Candy Cane Cocktail

This recipe takes a little bit of time to prepare, and you’ll need to make sure you have all the ingredients. But if you love to try new Christmas cocktail recipes, you can’t miss trying this one. Embrace your inner child and enjoy the taste of candy canes in an alcoholic beverage.

Get the recipe here.

Holiday Sangria

Red is the color of Christmas. And if you’re looking for a cocktail that’ll match your decorations, you’ve found one, all right! This dark red cocktail contains a mixture of red wine, brandy, sweet syrup, and cinnamon, offset by the refreshing taste of fresh fruits. This drink is full of depth and flavor and is perfect for warming you up on cold winter nights.

Get the recipe here.

Winter Snowflake Cocktail

To make this cocktail, mix Bailey’s Irish Cream with a light cinnamon flavor. It’s like a milkshake or a pudding, so it can double up as a dessert, too. Just top it with some whipped cream and finish off with a sprinkling of freshly ground cinnamon.

Get the recipe here

Cranberry Mimosa

It’s one of the easiest cocktails to make, but that doesn’t mean it tastes any less good! For cranberry mimosas, all you need are fizzy champagne and tart cranberries. This drink will certainly add some sparkle to your Christmas Day!

Get the recipe here

Mrs. Claus Cocktail

This Christmas, let’s celebrate and cheer for Mrs. Claus, too, by preparing this classic Christmas cocktail. This delicious mix of peppermint schnapps and eggnog is not your average Christmas drink. It’s a tribute to the woman behind the man with the sleigh. Just make sure you’ve made enough, because your guests are sure to ask for a top up!

Get the recipe here.

Hot Toddy

As a child, you might have had a hot toddy when you were ill with the flu. But this drink is definitely not for children! It’s a soothing mixture of honey, lemon, and whiskey… Basically, the alcoholic version of ‘curling up under a warm blanket’.

Get the recipe here.

There’s nothing better for getting you in the holiday mood than Christmas drinks! Try these classic Christmas cocktail recipes to discover drinks that are tasty, flavorful, and full of Christmas cheer. And for even more ideas, check out our list of last year’s favorites.

]]> The holidays are here! That means parties, fun, parties, family… did we mention parties? They’re undeniably one of the best parts of the festive season. It’s a great opportunity to connect with friends and family and get into the holiday spirit. That being said, planning a holiday party that runs smoothly can be stressful. This year, you don’t have to fret, because Roomi’s here to help! Stay tuned, as we list out some holiday party ideas and tips to help you make the most of this festive season.

Holiday Party Tips

1. What’s on the menu?

When planning your menu, remember to mix things up. You can include a variety of dishes, drinks, desserts, and bite-sized snacks.

Plan your menu according to the estimated number of guests, but leave room for more just in case. Use an online menu maker tool to design beautiful menu cards to let guests know what they’re eating.

2. Create your guest list and choose a date that works

Do you want to stick to close family members or will you invite all your friends, neighbors, their pets and the local line-dancing society?! Draft up your guest list early on and liaise with key guests to choose the best date.

3. Send out invites early

Once you’ve set a date for the party, you’ll need to send out invites. It’s advisable to send them out a few weeks – or even a month – before the due date. Mail them in the post, old-school style, or send out online invitations.

4. Venue

Selecting a venue depends on the number of people you’re inviting. If the party can’t be hosted at home, consider booking a bigger venue. If this is the case, then book the venue early to avoid anything spoiling your plans.

5. Choose your decor

Decorating the venue is a sure-fire way to capture the spirit of Christmas at your party. The kind of decorations you’ll use largely depends on the theme you’ve chosen. Let’s say you’re going for a classic dinner party. You’ll need decorations like candle holders, or even flowers. We’ll get into great theme ideas shortly! For your decorations, you can get a little holiday party inspiration from a movie or a local craft store.

6. Curate a Christmas playlist

Nothing like good music to give some holiday party inspiration, and stir up the festive mood at your party! Make a playlist of fun and popular holiday songs —both old and new. Use platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube to create a festive playlist.

7. Choose a theme

A great theme sets the atmosphere and tone for your party. It also determines the decorations, lighting, and color scheme. Here are some themes you can try:

  • Classic dinner party
  • Pajama party
  • Ugly Christmas Sweater
  • Secret Santa
  • Tree Trimming
  • Winter Wonderland

8. Plan parting gifts to give to your guests after the party

Don’t let your guests go home empty-handed. Include cool parting gifts in your budget while planning your party. You can buy to-go boxes for guests to take home yummy homemade desserts and treats.

Holiday Get Together Ideas

It’s okay if you’re stumped on ideas for your party. Here are some fun holiday party ideas you can try to make that day memorable:

1. Fun games

When choosing games, consider your guest list. How many people are coming and what mix of personalities can you expect? This will help you plan games and even prepare some prizes to spice things up.

  1. Gift wrap game: This could be an individual or team game. Test your guests to see who can wrap a gift the fastest – with only one hand!
  2. Holiday movie trivia: This is a fun holiday get-together idea. Have your guests answer questions on classic and modern Christmas films.
  3. 2 Truths & a Lie: In turns, let guests give 3 statements, and have the others try to spot the lie.
  4. Jingle Bell Toss: See which of your guests has the best aim. Hot-glue cups to a surface, and toss jingle bells in them to score a point.
  5. Christmas Limbo: Find out how low your guests can go in a Christmas-themed limbo challenge. You can use a candy cane or a Christmas scarf to set the bar.
  6. Charades: Make your party more entertaining by having your guests act out their favorite holiday movies.

Remember to choose the ones that will suit the kind of guests you’re inviting!

2. Christmas Karaoke

Another great holiday get-together idea is having people sing along to Christmas songs and carols. Sing songs like Feliz Navidad, Deck the Halls, Jingle Bell Rock, etc. It’s time for Christmas Carol-oke!

3. Watch holiday movies

Watching holiday movies is one of the most fun holiday party ideas ever! Offer your guests a great cinematic experience by sharing popcorn, sweets, or drinks.

4. Cookies and tree decoration

A Christmas tree decorating party is one of the most traditional Christmas parties there is. Make provisions for colorful Christmas ornaments for your guests to place on the tree.

Buy cookie decorations beforehand and let guests try their hand in decorating delicious cookies. Everyone can then swap their masterpieces after.

5. Pictures!

Picture taking is one of the best holiday party ideas because you get to save the fun party moments forever. You could even set up a quaint homemade photo booth to let your guests take beautiful holiday photos.

]]> It’s the most wonderful time of the year! Nowhere is better than New York City around Christmastime; from Macy’s window shopping to admiring the tree at Rockefeller Center, there’s no denying that NYC is the most magical place to be during Christmas. Lockdown or not, Christmas still came along. And so did the snow. It was solemn, maybe a little lonely. But we weren’t all alone.

With the pandemic still weighing heavily on our shoulders, there’s been much speculation as to whether or not NYC Christmas festivities will still be happening this year. The answer is yes, they will be happening! There will be new health and safety precautions at the traditional Christmas exhibits in order to keep visitors safe, and it’s a bonus that most activities are already held outdoors.

If you’re new to the city or are visiting and are looking for some Christmastime magic, here are 5 festive Christmas activities to do in New York City during December!

1. The Christmas Tree at Rockefeller Center

If you’re visiting NYC during Christmastime, Rockefeller Center should be number one on your must-see list. This year the infamous Rockefeller Center Christmas tree has traveled all the way from Oneonta, NY. Tourists and New Yorkers alike flock each year to see the marvelous tree, which stands at 75 feet tall!

2. Window Shopping at Herald Square

Every Christmas, large department stores such as Macy’s put on a spectacular holiday display in their store windows at Herald Square. Despite the pandemic, this holiday tradition is still on for this year.

This year’s windows will be dedicated to first responders and essential workers in order to pay tribute to those who have helped us through 2020. Since 2020 has been a tumultuous year, Macy’s windows at Herald Square will be a special tribute to the individuals who have made this year hopeful.

3. The Winter Village at Bryant Park

Bryant Park festivities are back this year in full swing! The Winter Village at Bryant Park consists of 60 different food and shopping spots. You’ll be sure to find numerous homemade gifts and trinkets for the perfect Christmas stocking stuffer.

After enjoying a hot chocolate at one of the many food kiosks, head over to the 17,000-foot ice skating rink located at the Park and enjoy a classic winter tradition. There’s something for everyone at the Winter Village!

4. The New York Botanical Garden

This Christmas tradition is brand new this year! This exhibit is outside and COVID safe. On 14 different evenings this holiday season, the The New York Botanical Gardens will be illuminated with spectacular LED lights. Other seasonal activities will be happening at the exhibit such as dance performances and ice carving. Get your tickets ahead of time so you don’t miss the festivities!

Get your tickets here!

5. LuminoCity Festival

If you’re taking a trip to see the Rockefeller Christmas tree, be sure to stop at the LuminoCity Festival! This festival is only a few miles from Rockefeller Center and is a new tradition that began last year.

This exhibit consists of numerous light and art sculptures for an immersive 45-minute experience. The exhibits this year include Mysterious Forest, Dangerous Dunes, Forgotten Ruins, Hidden Land of Hria, and Mystical Moon Land. You’ll be immediately transported into a magical dimension at the LuminoCity Festival!
