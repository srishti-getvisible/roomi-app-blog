How transit friendly is Hartford?

While most people prefer to have their own ride, you can get around Hartford even without owning a car. Public transit is used by commuters frequently, with a few preferring to walk or bike to their destination. You’ll have plenty of ways to explore the city.

1. Walkability

Hartford has decent walkability with a walk score of 71, with the most walkable neighborhoods being Downtown Hartford, Frog Hollow, and South Green. If you like taking an evening stroll around the neighborhood, try snagging rooms for rent in these Hartford neighborhoods!

2. Roads/traffic

On average, a one-way commute in Hartford takes about 23 minutes, which is much shorter than the national average. Most people prefer to drive around the city, but plenty of people also opt for public transportation to get to and from work. Don’t prefer either? You can always hitch a ride via carpooling options!

3. Taxis

Apart from Uber and Lyft, there are various taxi services in the city, with a few companies offering dedicated services for rides to and from the airport. Typically, the base fare for taxi services is $2.60, with an additional $2.70 per mile and a waiting fee of $0.50 per minute.

4. Public transportation

Hartford residents have plenty of public transportation options, whether they want to get around the city or travel outside. CT Transit provides several bus routes throughout the city, and you can get two-hour tickets or all-day passes. The CTFastrak is a rapid bus transportation system that operates on bus-only roads and connects Hartford to New Britain, making several stops along the way. Want to get around Downtown? Catch a ride for free on the Hartford Dash Shuttle that operates every 15-minutes in the downtown area during the weekdays.

The New Haven-Hartford-Springfield Rail Program makes it easier for residents to get to New Haven or Springfield. You can also hop on the Amtrak or CTrail, from Hartford’s Union Station to get to Boston or New York.

5. Airports

Located near Windsor Locks, the Bradley International Airport is the closes airport near the city. You can cover the 15-mile distance by driving along with the 1-91 or hop on the CT Transit Bus 30.

6. Cycling

Currently, the city aims to connect a bicycle network throughout the city to help make the roads safer for cyclists. The BiCi Co is a widely used bike-sharing program in the city to rent bikes, helmets, and more. Try it out at reasonable prices or become a member.
