]]> If it’s one thing you learn when you visit NYC, or have been living here is that there are so many things that you can experience here that you simply can’t anywhere else in the world. A pizza place may say they have NYC-style pizza, but the truth is that nothing beats an authentic NYC pizza. And the list of NYC tourist locations is of course, endless! So we’re going to take a look at just a few tourist attractions in NYC.

1. Head to Broadway when you visit NYC, or live here!

Nothing beats a true Broadway show when you visit NYC. And if you’re a New Yorker, it’s easy to take this NYC tourist location for granted. But be sure to check in often to see what’s showing. Broadway has seen some of the world’s biggest stars pass through it. This includes Nicole Kidman, Hugh Jackman, and Daniel Radcliffe, so you never know who you’re going to get a chance to see live.

2. Go see the Statue of Liberty

Overlooking NYC from her post on Liberty Island is the Statue of Liberty. She’s a constant reminder for those who visit NYC, or have been living here, of the American way of life. If you haven’t paid the tourist attraction in NYC, Statue of Liberty a visit yet, be sure to do so as it’s certainly something that you can’t find anywhere else.

3. Visit NYC for Central Park

The NYC tourist location is more than just a green place where you can go on a picnic. You can find a number of activities held throughout the year. Which includes fireworks displays, ice skating, concerts, and sporting events. No matter how long you’ve been living in NYC or how often you visit NYC, there’s always something new and fun to do in Central Park.

4. Take a stroll in Greenwich Village

Known by New Yorkers simply as “the Village,” this neighborhood has meant a lot of things to a lot of people throughout the years and has become one of the popular tourist attractions in NYC. In the 1960’s it was known for being the birthplace of counterculture movements, and now it is known for being the cradle of the LGBT movement. It’s also home to two NYC colleges, the New School and New York University (NYU). So now you know where to go when you visit NYC!

5. Spend a day at the Metropolitan Art Museum

Most often referred to as “the Met,” this is one of the NYC tourist locations that was first established back in 1870 and has since housed artwork from around the world going back over 5,000 years. Their exhibits can be found in three distinct locations and as they’re always bringing in new exhibits, there’s always something new to see when you visit NYC. If you’re living in NYC, getting a membership will let you in for free as often as you want.

6. Go up the Empire State Building

Don’t worry. We didn’t forget this one; we were just saving one of the most popular NYC tourist locations for last. After all, it is one of the 7 wonders of the world, and certainly one of the first things that comes to mind when you visit NYC. Host to mostly office spaces, the top 16 levels are the Art Deco towers. So if you’re living in NYC and haven’t gone up to the observatory at the top of the Empire State Building by now, what are you waiting for?

]]> Races in NYC don’t have to be full blown marathons that require you to train for months at the exclusion of your social life and sleep. You don’t have to get up at crack-o-dawn AM just to run laps in Central Park until you drop to have a good time on a 5 kilometer (5K) run. Additionally, fun races in NYC are a great way to practice fitness in NYC and do some light training with a roommate or grab some mutual friends, and just have a goofy good time participating in a beginner event.

Races in NYC can also be walked if you don’t want to run. Still, these events are 5K long, so you still need to know your fitness level and do an appropriate amount of training. And consult an expert to figure out how much training that is.

1. The Color Run

Pretty much exactly what it sounds like. The Color Run rules are pretty simple. So at the start, wear white. And at the finish line, you should look like you were practicing gymnastics in an art supply store. People in the race and on the sidelines are just throwing colored powders left and right. It’s a rainbow, prismatic free-for-all, making it one of the most fun races in NYC. Also called “The Happiest 5K On The Planet,” there is a very strong unicorn theme at this event. You can get your pictures taken with giant unicorns, which makes for a fun shareable memory. Over 6 million people have done this race, so it’s tried and tested as one of the best fun fitness races in NYC.

2. Cupids Undie Run

Yup, races in NYC can get absurd! Run in your underwear. A great choice when NYC is really hot. While some people literally prance about in their skivvies, other people get into costumes, and it gets to be a lot of fun. Moreover, the proceeds benefit the Children’s Tumor Foundation to help neurofibromatosis, a genetic disorder that causes tumors to grow on nerves throughout the body. So have a blast and take heart knowing your racing fees went to a good cause.

3. Jingle Bell Walk/Run

Support the Arthritis Foundation in one of the more cheerful, laid back races in NYC. And you get to deck yourself out in a holiday-inspired costume. Think elves, reindeer, Santa, and presents. Tie jingle bells to your running shoes, and you’re off. In brief, a super fun event with a festive atmosphere, this is a must-have on the list for any group looking for an event that checks the competitive attitude at the starting line.

4. New York Hot Dog Challenge

No need to double-back for these races in NYC, you read that right. New York City combines a friendly race with a hot dog eating contest. At the same time! Also, people like to dress as their favorite sack of mystery meat as they cruise down the road. A 2.5 mile run through New York’s Central Park, Midtown, and Times Square is accompanied by 10 eagerly awaiting hot dog carts. During the race, contestants have to eat their way to fame, glory, and possibly one seriously upset stomach. Even if this one isn’t for you, you might be able to encourage your roommate to do it and enjoy the show. Or, uh . . . be supportive on race day. Yeah, that’s it.

In conclusion, races in NYC don’t have to be super competitive events that require months of training. And you can have a perfectly good time at one of these events. So grab your roommate, recruit some pals, and enjoy the best fun fitness races in NYC.
