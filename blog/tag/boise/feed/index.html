The county seat of Ada County, Boise, is the capital and the most populous city in Idaho State. According to the Idaho State Historical Society – Reference Series, during the early 1800s an exploration party trekking through the dry, rough landscape, reached an overlook with a view of the Boise River. As claimed in the story, a French-speaking guide impressed by the sight of the verdurous river, yelled, “Les bois! Les bois!” (“The woods! The woods!”)—and the name stuck.

Getting to know Boise, ID

The city offers an abundance of everything, from history and culture to modern entertainment and outdoor recreational activities. The reason for its growing expansion — an influx of people.

July is the hottest month of the year with an average high temperature of 91.6°, making it one of the hottest months in Idaho. Pleasant months of the year are September, June, and May. 5-months of the year receive significant snowfall. December is the snowiest month, with 6.4 inches of snow.

Top neighborhoods in Boise, ID

  • Downtown Boise: is the heart and cultural center of the new energy constantly transforming the city. New apartments blocks appear every block downtown. It’s a walkable location for convenience, recreational activity, entertainment, or leisure.
  • North End Boise: is 5-minutes away from downtown and a pricey, vintage area. Here homes greatly vary from condos, apartments, infill to newer homes, with historic mansions and hilltop custom homes too. Most homes are from the 1900s, yet there’s a style for everyone in NE.
  • North West Boise: is farther out and takes approximately 20 to 55 minutes to travel downtown. NW has easy access to outdoor areas — the river, bike trails, and the mountains. How transit-friendly is Boise?

As the city expands and its popularity grows, the traffic has increased considerably. The city somewhat lacks convenient, pocket-friendly public transit options. However, it’s an exceptionally walk and bike-friendly city.

1. Walkability & bike-ability

The Downtown area has pathways and bikeways all over. With a walk score of 88 out of 100 and 99 for bike-ability, the city certainly promotes sustainable commuting options!

2. Roads/traffic

Over the years traffic has increased incredibly. Morning rush hour (7:00.am to 8:30.am) is heavy all the way through. More often than not, the traffic out-flow is pretty hectic in comparison to the return traffic in-flow. In Idaho News, the city ranked 16 in traffic congestion levels. Hear from a local Boisean in the video below to learn more.

3. Taxis

Taxis are available on call-in. Any local taxi service will have local drivers familiar with shortcuts and sidelines during rush hours. A few taxi services available noted for your ease.

  • Boise City Taxi – (208) 377-3333
  • Boise Airport Taxi – (208) 795-7777
  • Lyft
  • Uber

4. Public Transportation

The city Convention & Visitor Bureau lists all transit options available online. You can rent bikes and scooters, find bus services in your area, and source phone numbers for taxis and rental services too.

5. Airport

3-miles south of downtown Boise, the Boise Airport is a commercial and general aviation airport and the busiest in Idaho.
