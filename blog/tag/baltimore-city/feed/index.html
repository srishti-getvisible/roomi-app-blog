Getting to know Baltimore

It is the world-famous Inner Harbor, award-winning restaurants, historic neighborhoods, and internationally recognized cultural institutions that make Baltimore an unforgettable destination.

Established in 1729, Baltimore has played a key role in American history, first serving as a hub for trade to the Caribbean and then as a convergence point for the Continental Congress when Philadelphia was threatened. Thus, leading to the city’s rich history culture and a favorite tourist spot.

Francis Scott Key, a renowned lawyer and poet, wrote his famous “Defence of Fort McHenry,” as a result of the British attacks on Baltimore. The poem later became “The Star-Spangled Banner.” Other notable Baltimore-born authors include F. Scott Fitzgerald, Frederick Douglass, and John Dos Passos.

Top neighborhoods in Baltimore

  • MOUNT WASHINGTON – Mount Washington is located in the northwest part of Baltimore and is surrounded by large, open spaces such as Luckman Park and the large laying fields. The neighborhood offers a range of row homes, condominiums, apartments, and rental properties. In the eastern parts, there is Mount Washington Village, which features galleries, salons, restaurants, and boutiques.
  • CHARLES VILLAGE – Charles Village is known as one of the most prestigious row-house neighborhoods in Baltimore. In addition to the 16-acre Wyman Park Dell, a popular landmark of Baltimore, you can also find the Baltimore Museum of Art and the Johns Hopkins University here.
  • ROLAND PARK – Roland Park was founded in 1891, making it the first planned suburban community in the United States. This area is known for its large, beautiful historic homes surrounded by lots of restaurants such as the brunch cafe Miss Shirley’s, the French bistro Petit Louis, and the Indian restaurant Namaste Baltimore.

How transit friendly is Baltimore

With a majority of its residents not owning cars, Baltimore has a robust transport system within the city and its environs. Public transit is mostly handled by the Maryland Transit Administration and Charm City Circulator. The city provides multiple ways for residents to commute within the city and also to move out.

Walkability

Baltimore City has a Walk Score of 65, which makes it the 10th most walkable city in the United States.

Roads/Traffic

The 2019 INRIX Global Traffic Scorecard, which examined the performance of more than 975 cities worldwide, ranks Baltimore as one of the ten busiest cities in the United States.

Taxis

Yellow/Checker Cab has a fleet of nearly 600 taxis in Baltimore, providing safe, reliable transportation to the airports and within the city. Credit cards of all types are accepted. Taxis that are wheelchair accessible are available upon request.

Public transportation

For regular use of public transportation in Baltimore city, as well as Washington Area Metrorail and Metrobus, consider obtaining a MARC Transit Link Card (TLC). This card offers unlimited monthly use of these transport services, plus several others.

Airports

Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport popularly called BWI or BWI Marshall. The airport is located approximately nine miles from Downtown Baltimore and thirty miles from Washington, D.C.

Cycle friendly

Biking is an environmentally friendly and quick way to travel around Baltimore. And it gives you more of an intimate feel to Charm city.

The Inner Harbor is located as the city’s focal point atop Baltimore’s beautiful waterfront. It’s surrounded by shops, restaurants, hotels, and attractions, including the Maryland Science Center, the National Aquarium, and Port Discovery Children’s Museum in the fish market building. That is just in the world-renowned Inner Harbor.

Throughout the city, there are a huge number of educational, cultural, and entertainment options for all ages. The Baltimore Museum of Art is one of the city’s cultural jewels. Visit Mount Vernon, Baltimore’s cultural and historic center, which houses the Walters Art Museum, and century-old Washington Monument.
