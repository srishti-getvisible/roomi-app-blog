Exciting Outdoor Activities in New Delhi

1. Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple is a work of art in its own right. The entire edifice is built entirely of marble, from top to bottom. The temple is open to people of all cultures and is one of the best places to visit in New Delhi to meditate and pray.

2. Humayun’s Tomb

In the sixteenth century, this monument was built as a memorial for the Mughal emperor Humayun. This UNESCO World Heritage site now includes marble monuments of a number of other Mughal kings. Truly one of the best places to visit in New Delhi to witness exquisite Persian design.

3. Rashtrapati Bhavan

The Rashtrapati Bhavan, the President of India’s official home, is a magnificent palace structure with about 400 rooms. The beautiful Mughal Gardens, which draw a significant number of local and international visitors, are the pride of the presidential residence here.

Must Visit Museums in New Delhi

1. Nehru Planetarium

Space science buffs, this one’s for you! Observing and studying various astronomical phenomena with telescopes is one of the best things to do in New Delhi. The planetarium has evolved into a scientific research center for astronomy, as well as a venue for astronomical gatherings and lectures.

2. International Dolls Museum

The International Dolls Museum in Delhi is home to over 6500 exhibits from over 85 nations. The museum is divided into two sections: one for dolls from India and other Asian nations, and the other for dolls from the United States, Australia, Europe, and New Zealand.

3. National Science Centre

This is a great, family-friendly attraction that has been named one of the best places to visit in New Delhi. Learn about everything from interstellar physics to dinosaurs at the National Science Centre.

Cultural Places To Visit in New Delhi

1. India Gate

A visit to this 42m high iconic and historical monument is among the best things to do in New Delhi. Dedicated to the remembrance of the soldiers who lost their lives in the Afghan war, it’s not only an educational experience but also a photographer’s joy.

2. Qutub Minar

One of the best things to do in New Delhi is to go to Qutub Minar. It’s a well-known UNESCO world heritage monument and among the most popular places to visit in New Delhi. This tower, which stands 240 feet tall, was built in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the first ruler of Delhi. This minaret bears the name of its builder.

3. Akshardham Temple

Akshardham Temple is undoubtedly one of the best places to visit in New Delhi. This complex holds more than a temple. It also has a water show, a theme garden, hall of values, IMAX film of Swaminarayan’s life, cultural boat ride and much more. We have no doubt that its architecture will leave you in awe.

How transit-friendly is New Delhi

The crowded and busy city of Delhi provides several options for its residents to get around the city. Even if you don’t own a car while you’re in the city, you can always rely on other modes of transport to get to your destination!

1. Metro

The metro has a combination of both underground and elevated trains that cover the city’s busiest areas. The Delhi Metro has 10 lines that spread across the entire city, making it the most reliable mode of transport.

2. Taxis

New Delhi has an abundance of taxi services, from local taxi services to Ola and Uber. You can book your ride easily using apps and reach your destination without any hassle!

3. Auto rickshaw

Auto rickshaws are one of the most common modes of transport in the city. Autos are the most affordable as well as reliable means of commuting within Delhi. The fares for autos are determined mainly by the distance traveled using an electronic meter. You can try the auto ride while looking for rooms for rent in New Delhi!

2. Explore the Indian Culture at India International Trade Fair

The IITF is one of the oldest and largest exhibitions in India. It’s a huge annual event that draws visitors from all over the country and the world. The fair provides a platform for businesses from states around the country to showcase their cultures. Also, they sell their traditional foods, clothes, literature, and more to visitors!

The Delhi World Book Fair is one of the biggest book fairs in India and Asia. It showcases over 100,000 books with publishers from all around the world. It’s a perfect place for all book lovers and is nothing less than a dream come true for the readers and writers community!
