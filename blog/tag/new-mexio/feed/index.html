How transit friendly is Albuquerque?

1. Walkability in Albuquerque

Albuquerque is the 28th most walkable city of the 50 largest cities, as per WalkScore, in terms of commuting. Moreover, it has walkable zones where you can enjoy a quiet stroll that leads to nowhere.

2. Cycling in Albuquerque

Albuquerque has trails, bike routes and dedicated paths throughout. With its first Bicycle Boulevard up and running in 2009, there are plans for more investment in biking infrastructure and convenience in the future.

3. Public transportation in Albuquerque

The Alvarado Transportation Center hosts the city bus system, ABQ RIDE which includes the rapid ride express bus services for inter city travel. Amtrak’s railways serve Albuquerque too with daily commuter rail services.

4. Airports in Albuquerque

2 airports serve the city: Albuquerque International Sunport and Double Eagle II Airport. While the former serves civilians, the latter is primarily used as an air ambulance, corporate flight, military flight, training flight, charter flight, and private flight facility.

Cultural activities in Albuquerque

Albuquerque International Balloon Fiesta

If you’re looking for rooms for rent in Albuquerque in October, you’ll see the New Mexico skies filled with colorful balloons for 9 days as part of the International Balloon Fiesta! Want to join in the fun? You can hop on a hot air balloon of your choice and fly away into the beautiful skies overlooking the scenic landscape!

Sandia Peak Tramway

The Sandia Peak gets its name from Sandia that means ‘watermelons’ in Spanish, because the mountains resemble watermelons at dusk. Sounds super cute, doesn’t it? You can hike to the summit, or enjoy an aerial view of the peaks via the tramway. Either way, it’s a great way to see Albuquerque!

Casa Rondena Winery

Looking for cheap rooms for rent in Albuquerque, – like anywhere else – can get tiresome. So when you’re looking for some time out, head to the Casa Rondena Winery and delve into the music, culture, architecture, and of course, sample some wine from the area!

Historical spots in Albuquerque

Albuquerque Museum of Art and History

A city that boasts a history like no other, the Albuquerque Museum of Art and History preserves all that’s ancient about the city. Visit the museum to see displays of old Spanish settlement’s ways of living, to Georgia O’Keefe’s art, all in one place!

Indian Pueblo Cultural Center

If you’re interested in the Pueblo cultures of the New Mexico area, then this is one place you must visit. From an outstanding museum, tours and traditional dances, to lip smacking new native American cuisine, you’re bound to experience history with all your senses. And you’ll spot the multi storey stone buildings (pueblos) that we mentioned before too!

Petroglyph National Monument

Interested in primitive art? This outdoor museum is for you! The Petroglyph National Monument protects one of the largest petroglyph sites in North America, featuring designs and symbols carved onto volcanic rocks by Native Americans and Spanish settlers 400 to 700 years ago!
