Getting to know Ann Arbor

Ann Arbor, or ‘Tree Town’ gets its nickname from the dense forestation of parks and residential areas. So when looking for rooms for rent in Ann Arbor, expect a LOT of greenery around, with more than 50,000 trees along the streets and even more in the parks! And, thanks to its location on the Huron River, the city has an abundance of fresh agricultural produce to offer its residents as well.

How transit friendly is Ann Arbor?

Roads

Conforming to a grid pattern, downtown Ann Arbor branches out to the highways surrounding the city.

There are three freeways that service Ann Arbor, namely:

  • I-94, which runs along the southern and western portion of the city.
  • U.S. Highway 23 (US 23), which primarily runs along the eastern edge of Ann Arbor.
  • M-14, which runs along the northern edge of the city.

Other nearby highways include US 12 (Michigan Ave.), M-17 (Washtenaw Ave.), and M-153 (Ford Rd.).

Walkability & cycling

The city has a system of bike routes and paths, which includes the nearly complete Washtenaw County Border-to-Border Trail. The many trees that line the trails make it an even more beautiful ride or walk.

Bus service

  • The Ride, or the Ann Arbor Area Transportation Authority (AAATA) operates public bus services throughout the city and nearby Ypsilanti.
  • The AirRide or Route 98 connects to Detroit Metro Airport a dozen times a day.
  • There are also limited-stop bus services between Ann Arbor and Chelsea and Canton. The two routes, 91 and 92 respectively, are known as the ExpressRide.
  • Greyhound Lines provide intercity bus service.
  • The Michigan Flyer, a service operated by Indian Trails, cooperates with AAATA for their AirRide and additionally offers bus service to East Lansing.
  • Megabus has direct service to Chicago, Illinois, while a bus service is provided by Amtrak for rail passengers making connections to services in East Lansing and Toledo, Ohio.

Airports

  • Ann Arbor Municipal Airport is a small, city-run general aviation airport.
  • Detroit Metropolitan Airport, the area’s large international airport is located in Romulus.
  • Willow Run Airport near Ypsilanti serves freight, corporate, and general aviation clients.

Railway

Amtrak, which provides service to the city at the Ann Arbor Train Station, operates the Wolverine train between Chicago and Pontiac, via Detroit.

Cultural activities in Ann Arbor

University of Michigan Museum of Art

Built as a war memorial in 1909, the University of Michigan Museum of Art (UMMA) is one of the largest university art museums in the US.

Head over to view a comprehensive collection of more than 19,000 works that span an array of periods, cultures and forms of media, from works by Pablo Picasso and Franz Kline to Claude Monet, Randolph Rogers and Kara Walker.

There are also a variety of educational programs, workshops, and classes for both students and visitors, along with art events throughout the year for visitors.

Michigan Stadium

Sports lovers don’t need an introduction to this stadium. But for those of us who don’t know, ‘The Big House’, or the Michigan Stadium is the largest stadium in the United States and the second-largest in the world, seating crowds of up to 115,000 people. You can catch all sorts of sports events here, from college football and hockey to the U of M’s graduation ceremonies.

State Street District

Shoppers, head straight to the State Street District when in Ann Arbor. Known for its stores, restaurants and public events including fairs and festivals, this cool spot is also home to works of art by over 300 artists.

Historical spots in Ann Arbor

Kempf House

Kempf House, designed by local master Arden Ballard is a Greek Revival Style House that had been the residence of the Kempf family from 1888 to 1953. Now it’s a house museum furnished with antique Victorian pieces and Kempf family belongings. It is also home to the city’s first grand piano of 1877!

Burton Memorial Tower

The Burton Memorial Tower is a clock tower located on Central Campus at the University of Michigan. The tower was built in 1936 as a memorial for University President Marion Leroy Burton. The grand carillon contains 55 bells and is the world’s fourth heaviest carillon, weighing a total of 43 tons.

Hill Auditorium

The Hill Auditorium, opened in 1913, is named after Arthur Hill (1847-1909). Arthur Hill served as a regent of the university from 1901 to 1909. You can head here for beautiful music concerts owing to its incredible acoustics.
