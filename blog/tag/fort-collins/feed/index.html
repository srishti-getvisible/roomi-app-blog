Getting to know Fort Collins

Located in the west of the US, in Colorado, the city of Fort Collins is Larimer County’s most populated municipality. Approximately 65 miles from the state capital Denver, Colorado, and 45 miles south of Cheyenne, Wyoming, Fort Collins sits at the base of the Rocky Mountain foothills.

Its economy is a mix of service-related businesses and assembling. An outdoorsy, mid-sized, dynamic city, Fort Collins is surrounded by snow-capped mountains, clear rivers, and natural lakes.

Known for its 300-days of year-round sunshine, summers here are mild to hot, with July being the warmest. Moreover, winters are mild to moderately cold, with December being the coldest. Additionally, if you love the idea of a winter wonderland, early September through to the end of May is snow time!

How transit-friendly is Fort Collins

Fort Collins is very transit-friendly! For this reason, the city encourages alternative transportation, like cycling and using public transit, over driving.

1. Walk-ability

A very friendly walkable city. You can conveniently walk around the city and most errands can be accomplished on foot.

2. Roads/traffic

Rush hours are from 7:00 am to 9:00 am and 4:00 pm to 6:00 pm, so leave early if possible. At other times driving is smooth and easy.

3. Taxis

Taxis are available on call 24*7. A few taxi services available in Fort Collins are:

  • zTrip (formerly known as Fort Collins Yellow Cab) can be booked using the zTrip App, call via phone, hail on the street, or book on the website.
  • Pedicabs powered by cyclists are also available for tours if you’re after a unique way to travel!

4. Public Transportation

Fort Collins has excellent public transportation. With the Transfort city bus system, you can track your schedules, routes, late-night services, and much more. In addition, it has information on the MAX Bus Rapid Transit and FLEX regional bus route for traveling further afield.

5. Airports

Denver International Airport is the closest major airport to Fort Collins. By road, it is roughly 70 miles south of the city.

6. Cycling

This city is an award-winning bicycle-friendly town. Visitors, residents, and students who want to explore the city on a bicycle but don’t own their own, can rent one from the historic Fort Collins Bicycle Library.

Cultural Activities in Fort Collins

Tour De Fat

The New Belgium Brewing Company hosts the annual Tour de Fat, which sees droves of people riding bikes dressed in costumes. Fort Collins was a non-alcoholic town until 1968 when Colorado State University students protested to end the prohibition. This brewing company was conceived to recall the company’s beginnings and celebrate the established cycling community in Fort Collins.

Tour De Farms

Running 35-40 miles, Tour De Farm is a half-a-day bicycle charity fundraiser ride held annually in Fort Collins. It begins and ends at the Farmer’s Market or a local brewery. It also features a mix of paved and gravel road rides and a few stops at local farms. Moreover, at each farm stop, there will be tastings and refreshments on offer, along with welcoming representatives or owners to greet you.
