15 Best Things To Do In Oklahoma City

  • Posted byby Team Roomi
  • June 26, 2021

Planning a visit to Oklahoma City? You’re in for more than just an ‘OK’ experience! And if you’re wondering what you should do when in OKC, we’ve got you covered. Here’s your full guide to the best things to do in Oklahoma City.

Explore the Outdoors in Oklahoma City

1. Myriad Botanical Gardens

A 17-acre botanical garden and interactive urban park located in downtown Oklahoma City, the Botanical Garden is home to densely landscaped areas around a sunken lake. And when looking for the best things to do in Oklahoma in the outdoors, this is the perfect place!

However, if you’re planning a visit, you should know that the Crystal bridge is closed for renovation until fall 2022.

The costs and timings given below are for the outdoor gardens.

Costs: Free

Timings: 6 am – 11 pm

Event Calendar
Plan Your Visit

2. Get a rush of adrenalin at the Frontier City Theme Park

Frontier City Theme Park, Oklahoma’s only amusement park, features over 40 acres of roller coasters, family rides, entertainment and fun for all ages. And this is the place you must visit in Oklahoma if you love an adrenalin rush. From water rides and live shows to dining and shopping, there’s something for everyone here!

Costs: $30 – $50 for one day pass; $49.99 for season pass

Timings: 11 am – 6 pm

Buy Passes
Event Calendar

3. Float through Downtown in the Bricktown Water Taxi

The Bricktown Water Taxi in Downtown Oklahoma has been serving passengers since 1999, and is one of the most popular activities in OKC! So when looking for cool things to do in Oklahoma, hop on a ride on the water taxi! You will get an exquisite view of the city’s Bricktown area while floating down the Bricktown canal.

Costs: $4 – $12

Timings: Changes with season and weather. Check link below for details.

Book Tickets
Visit Website

Enjoy the sports scene in Oklahoma City

4. Boathouse District

An Olympic and Paralympic training site for rowing and kayaking, the Boathouse District invites everyone looking for cool things to do in Oklahoma! From white water rafting to zip-lining, the Riversport Adventure park has a range of activities for you to enjoy! And while you’re here, don’t forget to snap a shot with the huge bronze Oklahoma Land Run Monument.

Costs: $40 – $80 (Day Pass)

Timings: 11 am – 6 pm

Book Tickets
Event Schedule

5. Bricktown Ballpark

Home to the OKC Dodgers home games and many more, the Bricktown Ballpark is more than just a stadium! It is one of the most famous historical places to visit in Oklahoma, where you’ll find statues of some of history’s best baseball players – including Mickey Mantle, Johnny Bench and Warren Spahn.

Costs: $12 – $30

Event Schedule

6. Oklahoma Sports Hall of Fame

What was originally known as the Jim Thorpe Association, the Oklahoma Sports Hall of Fame displays great Oklahomans who have represented the state on state, national and international levels in sports.

Costs: Free

Timings: Sunday – Monday: Closed; Tuesday – Saturday: 10 am – 5 pm

Event Calendar

Visit the Museums in Oklahoma City

7. Oklahoma City Museum of Art

Featuring a diverse collection from North America, Europe and Asia, along with American art and postwar abstraction, the Oklahoma City Museum of Art is a must visit when looking for the best things to do in Oklahoma City. Film lovers can head to the Samuel Roberts Noble Theatre in the museum to view classic, independent and documentary films.

Costs: $5 – $12

Timings: Wednesday – Thursday: 11 am-5 pm; Friday:11 am – 8 pm; Saturday: 10 am – 5 pm; Sunday: 12 – 5 pm

Book Tickets

8. Museum of Osteology

If you find skeletons fascinating rather than spooky, then a cool thing to do in Oklahoma city would be to visit the Museum of Osteology that focuses on the form and function of the skeletal system. From a tiny hummingbird to a 40 foot humpback whale, there are all kinds of species on display.

Costs: $7 + tax

Timings: Monday – Friday: 9 am – 5 pm; Saturday: 11 am – 5 pm; Sunday: 1 pm – 5 pm

Website

9. American Banjo Museum

A 21,000 square foot facility that has a rich history of the banjo on display, the American Banjo museum is home to a collection of more than 400 banjos along with recordings, films, videos, printed music and more from all eras of civilization!

Costs: $5 – $8

Timings: Tuesday-Saturday: 11 am – 6 pm; Sunday: Noon – 5 pm; Monday: Closed

Event Calendar

Indulge in the Culture of Oklahoma City

10. Paseo Arts District

If you’re in the mood for art and culture when in OKC, head to the Paseo Arts District. It is home to over 20 galleries with artists working in the two block street with Spanish revival architecture, including stucco buildings with clay roof tiles. And there’s more to it than art: the district also has restaurants, boutiques, art schools and other establishments for you to stroll through.

Timings: Most galleries & shops open Tuesday – Saturday: 11 am – 5 pm; Restaurants & bars open daily.

Event Calendar

11. Civic Center Music Hall

Recognized as one of the national premier acoustic performing arts facilities, there’s no better way to enjoy the culture of OKC than visiting the Civic Center Music Hall. With three theatres, a reception hall, and smaller rental facilities, there are all kinds of shows you can enjoy here when looking for the best things to do in Oklahoma.

Timings: Monday – Friday: 10 am – 5 pm; Saturday – Sunday: Closed

Events and Tickets

12. Oklahoma History Center

The Oklahoma History Center is an 18 acre learning center, a Smithsonian Affiliate and also accredited by the American Alliance of Museums. It explores Oklahoma’s unique history of native culture, aviation, commerce and more, making it one of the best places to visit in Oklahoma for history buffs.

Costs: $7 – $10

Timings: Tuesday – Saturday: 10 am – 4:45 pm; Sunday – Monday: Closed

Event Calendar
Exhibits

Eat, Drink and Be Merry in Oklahoma City

13. Cafe 501

Cafe 501 started as a little bakery, but has now reached new heights in Oklahoma city. A highly diversified cuisine, they have recipes from France, Italy, South America, Morocco, Germany and Austria.

From rustic stone oven pizzas, to unique salads and specialty sandwiches, your taste buds will rejoice when feasting here!

Timings:

Monday –Saturday 11am-9pm

Sunday Brunch 9am-2pm

View Menu

14. Pub W

A beloved local neighborhood pub and one of the best places to visit in Oklahoma , Pub W or Pub Dub as it’s called, will welcome you with open arms for a quality time. Enjoy a delicious meal and cool down with a beer to while away the hours here.

Timings: 11 am – 12 am; sat – 10:30 – 12 am; sun – 10:20 – 10 pm

View Menu

15. Automobile Alley

A small, hip neighborhood known for its brick buildings and neon signs straight from the 1920s, Automobile Alley is home to remnants of the area’s model car dealerships. And yes, you guessed right! That’s where it gets its name from.

Right now, the buildings are home to great many restaurants, bars, small businesses, clothes stores, and more for you to eat, drink and shop!
