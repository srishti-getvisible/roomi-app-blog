Where is SoHo?

SoHo is an acronym for “South of Houston Street” and is bordered by Houston Street, Lafayette Street, and the western part of Broadway. This world-famous neighborhood is home to many celebrities and is regularly featured in movies. You’re likely to come across one if you find a room for rent in SoHo.

SoHo is a world-famous shopping paradise with a plethora of shopping stores, art galleries, restaurants, and cast-iron architecture that locals and tourists love so much.

Is SoHo safe?

Soho is a relatively safe area of New York City so you needn’t worry if you’re looking for rooms for rent here. However, it is important to always be vigilant and have your wits about you. Here are a few resources to help you stay updated on the area in real-time.

  • AreaVibes: AreaVibes assigns a liveability score to your neighborhood based on the zip code. The score is generated by weighing available amenities, cost of living, safety rates, education, employment, housing and weather.
  • NextDoor: This app keeps you abreast of what’s going on ‘next door’. It’s a private social network for your area. It allows the exchange of safety tips, reporting of suspicious activities, and even arrangement of a babysitter.
  • AroundMe: AroundMe helps you locate nearby hospitals, supermarkets and pharmacies so you can get to where you need to go faster.
  • Hotline Numbers: 911 and 311 are the two relevant numbers New York residents use to make reports or in emergencies. Call 911 to report any emergency. 311 can be dialled to report issues like abandoned vehicles, road repairs, noise complaints and graffiti.

Public Transit in SoHo

When looking for rooms for rent in SoHo, it’s helpful to use the public transit options in the neighborhood so you don’t have to worry about traffic.

1. Walkability

SoHo is the 9th most walkable neighborhood with a Walk Score of 100. This means you can get around the neighborhood very easily on foot, without needing a car if you find a room for rent in SoHo.

2. Roads & Traffic

For the most part, traffic is relatively light in SoHo. This is probably because of the availability of train stations nearby, which reduces the need for people to drive.

Parks in SoHo

Washington Square Park

Located just 7-15 mins away from SoHo – depending on the transit mode – this park is a regular relaxation spot for residents. It has more than five acres of open space, sheltered benches, a central fountain, and a statue of George Washington mounted on a horse.

Spring Street Park

Spring Street Park, formerly known as SoHo Square park, is a small park right at the center of SoHo. With a few benches and open space, you’ll find respite here from the hustle and bustle of SoHo.

Museums in SoHo

1. New York City Fire Museum


Built in 1904, the former firehouse was repurposed into a three-floor museum showing the history of New York City Fire Department history. There is also a memorial to the 343 members of the FDNY-EMS who dies in the World Trade Center attacks. Opening hours are 10am – 5pm and the entry fee is $8.

2. Museum of Ice Cream

The Museum of Ice cream is not a traditional museum but is definitely worth a visit when you’re looking for rooms for rent in SoHo. It has 13 interactive art installations across 25,000 square feet over 3 floors, including multi-sensory installations and the world’s biggest sprinkle pool!

3. The Drawing Center in NYC

Since opening in 1977, the Drawing Center has continued to attract tourists and locals. The museum focuses on historical and contemporary drawing, so if you appreciate immersive arts, this is the place to go.
