A flavorful blend of art and culture represents the ‘heart of Oklahoma.’ Tulsa is the second-largest city in Oklahoma, famous for its dynamic urban civilization. Be it a calming walk in the park or a craving for some good ol’ opera, this city caters to every mood! You can feel influences from South, Southwest, Mid-West and Native American cultures throughout the city. You’ll experience this especially in the wildlife reserves and ethnic festivals that represent the city. So, if you’re looking for rooms for rent in Tulsa, use this guide to see where the best things are.

Getting to know Tulsa

Let us guide you on finding cheap rooms for rent in Tulsa. But before that, we’ll take you on a tour of the culture and history of the city.

What was Tulsa originally called? ‘Tallasi,’ which means ‘old town,’ was the name given to the city by Creek Council Oak Tree back in the 19th Century.

During your time here, you’ll experience all four seasons. Not only does Tulsa promise hot and humid summers, but also, cold to mild winters. Because it’s situated between the Great Plains and the rolling hills of the Ozarks, Tulsa holds the “Green Country” title. That means the city is full of the greenest vegetation in Oklahoma. Also, Tulsa is the most topographically diverse city in the entire state! So, if you’re a nature lover, there’s much to explore in Tulsa’s 135 parks.

How transit-friendly is Tulsa?

Transportation should be one of the main criteria to judge when finding rooms for rent in Tulsa as a budget-conscious person.

1. Cycling

Cycling is immensely supported with protected bike lanes and bike maps for the city! Exploring Tulsa as you cycle is therapeutic, especially for those of you who prefer keeping an eye on your health.

2. Walkability

Tulsa ranked 34th out of 141 U.S. cities for being the most ‘walkable.’ So, we hope you keep your sneakers handy!

3. Public Transportation

Tulsa Bus Transit consists of 97 vehicles running across 19 different routes connecting all four; Downtown, Midtown, Southern and Western Tulsa.

4. Airport

The international Airport in Tulsa is responsible for over 80 departures a day!

5. River Port

Port of Catoosa is an inland port that helps connect the Mississippi River with the Arkansas River,. Actually, it’s become one of the largest river ports in the U.S.!
