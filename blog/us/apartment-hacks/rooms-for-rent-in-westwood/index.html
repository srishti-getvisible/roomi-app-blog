Where is Westwood?

Settled on Wilshire Blvd., Westwood is nestled among some of L.A.’s wealthiest neighborhoods. To the north, you’ll find Bel Air and to the south, there’s Century City. On the west lies Brentwood and on the east, there’s Beverly Hills. It’s now attracting a huge number of young families and professionals due to the comparatively reasonable average rent in Westwood than its surrounding neighborhoods.

Museums in Westwood

1. Hammer Museum

The Hammer Museum provides something for every art enthusiast, with a diverse range of exhibits, films, and discussions. The museum also gives free entry to the general public, making a visit to the Hammer all the more appealing.

2. The Fowler

Having museums near potential rooms for rent in Westwood is a must if you’re a history buff! This internationally famous museum, located in the center of UCLA’s campus, focuses on works of art from Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The Fowler is ideal for anyone interested in world arts and culture. The museum is open to the public for free.

3. The Planetarium

Located in the university’s South Campus area, the Planetarium hosts free Wednesday evening lectures, as well as planetarium presentations. If you’re looking for museums in Westwood that offer telescope gazing, this place won’t disappoint!
