Rooms For Rent In Dallas

  • Posted byby Team Roomi
  • April 30, 2021

Making a move to the Big D? Dallas has 34 neighborhoods to choose from and each has its quirks. Harboring all the charm of Texas, Dallas is a cosmopolitan city known for its high concentration of restaurants and shopping centers. Dallas, Texas has almost 20 percent more renters than the region and over 10 percent more than the county. When it comes to the rental market, the rooms for rent in Dallas come in a diverse variety with the many residential neighborhoods found here.

Getting to know Dallas

Skyscrapers, such as Bank of America Plaza (1985) and Reunion Tower (1978), create a striking night skyline that is a sight not to miss. You may have also heard that everything is bigger in Texas. Well, Dallas goes on to fulfill that promise quite well! Get ready to get spoilt for choice among the many museums, and vast art collections, like The Sixth Floor Museum/Texas School Book Depository, which, though small in size, offers an immense amount of information about the assassination of John F. Kennedy.

Finding rooms for rent in Dallas, TX

Dallas City Hall reports that within Dallas, the majority of residents are not homeowners, and unlike the county and region, the city has a greater diversity of housing types and a much higher percentage of renters. If you’re looking for rooms to rent in Dallas, here are some of the most preferred neighborhoods!

Top Neighborhoods in Dallas, TX

Uptown

Located immediately north of downtown Dallas and west of North Central Expressway, this fashionable neighborhood is teeming with young professionals and empty-nesters looking for an urban lifestyle.

Preston Hollow

Preston Hollow is one of Dallas’s most elite neighborhoods. Located in North Dallas, but inside the LBJ/I-635 loop, the neighborhood stretches north from Northwest Highway to Royal Lane and east from Inwood Road to Hillcrest Road, offering easy access from LBJ/I-635 and the Dallas North Tollway.

M Streets

With its leafy, tree-lined streets and darling homes dating as far back as the 1920s, the M Streets continues to be a popular neighborhood for couples and young families who want to be close to downtown and near White Rock Lake.

Average rent in Dallas, TX

If you’re moving to Dallas from a bigger city like Chicago, New York, or Los Angeles, you’ll be shocked at how affordable rent is here. Plus, a lot of the apartments for rent are new developments, which means the amenities provided can be a delight.

When looking for rooms for rent in Dallas, TX, you need to keep one thing in mind. These numbers could be higher in neighborhoods like Uptown and the Design District and lower in areas like Addison and Oak Lawn.

University life in Dallas

With a diverse economy and Dallas being the third most populous city in the state of Texas, there are about 38 colleges to choose from in the metropolitan area. As a cultural hub, the metro area has the perks of a modern city with western influence, and business opportunities are plenty due to the many Fortune 500 companies that are headquartered in Dallas.

Some schools that you’ll find in Dallas take the name of specific religions, even though their open to students of all faiths, including Texas Christian University and Southern Methodist University.

For students specifically or a single person living in Dallas, the rental cost of living can average between $1400 and $1800. The overall cost of living, however, will depend on where you live as Dallas is a large city.

The top universities in Dallas-Fort Worth area include:

  • Southern Methodist University
  • Texas Christian University
  • University of Texas Dallas
  • University of Dallas
  • University of North Texas

How transit-friendly is Dallas?

Despite being one of those cities where suburb life comes full circle with car driving individuals, Dallas is home to the country’s longest light-rail system. The Dallas Area Rapid Transit authority.

Along with the DART, Dallas is also home to a variety of buses, taxis, rideshares, and trolleys.

Taking the DART in Dallas

The DART is more than just a typical bus system; it’s a bus and connecting train system that links downtown Dallas and the surrounding suburbs, and it’s constantly expanding.

All in all, there are 64 DART Rail stations and 14 bus transfer facilities, in addition to 10 Trinity Railway Express (TRE) stations — the TRE connects downtown Dallas Union Station and Fort Worth, as well as several towns in between and the DFW International Airport. Personalized, on-demand, curb-to-curb service is also available via their GoLink app. Finally, the Dallas Streetcar operates in Oak Cliff and makes connections with the final DART Rail trains at Union Station; trains are free and operate every 20 minutes.

Ride Shares in Dallas

If you’re looking for Ride Shares in Dallas, your options include popular rideshare apps like Uber and Lyft. There’s also the Curb app, which connects people to taxis and other professional for-hire drivers. SuperShuttle is also an excellent way to get around the city! SuperShuttle runs from Dallas Love Field or DFW to downtown and most major hotels.

The McKinney Avenue Trolley

Also known as the M-Line, this is a beloved mode of transportation for all of Dallas. The coolest thing about the M-Line is that consists of restored vintage trolleys that operate 365 days per year. Not only is taking the trolley a unique way to see the city and get around, but it’s also free (though do consider dropping a donation).

Population in Dallas

Dallas is the 9th most populous city in the United States, and the third in Texas after the city of Houston and San Antonio. Its metropolitan area encompasses one-quarter of the population of Texas and is the largest in the Southern U.S. and Texas followed by the Greater Houston metropolitan area.

The population of Dallas reported for 2021 is 1,347,120 in a land area of 339.7 sq mi.

The population, thus, is quite diverse — and will most likely show up as a factor when you’re looking at rooms for rent in Dallas, Tx. The suburbs of Dallas are home to a large number of Asian people, including Koreans, Taiwanese, Chinese, Vietnamese, Thai, Indians, Pakistanis, and Sri Lankans. Popular suburbs for Asian residents include Arlington, Irving, Flower Mound, and Garland. Dallas also has a large population of people from the Horn of Africa, including immigrants from Somalia, Eritrea, and Ethiopia.
