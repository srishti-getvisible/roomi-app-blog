A suburb in the Los Angeles metropolitan area that’s surrounded by the Verdugo Mountains, Glendale is center for commerce, natural beauty, parks, healthcare and entertainment in abundance! Getting to know Glendale

If you like Mediterranean climate and cuisine alike, Glendale is a great city for you to find both! A climate with hot summers and mild-to-warm winters and occasional rainfall, and the home of one of the country’s largest Armenian-American populations, you’ll find lip smacking Middle Eastern food here.

And if you consider yourself (or your dog) a park lover, you’d be happy to know there are public parks everywhere in the city, from Deukmejian Wilderness Park in the north to Cerritos Park in the south.

Furthermore, Glendale is one of Southern California’s leading corporate markets, featuring a wide range of properties and amenities. It has over six million square feet of office space and is home to recognized firms like Walt Disney Imagineering, ServiceTitan, IHOP/Applebees, DreamWorks, LegalZoom, and Public Storage.

Top neighborhoods in Glendale

Downtown Glendale: This is a great walkable neighborhood that lies along the Brand Boulevard corridor. From the Bellagio water fountain show to a trolly ride at the Americana, there’s always something fun to see or do!

The Canyons: If you want to be close to nature, but also need to be close to the city, the Canyons is the best option for you to find rooms for rent in Glendale, CA.

Crescenta: A hip neighborhood with many joints to hang out in, as well as a neighborhood with many schools, you’ll find all types of folks living in Crescenta. And with many bus stops around, your commute will be made easier if public transport is your way to go.

How transit friendly is Glendale?

Walkability & Cycling

The second most walkable city in SoCal as of 2016, Glendale has an average Walk Score of 71 with 191,719 residents, and it’s somewhat bikeable, as per WalkScore.

Buses & Trains

The Glendale Transportation Center provides connections to Greyhound buses in the city. Additionally, Glendale is also served by LADOT, Metro Local, Metro Rapid and Glendale Beeline for you to commute when finding rooms for rent in Glendale, CA.

Roads

You’ll find four freeways in the city:

  • The Glendale Freeway (State Route 2)
  • The Ventura Freeway (State Route 134)
  • The Foothill Freeway (Interstate 210)
  • The Golden State Freeway (Interstate 5)

Heads up: your highest risk of congestion is on the Golden State Freeway.

Airports

Owned by the Burbank-Glendale-Pasadena Airport Authority, a joint powers agreement between the cities of Burbank, Glendale, and Pasadena, the closest airport is the Hollywood Burbank Airport.

Cultural activities in Glendale

Museum of Neon Art

Interested in shiny, artsy, mobile stuff? Head straight to the Museum of Neon Art. You’ll find a collection of neon, electric and kinetic art to feed the curiosity in you. Integrating electrical technology, creative design, and concepts of physics and chemistry, it is the only museum in the world devoted to electric and kinetic art.

Raffi’s Place

There’s no better way to take in the culture of a place than to delve into its cuisine! If you agree, Raffi’s Place is a must visit when in Glendale. Authentic Persian and Middle Eastern cuisine with a beautiful indoor + outdoor setting, it’s a landmark in Glendale and beyond!

Alex Theatre

A Glendale landmark since 1925, the Alex Theatre is a Vaudeville house and a movie palace that now hosts wonderful performing arts. It is an entertainment center and culture hub in the city.

Historical spots in Glendale

Derby House

Built in 1926 for James Derby and his family by Lloyd Wright, the Derby House is an awe-inspiring architectural wonder. Created from concrete textile blocks, wood and stucco, the plant-like patterns in the concrete blocks have been called one of Wright’s ‘strongest achievements’ by UCLA historian Thomas Hines. So, whether you’re just visiting or you’re finding rooms for rent in Glendale, CA, this is a must visit!

Rockhaven Sanitarium Historic District

Founded in 1923 by psychiatric nurse Agnes Richards, Rockhaven Sanitarium was the first mental asylum that wanted to provide better care to patients back in the day. It all started with one little rock house, which gives it its name, and was America’s first woman-founded mental health facility.

Schaffer House

John Lautner designed and built the Schaffer House in 1949. The main attraction of the house remains how it was planned around the oak trees and sits nestled neatly between them. Made of redwood and glass, supported by brick and concrete, it looks beautiful and includes nature in its architecture in a wonderful way.
