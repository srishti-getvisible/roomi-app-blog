5 Best Apps to Find a Roommate in NYC for 2024

  • Posted byby Team Roomi
  • April 5, 2024

Moving to a new city can be both exciting and overwhelming, especially when it comes to finding a roommate in one of the busiest and most expensive cities in the world – New York City. Fortunately, technology has come to the rescue with a plethora of apps designed to help you find the perfect roommate for your 2024 NYC adventure.

Whether you’re looking for someone to share the rent, split the bills, or simply make new friends in the Big Apple, these 5 best apps will make your roommate search a breeze.

Say goodbye to Craigslist ads and hello to stress-free apartment hunting with the help of these innovative and user-friendly apps!

5 Best Apps to Find A Roommate in NYC

New York also doubles as a great place to find neighborhoods to call home. Some of these are even quite affordable, which is about as common in NYC as peanut butter and chocolate unicorn. So, it’s best to find a roommate to share expenses with!

Your search for a roommate finder app ends here with this complete guide of five of the best apps to find a roommate in NYC.

1. Roomi

Source: Roomi

With listings available worldwide, Roomi is one of the best apps to find a roommate in NYC. It’s an all-in-one platform to find rooms, roommates, and co-living space listings with a user-friendly interface.

Roomi uses a unique algorithm to help you find the perfect roommate with just a few clicks. When creating your profile, you can include fun facts about you and your interests to make the roommate-matching process easier. There are also multiple filters to select from to narrow down your searches.

To ensure safety and avoid scams, all Roomi users undergo a thorough background check before being accepted and are required to upload a profile picture when creating a profile to make sure the profile is genuine. Roomi also ensures your safety by using Global ID, a system that verifies contact information, while maintaining user privacy.

Features:

  • Filter your search by location, gender preferences for your roommate, smoking habits, pets, and many more.
  • Has the option to see completely or partially verified profiles, and any linked social media profile.
  • Features in-app chat messaging and uses Global ID for data privacy.
  • Offers 24/7 support.

Pricing: Roomi is free to use, whether for personal or business use. But the roommate finder app does have some upgrades and subscriptions available:

For personal users:

  • Safety Bundle: unlimited messaging, five active listings, up to 7x boost in visibility, and user verification.
  • Listing Boost Credits: super boosts listings for 24 hours.
  • Profile Boost Credits: pushes profile to the top of the search page in the area for 3 hours.

For business users:

  • Solo Agent: direct messaging with up to 10 tenants per day, a verified Partner badge, and 10 active listings.
  • Business Unlimited: unlimited listings and unlimited tenant messages.

2. Diggz

Source: Diggz

Diggz is a roommate finder app that uses an algorithm to match users according to lifestyle, preferences, and other roommate prospects.

Features:

  • Has a matching algorithm for roommates.
  • Vets profiles against scammers and fake profiles.
  • Equipped with filters and secure in-app messaging.

Pricing: Diggz has premium membership plans with extra features like free instant messages, unlimited likes, and extra premium filters.

  • 1 Week: $8.99
  • 2 Weeks: $14.99
  • 1 Month: $23.99

3. Listings Project

Source: Listings Project

More than a roommate finder, Listings Project posts rooms, jobs & gigs, and services offered listings weekly. Every Wednesday at 8 AM EST, a new set of verified listings are posted on the website. It’s free to browse for listings, but if you want to post one, you have to pay around $9 to $39 per listing.

Features:

  • Offers other kinds of listings.
  • Has a team that vets and reviews each listing.

Pricing: Listing fees vary depending on the type and location.

  • Real Estate: $29 to $44 per listing
  • Jobs & Gigs and Services Offered: $39 to $59 per listing
  • Seeking Living or Work Space: $9 to $39 per listing

4. Cohabs

Source: Cohabs

Cohabs is one of the best apps to find a roommate in NYC as it emphasizes community living with high-quality rooms and apartments. The platform shows listings of sustainable shared houses with an all-included (rent, utilities, wifi, Netflix, and weekly cleaning) price depending on the location and collection.

Features:

  • Can switch rooms or houses at any time.
  • Has a mobile app for easier navigation.
  • Hosts different kinds of events and activities.

Pricing: Cohabs is free to use.

5. RoomZoom

Source: RoomZoom

Another app to find a roommate in NYC, RoomZoom is designed to simplify the roommate finding or moving process of its users. The app sorts and ranks compatible roommates based on lifestyle and personality.

Features:

  • Sorts and ranks best matches, then lets its users message potential roommates freely.
  • No search function.
  • Only works in New York.

Pricing: RoomZoom is free.
