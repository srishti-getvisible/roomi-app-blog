Getting to know Corpus Christi, TX

Corpus Christi is the place to be when you want each day to feel like a vacation. Although it is a small city, it isn’t lacking in any amenities. Its small population of 348,000 usually goes unnoticed – as the city tends to be bursting full of tourists!

Take a scenic drive around the beach loop, and if you’re up for an adventure, try out windsurfing or kayaking. Like to hang out by the beach? You’ll find plenty of options in this city! When it’s time, don’t forget to do your bit and take part in the annual Billy Sandifer Big Shell Beach Clean-Up.

University life in Corpus Christi, TX

With only two colleges within the city, university life in Corpus Christi is not what you might expect. While the rest of the year may seem pretty subdued, you’ll find students flocking to the city when it’s time for spring break. The coastal areas are especially crowded, with students eager to hit the beach and local bars.

Although Spring Break is fun, students have a lot more to look forward during the rest of the school year, too! Students can explore the Art Museum of South Texas, Texas State Aquarium, or explore Padre Island when they get a break. As a student, you can also use your college ID to get free bus rides across the city!

How transit friendly is Corpus Christi, TX?

For most residents, driving is the easiest way to get around. However, you will find plenty of public transit options when you want to explore the city. If you’re in a rush, make use of any of the taxi services or car rentals in the city.

Walkability in Corpus Christi, TX

Corpus Christi has an average walkscore of 41 which indicates low to average walkability. While it isn’t necessary to own a car, it would be useful when running most errands. The most walkable neighborhoods in the city are Central City, Bay Area and South Side.

Roads/traffic in Corpus Christi, TX

Although traffic has gotten worse in the city over the last couple of years, it’s still better compared to some other cities in Texas. For most commuters, the commute time ranges from 15 to 19 minutes, indicating light traffic during the morning.

The existing Harbor Bridge is being redeveloped with the new 181 Harbor Bridge Project. With this construction, residents can enjoy faster-moving traffic and a safer commute. It is set to have 6 traffic lanes and will serve as an efficient transport corridor.

Taxis in Corpus Christi, TX

Apart from Uber and Lyft, residents can also opt for a taxi or rental car service in Corpus Christi. Fares start from $2.50 with an additional $2.75 for every mile and a waiting fee of $12.00 per hour.

Public transportation in Corpus Christi, TX

Apart from some of the more walkable neighborhoods, like Downtown, it’s not easy to explore Corpus Christi on foot. However, you can still opt for City Buses, Taxis, Segway Tours or hop on the Hop Corpus vehicles.

For other parts of the city, you have various public transit options serviced by the Corpus Christi Regional Transit Authority (CCRTA). From Express services which provide direct long-distance transit, to City Buses and Trolley services, that take you to your favorite destinations. It’s easy to track schedules and get passes directly from their official website.

Airports in Corpus Christi, TX

The Corpus City International Airport (CRP) is located just 6 miles away, to the west of the city. Serviced by United Express, Southwest Airlines, and American Eagle, there are non-stop flights to Houston and Dallas throughout the day.

Residents and visitors also have access to San Antonio International Airport or the Austin-Bergstrom International Airport, which are a three-hour drive away from the city. Both airports offer frequent flights to several destinations which makes them a convenient starting point for most trips.

Cycling in Corpus Christi, TX

The CCRTA offers a city-wide bike-sharing program for residents. Buses come equipped with bike racks for hassle-free transit. Keep cycle-safety tips in mind, as most roads are not equipped with safe biking infrastructure. Make yourself as visible as possible and try to cycle in groups, and don’t forget to protect yourself and wear a helmet when you’re out for a ride.

If you’re planning on cycling around the coastal bend of the city, you’ll find several groups that offer a cycle-friendly experience.

Restaurants in Corpus Christi, TX

When talking about Corpus Christi, you’re more likely to hear about the city’s weather, culture or beaches before you hear anyone mention the food. But the city does not fall behind when it comes to delicious meals and Instagram-worthy dishes. If you’re yet to explore the city’s food scene, you’re in for a treat! From unique seafood dishes to authentic cuisines from around the world, there’s a lot to try out!

Explore local favorites or old joints which have won hearts for years. Some places you won’t want to miss are:

  • Thai Spice
  • The Yardarm
  • Kiko’s Mexican Food Restaurant and Cantina
