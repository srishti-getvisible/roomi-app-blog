Co-living In Los Angeles: Where Should You Look?

  • Posted byby Team Roomi
  • January 8, 2022

Thanks to the pandemic, life has become more digital than we’re used to. Meeting new people – especially when you’ve moved to a new city – seems almost impossible, and virtual meet-ups can only help so much. That’s why spaces dedicated to co-living in Los Angeles could just be what you need.

Co-living spaces allow you to share your space with like-minded people from various walks of life. You get to meet and interact with new people and get a glimpse into their lives. Don’t worry, most co-living spaces offer a private bedroom so that you don’t have to interact with everyone at all times – only when you’re in the mood for an exciting evening. Many prefer community living when they’ve just moved to a new city, but managing expenses is another reason people might seek out co-living in Los Angeles.

It’s no secret that LA isn’t exactly cheap, but with co-living spaces, you don’t have to worry about splurging on living expenses. Instead, community living is usually much more affordable, even more so than living with a roommate at times. Sounds brilliant, right? Well, Roomi’s got you covered with a list of co-living companies that make it easier to find co-living spaces in LA.

Venice Beach Glyndon Co-living: The waves are calling for you!

Working and living on the beach? Sounds too good to be true, doesn’t it? Well, if you head to the Venice Beach Glyndon Co-living space, you’ll learn how fun communal living in LA can be. With private bedrooms equipped with desks, you will be living and working from the coziest place you could imagine. Unfortunately, only one of the bedrooms comes with an ensuite bathroom, so you’ll probably have to share one with others living in the space.

Whenever you feel like socializing, you can head to the shared kitchen or go out to the beach to mingle with others. It’s not rare to find fortune tellers or street players here, so you know you’re in for a good time. Of course, there’s weekly housekeeping too, so you will get by doing minimal chores—just the co-living experience in Los Angeles you’re looking for!

MySuite Apartments Co-Living Spaces: Upscale places to splurge on

MySuite’s website offers extensive details on what living in these suites is like. You’ll have onsite management, bike storage, and roommate matching services, among other perks. It’s a pretty sweet deal if you’re looking to try upscale co-living spaces in Los Angeles and don’t mind shelling out a few extra bucks. However, for those looking to manage expenses, this may not be the perfect fit. Since MySuites offers only suites to residents, the prices tend to be higher and don’t include the additional expense of utilities.

However, the website is user-friendly with easy navigation tabs and provides you with all details you need about co-living in Los Angeles.

Ambitious Co-living: Live among creatives like you

Most people opt for co-living as they get to stay with like-minded people who often share the same aspirations or career. It’s easy to meet more people in your field or to get inspired by others. Well, Ambitious Co-living is ideal for creatives who follow their passion. Those in creative fields often look for inspiration all around them; what better way to test new ideas than with other creatives?

Here, you get private bedrooms for more privacy and utilities like laundry services and a parking space. You also get a coworking space and shared kitchens, perfect for getting to know the people you’re living with. The budget is on the higher end, but it may be just what you’re looking for.

Common Co-Living Spaces: Test out Co-living in Los Angeles

Not sure if co-living is the right choice for you? Through community, living is meant to be accessible to people from all walks of life and all ages, but finding senior home shares is a bit more complicated. For those who may be worried that they won’t be cool enough to blend in with the “youngsters” (c’mon, we’re all kids at heart!) Common Co-living Spaces may be just what you need since the lease can be as short as three months – giving you ample time to test out if co-living is the right fit for you. Start slow, finish strong? With a shorter lease, you can ease into co-living and meeting other people, without being tied down to the prospect of living with strangers for a year. You may even be surprised to see that it’s more than just the Millenials or Gen Zs that find co-living exciting.

Common Co-living Spaces is one of the few co-living companies with locations across the country. So, they know what they’re doing. You get furnished apartments, along with shared spaces like community lounges. In addition, there are community events hosted from time to time to allow you to mingle with others along. The best part? There’s a co-living app for residents to stay connected. The downside is that it’s not the easiest coliving app to navigate and there are limited options for co-living in Los Angeles. In addition, though the short lease duration sounds great, it can sometimes be a hindrance when trying to connect with others.
